# Examination of Anterior Segment

The examination of the anterior segment includes the assessment of structures from the [[eyelids]] down to the [[crystalline lens]] and anterior vitreous face. This is fundamental for diagnosing conditions ranging from [[conjunctivitis]] to [[cataract]] and [[glaucoma]].

### I. Methods of Examination

#### 1. Diffuse Illumination
*   **Technique:** Examination under general room light or daylight.
*   **Purpose:** To observe gross lesions of the eyelids, [[conjunctiva]], and [[cornea]].
*   **Method:** The upper and lower lids are retracted digitally to inspect the bulbar conjunctiva. Eversion of the upper lid is required to inspect the upper tarsal conjunctiva (for follicles, papillae, or foreign bodies).

#### 2. Focal (Oblique) Illumination
This method provides better contrast and magnification for detecting subtle lesions.
*   **Principle:** A zone of light is focused on the structure while the surroundings remain dark, making the illuminated object stand out.
*   **Equipment:**
    *   **Light Source:** Focused pen torch.
    *   **Condensing Lens:** +13 D lens.
    *   **Magnification:** **Corneal Loupe** (Uniocular or Binocular). A standard corneal loupe has a power of **+41 D** and provides **10x magnification**.
*   **Technique:** The light is thrown from the temporal side at an angle, while the examiner views through the loupe from the front.

#### 3. Slit-Lamp Biomicroscopy
This is the gold standard for anterior segment examination. It offers stereoscopic vision and high magnification.
*   **Structure:** Consists of an illumination arm (slit beam) and an observation arm (microscope).
*   **Illumination Techniques**,:
    *   **Diffuse Illumination:** General survey.
    *   **Direct Focal Illumination:** Focusing the beam and microscope on the same area (e.g., corneal layers).
    *   **Retroillumination:** Light is reflected off the iris or fundus to view corneal edema or lens vacuoles (they appear dark against a bright background).
    *   **Sclerotic Scatter:** Beam focused on the limbus to check corneal transparency (total internal reflection).
    *   **Specular Reflection:** To view the corneal [[endothelium]] mosaic.

### II. Systematic Examination of Structures

#### 1. Eyelids and Eyelashes
*   **Position:** Check for [[ptosis]] (drooping) or [[lid retraction]] (seen in thyroid eye disease).
*   **Movements:** Check for blinking (normal rate 12–16/min) and lagophthalmos (inability to close eyes).
*   **Lid Margin:**
    *   **Entropion/Ectropion:** Inward or outward turning of margins.
    *   **Tylosis:** Thickening of lid margins.
    *   **Inflammation:** [[Blepharitis]] (scales/crusts), [[Stye]] (hordeolum), [[Chalazion]].
*   **Eyelashes:**
    *   **Trichiasis:** Misdirected lashes rubbing the cornea.
    *   **Distichiasis:** Extra row of lashes.
    *   **Madarosis:** Loss of lashes.
    *   **Poliosis:** Greying of lashes.

#### 2. Conjunctiva
*   **Congestion:** Differentiate between conjunctival and ciliary congestion.

| Feature | Conjunctival Congestion | Ciliary (Circumcorneal) Congestion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Site** | More at fornices | More at limbus |
| **Color** | Bright Red | Purple/Violaceous |
| **Vessels** | Superficial, branching | Deep, radiating |
| **Moveability** | Moves with conjunctiva | Does not move |
| **Blanching** | Blanches with adrenaline | Does not blanch |
| **Significance** | [[Conjunctivitis]] | [[Uveitis]], [[Keratitis]], Acute [[Glaucoma]] |

*   **Discoloration:** Pallor (anemia), yellow (jaundice), congestion (red).
*   **Surface:**
    *   **Follicles:** Lymphoid aggregations (looks like sago grains); seen in viral conjunctivitis, chlamydia.
    *   **Papillae:** Vascular structures (velvety appearance); seen in allergic conjunctivitis, bacterial infection.
    *   **Concretions:** Yellowish hard deposits (inspissated mucus/calcium).
    *   **Growth:** [[Pterygium]] (encroachment onto cornea), Pinguecula.

#### 3. Sclera
*   **Color:** Yellow (jaundice), Blue (Osteogenesis imperfecta, high myopia), Black/Brown (Alkaptonuria).
*   **Inflammation:** Nodule (Episcleritis), diffuse deep redness (Scleritis).
*   **Staphyloma:** Thinning and bulging of sclera lined by uveal tissue.

#### 4. Cornea
*   **Size:**
    *   **Microcornea:** < 10 mm.
    *   **Megalocornea:** > 13 mm (seen in [[Buphthalmos]]).
*   **Curvature:**
    *   Check for [[Keratoconus]] (cone-shaped protrusion).
    *   **Placido’s Disc:** Used to assess surface regularity. Irregular rings indicate astigmatism or keratoconus.
*   **Transparency/Opacities:**
    *   **Nebula:** Faint opacity (iris details visible).
    *   **Macula:** Semi-dense (iris details not visible).
    *   **Leucoma:** Dense white opacity (iris completely obscured).
*   **Vascularization:**
    *   **Superficial:** Continuous with conjunctival vessels, branching pattern (e.g., Pannus).
    *   **Deep:** From anterior ciliary arteries, parallel/radial pattern (e.g., Interstitial keratitis).
*   **Sensations:** Tested with a cotton wisp touching the cornea. Loss of sensation indicates [[Herpes Simplex]] keratitis or neurotrophic keratopathy.
*   **Deposits:** **Keratic Precipitates (KPs)** on the endothelium (sign of uveitis).
    *   **Mutton Fat KPs:** Granulomatous uveitis.
    *   **Fine KPs:** Non-granulomatous uveitis.

#### 5. Anterior Chamber (AC)
*   **Depth Assessment:**
    *   **Oblique Flashlight Test:** Light thrown from temporal side. If nasal iris is shadowed, AC is shallow (Eclipse sign).
    *   **Van Herick’s Method (Slit Lamp):** Compares peripheral AC depth to corneal thickness (CT). Grade 4 (AC > CT) is wide open; Grade 1 (< 1/4 CT) is narrow.
*   **Contents:**
    *   **Aqueous Flare:** Scattering of light due to protein leakage (Tyndall effect).
    *   **Aqueous Cells:** White blood cells (sign of activity).
    *   **Hypopyon:** Pus in AC (sterile in uveitis; infective in ulcer).
    *   **Hyphema:** Blood in AC.

#### 6. Iris
*   **Color:** [[Heterochromia]] (difference in color). Muddy iris seen in acute [[iridocyclitis]].
*   **Pattern:** Loss of crypts and collarette pattern in atrophy or inflammation.
*   **Synechiae:** Adhesions.
    *   **Anterior Synechia:** Iris to cornea (adherent leucoma).
    *   **Posterior Synechia:** Iris to lens (festooned pupil).
*   **Neovascularization:** **Rubeosis Iridis** (seen in Diabetic Retinopathy, CRVO).
*   **Nodules:**
    *   **Koeppe nodules:** At pupillary margin.
    *   **Busacca nodules:** On iris surface (Granulomatous Uveitis).
*   **Tremulousness:** **Iridodonesis** (sign of aphakia or subluxation).

#### 7. Pupil
*   **Size:** Miosis (constriction) vs. Mydriasis (dilation).
*   **Shape:** Normal is circular.
    *   **Festooned:** Due to irregular posterior synechiae.
    *   **Pear-shaped/Key-hole:** Coloboma or surgical iridectomy.
    *   **D-shaped:** Iridodialysis.
    *   **Vertically Oval:** Acute Angle Closure Glaucoma.
*   **Reflexes:**
    *   **Direct Light Reflex:** Constriction of pupil when light is shone.
    *   **Consensual Reflex:** Constriction of contralateral pupil.
    *   **Near Reflex:** Constriction on accommodation.
    *   **RAPD (Marcus Gunn Pupil):** Paradoxical dilation when light is swung from normal to affected eye (Optic nerve pathology).

#### 8. Crystalline Lens
*   **Position:** Check for **Subluxation** (partial displacement) or **Dislocation**.
*   **Transparency:**
    *   **Cataract:** Opacity of the lens.
    *   **Color:** Grey/white (immature/mature), Amber/Black (nuclear sclerosis).
*   **Purkinje Images:**
    *   Image 1 (Cornea anterior): Erect.
    *   Image 2 (Cornea posterior): Erect.
    *   Image 3 (Lens anterior): Erect.
    *   Image 4 (Lens posterior): **Inverted** and moves opposite to light.
    *   *Clinical Note:* In [[aphakia]], images 3 and 4 are absent.

### III. Special Investigations for Anterior Segment

#### 1. Gonioscopy
*   **Purpose:** To visualize the angle of the anterior chamber (not visible directly due to total internal reflection).
*   **Structures Viewed (Posterior to Anterior):**
    1.  Root of Iris.
    2.  Ciliary Body Band.
    3.  [[Scleral Spur]].
    4.  [[Trabecular Meshwork]].
    5.  Schwalbe’s Line.
*   **Mnemonic:** **"I Can See The Schwalbe's"** (Iris, Ciliary body, Scleral spur, Trabecular meshwork, Schwalbe's line).

#### 2. Transillumination
*   Used to differentiate solid intraocular tumors (opaque to light) from serous fluid/cysts (translucent).

#### 3. Vital Staining
*   **Fluorescein:** Stains corneal epithelial defects (ulcers/abrasions) bright green.
*   **Rose Bengal:** Stains devitalized cells and mucus (Dry eye, Herpes).
