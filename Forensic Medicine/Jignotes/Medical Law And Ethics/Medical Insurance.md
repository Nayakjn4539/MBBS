# [[Medical Law And Ethics]] > Medical Insurance

### Medical Indemnity Insurance (Professional Indemnity Insurance)

**Medical Indemnity Insurance** is a contract under which an insurance company agrees, in exchange for the payment of premiums, to **indemnify** (reimburse or compensate) the insured registered medical practitioner against any loss or expense directly arising from actions, claims, and demands against them on the grounds of [[Professional Negligence]].

This is essentially a form of protection for doctors against the financial risks of **Civil Negligence** suits (Malpractice litigation).

#### Objectives
The primary purposes of taking a Professional Indemnity Policy are:
1.  **Protection:** To look after and protect the professional interests of the insured doctor.
2.  **Defense:** To arrange, conduct, and pay for the legal defense of the doctor in court (fees for lawyers/advocates).
3.  **Advice:** To arrange professional assistance, including **pre-litigation advice**, when a claim is threatened.
4.  **Compensation:** To indemnify the insured doctor regarding any financial loss (damages/compensation awarded by the court) arising from claims of negligence or misconduct.

#### Mechanism and Procedure
*   **Premium:** Doctors can insure their professional practice by paying a premium. The amount depends on the clinical setup, the risk associated with the specialty (e.g., surgeons pay higher premiums than general practitioners), and the coverage limit desired.
*   **Providers:** Services are provided by general insurance companies or by professional bodies like the [[Indian Medical Association]] (IMA) at state levels (Professional Protection Schemes).
*   **Dispute Protocol:** When a dispute or allegation of negligence arises:
    *   The doctor **must** contact the insurance society/company immediately.
    *   The doctor **should not** make any admissions of guilt or enter into correspondence with the complaining party without advice from the insurer/legal advisor.
    *   Admitting fault prematurely can void the insurance coverage.

#### Mnemonic for Objectives
**PAID**
*   **P** - **P**rotect professional interests.
*   **A** - **A**rrange legal defense.
*   **I** - **I**ndemnify financial loss (pay compensation).
*   **D** - **D**ispense pre-litigation advice.

#### Medicolegal Aspects
*   **Civil Liability:** This insurance covers **Civil Liability** (monetary compensation). It does *not* protect against criminal prosecution (imprisonment) or ethical disciplinary action (erasure of name) by the [[State Medical Council]].
*   **Consumer Protection Act (CPA):** With the inclusion of medical services under the [[Consumer Protection Act]], the number of litigations and the amount of compensation awarded have increased significantly. Indemnity insurance is practically mandatory for modern medical practice to prevent financial ruin.
*   **Vicarious Liability:** Hospitals usually take large indemnity policies to cover the acts of their employees under the doctrine of **Respondeat Superior** (Vicarious Liability). However, individual consultants are advised to have personal cover as well.
