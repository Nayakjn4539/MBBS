### Employees' State Insurance (ESI) Scheme

The [[Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948]] is a pioneering measure of social security and health insurance in India. It provides protection to employees against loss of wages due to sickness, maternity, disability, and death due to employment injury.

---

### 1. Eligibility Criteria for Enrolment

The scheme applies to non-seasonal factories using power and employing 10 or more persons, and non-power using factories employing 20 or more persons.

| **Criteria** | **Details** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Wage Ceiling** | Employees drawing wages up to **Rs. 21,000/- per month** (excluding overtime remuneration). |
| **Establishments Covered** | Factories, shops, hotels, restaurants, cinemas, road-motor transport, and newspaper establishments. |
| **Exclusions** | The act generally excludes [[Mines]], [[Railways]], and [[Defence]] establishments. |
| **Exemption form Contribution** | Employees whose average daily wage is **below Rs. 176/-** are exempted from contributing their share, though the employer must still contribute. |

**Finance and Contribution Rates (Revised w.e.f. 01.07.2019):**
*   **Employee's Share:** 0.75% of wages.
*   **Employer's Share:** 3.25% of wages.
*   **State Govt. Share:** Bears 1/8th of the total cost of medical care.

---

### 2. Benefits Under ESI Scheme

The benefits provided to insured persons (IPs) and their dependents are categorized into **Medical Benefits** (in kind) and **Cash Benefits**.

**Mnemonic for Benefits:** **"M**edical **S**tudents **M**ust **D**o **D**aily **F**uneral **R**ites"
*   **M**edical Benefit
*   **S**ickness Benefit
*   **M**aternity Benefit
*   **D**isablement Benefit
*   **D**ependant's Benefit
*   **F**uneral Expenses
*   **R**ehabilitation Allowance

#### A. Medical Benefit
*   **Scope:** Full medical care is provided to the Insured Person (IP) and their family members from day one of entering insurable employment.
*   **Nature:** Comprehensive care including outpatient, inpatient, specialist consultation, drugs, dressings, and diagnostic investigations.
*   **Cost:** No ceiling on expenditure for the treatment of the beneficiary.
*   **Delivery:** Provided through "Service System" (ESI Hospitals/Dispensaries) or "Panel System" (Private Medical Practitioners).

#### B. Sickness Benefit
Periodic cash payments are made to the IP during periods of certified sickness.
1.  **Standard Sickness Benefit:**
    *   **Rate:** Approx. **70%** of average daily wages.
    *   **Duration:** Maximum **91 days** in any continuous 365-day period.
    *   **Eligibility:** The worker must have contributed for at least 78 days in a contribution period of 6 months.
2.  **Extended Sickness Benefit:**
    *   **Condition:** For 34 specified long-term diseases (e.g., Tuberculosis, Leprosy, Cancer, AIDS).
    *   **Rate:** **80%** of wages.
    *   **Duration:** Payable up to **2 years**.
3.  **Enhanced Sickness Benefit:**
    *   **Condition:** For undergoing sterilization operations (Family Planning).
    *   **Rate:** **100%** of wages (Full wages).
    *   **Duration:** **7 days** for Vasectomy and **14 days** for Tubectomy.

#### C. Maternity Benefit
*   **Scope:** Payable to an insured woman for confinement (delivery), miscarriage, or sickness arising out of pregnancy.
*   **Rate:** **100%** of average daily wages (Full wages).
*   **Duration:**
    *   **Confinement:** **26 weeks** (increased from 12 weeks).
    *   **Miscarriage:** 6 weeks.
    *   **Sickness due to pregnancy:** up to 1 month.

#### D. Disablement Benefit
Provided for injuries arising out of employment (including occupational diseases).
1.  **Temporary Disablement Benefit:**
    *   **Rate:** **90%** of wages.
    *   **Duration:** Payable as long as the temporary disability lasts.
2.  **Permanent Disablement Benefit:**
    *   **Rate:** Life pension at **90%** of wages (for total disability) or a proportionate rate (for partial disability) determined by a Medical Board.

#### E. Dependant's Benefit
*   **Condition:** Paid to dependants (widow, children, aged parents) if the IP dies due to an employment injury or occupational disease.
*   **Rate:** Pension at the rate of **90%** of the wages shared among dependants.

#### F. Funeral Expenses
*   **Scope:** Cash payment to the eldest surviving member of the family or the person who performs the funeral rites.
*   **Amount:** Maximum of **Rs. 15,000/-**.

#### G. Rehabilitation Allowance
*   **Scope:** Granted to IPs for each day they are admitted to an Artificial Limb Centre for the fixation/repair of artificial limbs.
*   **Rate:** 100% of standard sickness benefit rate.

#### H. Rajiv Gandhi Shramik Kalyan Yojna
*   **Purpose:** Unemployment allowance for employees rendered unemployed involuntarily due to retrenchment or closure of the factory.
*   **Benefit:** **50% of wages** for a maximum period of **2 years**.
*   **Additional:** Medical care is also provided to the family during this period.

### Clinical Relevance
Doctors working in industrial areas must be aware of [[Occupational Diseases]] covered under the ESI Act to issue proper certification, ensuring patients receive the **Extended Sickness Benefit** or **Disablement Benefit** they are entitled to.
