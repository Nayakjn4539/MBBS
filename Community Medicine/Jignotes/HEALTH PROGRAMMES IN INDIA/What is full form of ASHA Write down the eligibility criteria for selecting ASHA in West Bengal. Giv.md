# [[HEALTH PROGRAMMES IN INDIA]] > What is full form of ASHA? Write down the eligibility criteria for selecting ASHA in West Bengal. Give a brief outline of role of ASHA in maternal and new-born care.

### Full Form
**ASHA**: [[Accredited Social Health Activist]]

### Eligibility Criteria for Selection (West Bengal/National Norms)
The ASHA must be a resident of the village she serves. The general selection norms (applicable to West Bengal under the [[National Health Mission]]) are:

1.  **Residency:** She must be a resident of the village.
2.  **Gender & Marital Status:** She must be a **woman** who is either **married, widowed, or divorced**.
3.  **Age:** She should preferably be in the age group of **25 to 45 years**.
4.  **Education:** She should be a literate woman with formal education up to **Class VIII** (Eighth Standard).
    *   *Note: In some contexts/recent updates, Class X is preferred, but standard text guidelines cite Class VIII.*
5.  **Qualities:** She should have effective communication skills and leadership qualities.
6.  **Representation:** Adequate representation from disadvantaged population groups (SC/ST) should be ensured.

> **Mnemonic for Eligibility: MARS**
> *   **M** - Married/Widowed/Divorced
> *   **A** - Age (25-45)
> *   **R** - Resident of Village
> *   **S** - Schooling (Class 8 pass)

### Role of ASHA in Maternal and Newborn Care

The ASHA acts as a bridge between the community and the health system. Her roles can be categorized as follows:

#### 1. Maternal Care (Antenatal & Intranatal)
*   **Registration:** Early registration of pregnancies within the first trimester.
*   **Counselling & Awareness:**
    *   Educate women on **birth preparedness**, safe delivery, and breastfeeding.
    *   Counsel on nutrition, hygiene, and contraception.
    *   Identify danger signs during pregnancy and labor.
*   **Facilitation of Services:**
    *   Mobilize pregnant women for [[Antenatal Care (ANC)]] check-ups (minimum 4 visits).
    *   Ensure Tetanus Toxoid (TT) immunization and Iron Folic Acid ([[IFA]]) supplementation.
*   **Institutional Delivery:**
    *   Motivate and escort pregnant women to the health facility for safe delivery under [[Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)]].
    *   Arrange transport for the mother to the [[First Referral Unit (FRU)]] in case of complications.

#### 2. Newborn Care (Home-Based Newborn Care - HBNC)
ASHA provides essential newborn care through a series of home visits.

*   **Home Visit Schedule:**
    *   **Institutional Delivery:** 6 visits (Days 3, 7, 14, 21, 28, 42).
    *   **Home Delivery:** 7 visits (Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, 28, 42).
*   **Key Responsibilities during Visits:**
    *   **Weighing:** Recording the birth weight of the newborn.
    *   **Thermal Care:** Preventing [[hypothermia]] by ensuring warmth (Kangaroo Mother Care).
    *   **Breastfeeding:** Supporting early initiation (within 1 hour) and **exclusive breastfeeding** for 6 months.
    *   **Infection Control:** Care of the cord (keeping it dry/clean), eyes, and skin.
    *   **Detection & Referral:** Identifying "at-risk" infants (Preterm/[[Low Birth Weight]]) and detecting signs of sepsis/pneumonia for prompt referral to the ANM or PHC.

> **Mnemonic for HBNC Visits: 3-7-14-21-28-42**
> *(Add Day 1 for Home Deliveries)*
