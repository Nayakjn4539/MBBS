# Paraphilias (Sexual Deviations/Perversions)

**Definition:**
Paraphilias are recurrent, intense, sexually arousing fantasies, sexual urges, or behaviors generally involving:
1.  Non-human objects.
2.  The suffering or humiliation of oneself or one's partner.
3.  Children or other non-consenting persons.

From a medicolegal perspective, these are often termed [[Sexual Perversions]] or [[Unnatural Sexual Offences]].

### Classification of Paraphilias

#### 1. Sadism (Active Algolagnia)
*   **Definition:** Sexual gratification is obtained by inflicting physical or mental pain, suffering, or humiliation on the partner.
*   Derived from *Marquis de Sade*.
*   **Behavior:** Ranges from biting ("love bites"), pinching, and whipping to severe mutilation and even murder ([[Lust Murder]]).
*   **Medicolegal Aspect:**
    *   If injuries are severe, it constitutes [[Homicide]] or Grievous Hurt.
    *   The defense of "consent" by the partner is generally not valid for severe injuries or death.

#### 2. Masochism (Passive Algolagnia)
*   **Definition:** Sexual gratification is obtained by suffering pain, humiliation, or being dominated/bound.
*   Derived from *Leopold von Sacher-Masoch*.
*   **Behavior:** Being beaten, tied up (bondage), or partially asphyxiated.
*   **Bondage:** The practice of tying up arms, legs, or genitals for sexual pleasure.
*   **Medicolegal Aspect:** High risk of accidental death, particularly due to [[Sexual Asphyxia]].

**Comparison: Sadism vs. Masochism**

| Feature | Sadism (Active Algolagnia) | Masochism (Passive Algolagnia) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Role** | Active agent (Inflictor) | Passive agent (Receiver) |
| **Action** | Inflicts pain/cruelty | Suffers pain/humiliation |
| **Outcome** | May commit [[Lust Murder]] | May die of accidental injury/asphyxia |
| **Psyche** | Need for dominance | Need for submission |

#### 3. Exhibitionism
*   **Definition:** Recurrent urge to expose one's genitals to an unsuspecting stranger to achieve sexual arousal.
*   Also known as **Indecent Exposure**.
*   The shock or fear reaction of the victim reinforces the perpetrator's arousal. There is usually no attempt at further sexual contact.
*   **Subtypes:**
    *   **Flashing:** Sudden undressing and exposing private parts.
    *   **Streaking:** Running naked in public places.
    *   **Mooning:** Exposing the gluteal region (buttocks).
*   **Legal:** Punishable under Section 294 IPC (Obscene acts) or **Section 296 BNS** (Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita).

#### 4. Voyeurism (Scopophilia/Scoptophilia)
*   **Definition:** Obtaining sexual pleasure by secretly watching others when they are naked, disrobing, or engaging in sexual activity.
*   Commonly referred to as a **"Peeping Tom"**.
*   **Legal:** Punishable under Section 354C IPC (specific to watching women).

#### 5. Fetishism
*   **Definition:** Sexual arousal derived from inanimate objects (e.g., shoes, undergarments, hair, rubber, leather).
*   The object is called a **Fetish**.
*   The fetish is usually required for sexual gratification.
*   **Medicolegal:** May lead to theft (Kleptomania) or burglary to obtain the objects.

#### 6. Transvestism (Eonism)
*   **Definition:** Achieving sexual excitement by dressing in the clothes of the opposite sex (Cross-dressing).
*   Named after *Chevalier d'Eon*.
*   Typically heterosexual males who cross-dress for arousal.
*   Distinct from *Transsexualism* (Gender Dysphoria), where the person feels they belong to the opposite sex.

#### 7. Frotteurism (Thigmaphilia)
*   **Definition:** Sexual arousal achieved by touching or rubbing one's genitals against a non-consenting person, usually in crowded places (buses, trains, elevators).
*   The perpetrator often fantasizes a relationship with the victim.
*   **Legal:** Punishable under **Section 75 BNS** (Sexual harassment).

#### 8. Necrophilia
*   **Definition:** Sexual attraction to or sexual intercourse with a dead body.
*   **Necrophagia:** A rare extreme form involving eating the flesh of the corpse.
*   Often associated with individuals working in mortuaries or graveyards.
*   **Medicolegal Aspect:**
    *   Punishable under Section 297 IPC (Trespassing on burial grounds/indignity to a human corpse) and Section 377 IPC ([[Unnatural Offences]]).
    *   Under **Section 301 BNS**, specific provision for sexual intercourse with a dead body is punishable.

#### 9. Bestiality (Zooerasty/Zoophilia)
*   **Definition:** Sexual intercourse with animals.
*   Usually involves lower animals (dogs, goats, cattle, etc.).
*   **Signs:**
    *   Animal hair on the accused's clothes/body.
    *   Injuries (kicks, bites) on the accused.
    *   Vaginal/Anal swabs from the animal showing human semen.
*   **Legal:** Punishable under Section 377 IPC (Carnal intercourse against the order of nature).

#### 10. Pedophilia
*   **Definition:** Sexual preference for prepubescent children.
*   Offender is usually known to the child.
*   **Legal:** Covered under the **POCSO Act** (Protection of Children from Sexual Offences).

### Other Specific Paraphilias

*   **Pyromania:** Sexual arousal from setting or watching fires.
*   **Kleptomania:** Sexual gratification from stealing articles (often of little value).
*   **Coprophilia:** Sexual pleasure from feces.
*   **Urophilia (Undinism):** Sexual pleasure from urine (sight/smell).
*   **Klismaphilia:** Sexual pleasure from the use of enemas.
*   **Scatologia:** Making obscene telephone calls (Telephone Scatologia).
*   **Gerontophilia:** Sexual attraction towards elderly persons.
*   **Troilism (Pluralism):** Performing sexual acts in a group (Group Sex) or watching one's partner have sex with another.

### Medicolegal Importance: Sexual Asphyxia

Also known as **Autoerotic Asphyxia** or **Hypoxyphilia**. This is a form of masochism where hypoxia (oxygen deprivation) is induced to enhance sexual arousal and orgasm.

**Mechanism:**
The individual induces cerebral hypoxia (via hanging, plastic bags, or chemicals) to heighten the sensation of orgasm.

**Autopsy Findings & Scene Investigation:**
1.  **Scene:** Usually secluded (bedroom, bathroom). Door locked from inside.
2.  **Ligature:** Often padded (to prevent pain/marks) with a towel or handkerchief.
3.  **Rescue Mechanism:** Evidence of a fail-safe mechanism (knife nearby, feet touching ground) that failed.
4.  **Nudity:** Body often naked or dressed in female clothing ([[Transvestism]]).
5.  **Pornography:** Presence of erotic literature or mirrors.
6.  **Bondage:** Hands or feet may be tied, but in a way that allows self-release.
7.  **No Suicide Note:** Death is accidental, not suicidal.
