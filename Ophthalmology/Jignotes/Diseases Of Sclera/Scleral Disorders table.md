# Scleral Disorders: Comprehensive Overview

The diseases of the [[sclera]] range from benign inflammations to sight-threatening destructive conditions. Due to the avascular nature of the sclera and its dense fibrous structure, inflammations here tend to be chronic and often associated with systemic collagen vascular disorders.

### Clinical Implications & Pearls

*   **Pain Character:** The hallmark of **[[Scleritis]]** is severe, boring pain that often wakes the patient at night and radiates to the jaw or temple. **[[Episcleritis]]** usually causes only mild discomfort or pricking sensation.
*   **Phenylephrine Test:** To differentiate Episcleritis from Scleritis, instill 10% or 2.5% [[Phenylephrine]]. If the redness blanches (whitens), it is **Episcleritis** (superficial vessels). If redness persists, it is **Scleritis** (deep vascular plexus).
*   **The "T-Sign":** In **[[Posterior Scleritis]]**, B-scan ultrasonography shows fluid in the sub-Tenon's space causing a "T" shape shadow. This is a crucial diagnostic sign.,
*   **Scleromalacia Perforans:** Paradoxically, this is the most severe form of scleritis (necrotizing) but presents as **painless**. It is strongly associated with long-standing Rheumatoid Arthritis.,
*   **Contraindication:** Do **NOT** give subconjunctival steroid injections in necrotizing scleritis as it may lead to scleral melting and perforation.
