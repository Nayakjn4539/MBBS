# Anatomy and Physiology of the [[Pharynx]]

The [[pharynx]] is a conical fibromuscular tube forming the upper part of the air and food passages. It acts as a common aerodigestive pathway.

**Extent:**
*   **Superiorly:** Base of the skull (basiocciput and basisphenoid).
*   **Inferiorly:** Lower border of [[cricoid cartilage]] (level of C6 vertebra), where it becomes continuous with the [[esophagus]].
*   **Length:** Approximately 12–14 cm.
*   **Width:** Widest at the base (3.5 cm) and narrowest at the pharyngo-esophageal junction (1.5 cm).

**Structure of Pharyngeal Wall (From within outwards):**
1.  **Mucous Membrane:**
    *   **Nasopharynx:** Pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium (Respiratory epithelium).
    *   **Oropharynx & Hypopharynx:** Non-keratinizing stratified squamous epithelium.
2.  **[[Pharyngobasilar Fascia]]:** A fibrous layer thickest near the base of the skull (Sinus of Morgagni) and thins out inferiorly. It fills the gap between the skull base and the superior constrictor.
3.  **Muscular Coat:** Consists of outer circular and inner longitudinal layers.
4.  **[[Buccopharyngeal Fascia]]:** Covers the outer surface of the constrictor muscles and extends forward to cover the buccinator.

## Subdivisions of the Pharynx

The pharynx is anatomically divided into three parts: [[Nasopharynx]], [[Oropharynx]], and [[Hypopharynx]] (Laryngopharynx).

### 1. [[Nasopharynx]] (Epipharynx)
It lies behind the nasal cavities and above the soft palate.

| Boundary | Structures |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anterior** | Choanae (Posterior nasal apertures). |
| **Posterior/Roof** | Basisphenoid, Basiocciput, Anterior arch of Atlas (C1). Contains [[Adenoids]] (Nasopharyngeal tonsil) and [[Pharyngeal bursa]] (Thornwaldt's bursa). |
| **Inferior** | Soft palate and [[Nasopharyngeal isthmus]]. |
| **Lateral Wall** | 1. **[[Eustachian Tube]] Orifice:** 1.25 cm behind the posterior end of the inferior turbinate.<br>2. **[[Torus Tubarius]]:** Cartilaginous elevation guarding the opening.<br>3. **[[Fossa of Rosenmuller]] (Pharyngeal Recess):** A slit-like depression behind the torus tubarius. **Clinical Importance:** Most common site for [[Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma]] (NPC). |

**Clinical Implications:**
*   **Passavant's Ridge:** A mucosal ridge on the posterior wall formed by [[Palatopharyngeus]] fibers. It helps close the nasopharyngeal isthmus during swallowing/speech.
*   **Adenoid Hypertrophy:** Enlargement can obstruct the Eustachian tube causing [[Serous Otitis Media]] or obstruct the choanae causing mouth breathing and "Adenoid Facies".

### 2. [[Oropharynx]]
It lies behind the oral cavity, extending from the soft palate superiorly to the upper border of the epiglottis/hyoid bone inferiorly.

**Boundaries:**
*   **Anterior:** Oropharyngeal isthmus, base of tongue (posterior 1/3), [[Vallecula]].
*   **Posterior:** C2 and upper C3 vertebrae.
*   **Superior:** Soft palate.
*   **Lateral:** [[Palatine Tonsils]] located in the tonsillar fossa between the anterior pillar ([[Palatoglossus]]) and posterior pillar ([[Palatopharyngeus]]).

**Key Structures:**
*   **[[Palatine Tonsils]]:** Lymphoid tissue in the lateral wall.
*   **[[Vallecula]]:** Space between the base of tongue and epiglottis.
*   **Lingual Tonsils:** Lymphoid tissue on the base of the tongue.

### 3. [[Hypopharynx]] (Laryngopharynx)
It lies behind the larynx, extending from the hyoid bone (upper border of epiglottis) to the lower border of the cricoid cartilage (C6).

**Subsites of Hypopharynx:**
1.  **[[Pyriform Fossa]] (Sinus):** Lateral channels for food passage. Bounded medially by the aryepiglottic fold and laterally by the thyroid cartilage.
    *   *Clinical:* Common site for hidden malignancies; rich lymphatic drainage leads to early nodal metastasis.
2.  **[[Postcricoid Region]]:** Anterior wall of the hypopharynx, behind the cricoid cartilage. Extends from the arytenoids to the inferior border of the cricoid.
    *   *Clinical:* Common site for carcinoma in females with [[Plummer Vinson Syndrome]].
3.  **[[Posterior Pharyngeal Wall]]:** Extends from the level of the hyoid to the cricoid.

## [[Waldeyer's Ring]]
A collection of subepithelial lymphoid tissue guarding the entrance of the aerodigestive tract.

**Components:**
1.  **[[Adenoids]]** (Nasopharyngeal tonsil) - Roof of nasopharynx.
2.  **[[Palatine Tonsils]]** (Faucial tonsils) - Oropharynx.
3.  **[[Lingual Tonsils]]** - Base of tongue.
4.  **[[Tubal Tonsils]]** (Gerlach's tonsil) - Around Eustachian tube opening.
5.  **Lateral Pharyngeal Bands.**
6.  **Nodules** on the posterior pharyngeal wall.

## Muscles of the Pharynx

### 1. External Circular Layer (Constrictors)
These muscles overlap each other from below upwards (like stacked cups). They insert into the median pharyngeal raphe.

*   **[[Superior Constrictor]]:** Quadrangular muscle.
*   **[[Middle Constrictor]]:** Fan-shaped.
*   **[[Inferior Constrictor]]:** Composed of two parts:
    *   **[[Thyropharyngeus]]:** Oblique fibers (propulsive).
    *   **[[Cricopharyngeus]]:** Transverse fibers (sphincteric). Acts as the upper esophageal sphincter.

**Clinical Implication: [[Killian's Dehiscence]]**
*   A potential weak area between the Thyropharyngeus (oblique) and Cricopharyngeus (transverse) fibers of the inferior constrictor.
*   **Pathology:** Site of herniation for **[[Zenker's Diverticulum]]** (Pharyngeal Pouch).

### 2. Internal Longitudinal Layer (Elevators)
These muscles shorten and elevate the pharynx during swallowing.
1.  **[[Stylopharyngeus]]**
2.  **[[Salpingopharyngeus]]**
3.  **[[Palatopharyngeus]]**

### Structures Passing Between Constrictors (Applied Anatomy)
The gaps between the constrictor muscles allow neurovascular structures to pass.

| Gap Location | Structures Passing Through |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Sinus of Morgagni** (Above Superior Constrictor) | 1. [[Eustachian Tube]]<br>2. Levator veli palatini<br>3. Tensor veli palatini<br>4. Ascending palatine artery |
| **Between Superior & Middle Constrictor** | 1. [[Stylopharyngeus]] muscle<br>2. [[Glossopharyngeal nerve]] (CN IX) |
| **Between Middle & Inferior Constrictor** | 1. Internal laryngeal nerve (Branch of SLN)<br>2. Superior laryngeal vessels |
| **Below Inferior Constrictor** | 1. [[Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve]] (RLN)<br>2. Inferior laryngeal vessels |

**Mnemonic for Sinus of Morgagni Contents:** **TAALA** (Tensor, Ascending palatine, Ascending pharyngeal branch, Levator, Auditory tube).

## Nerve Supply

**Nerve Supply of Pharynx:** Derived from the **[[Pharyngeal Plexus]]** (formed by CN IX, X, and Sympathetic fibers).

1.  **Motor Supply:**
    *   All pharyngeal muscles are supplied by the **Pharyngeal Plexus (Cranial Accessory nerve via Vagus)**.
    *   **EXCEPTION:** **[[Stylopharyngeus]]** is supplied by the **[[Glossopharyngeal Nerve]] (CN IX)**.
    *   [[Inferior Constrictor]] also receives supply from the External and Recurrent Laryngeal Nerves.

2.  **Sensory Supply:**
    *   **Nasopharynx:** [[Maxillary Nerve]] (V2).
    *   **Oropharynx:** [[Glossopharyngeal Nerve]] (CN IX).
    *   **Hypopharynx:** [[Vagus Nerve]] (CN X) — Internal laryngeal nerve (upper part) and Recurrent laryngeal nerve (lower part).

## Pharyngeal Spaces

Deep neck spaces prone to abscess formation.

**1. [[Retropharyngeal Space]] (Space of Gillette)**
*   **Location:** Between Buccopharyngeal fascia (anteriorly) and Alar/Prevertebral fascia (posteriorly).
*   **Extent:** Base of skull to bifurcation of trachea (T4/mediastinum).
*   **Contents:** **[[Nodes of Rouviere]]** (Retropharyngeal lymph nodes). Important in children; they atrophy by age 3-4.
*   **Clinical:** Abscess here causes difficulty in breathing, stridor, and aparamedian bulge in the posterior pharyngeal wall.

**2. [[Parapharyngeal Space]]**
*   **Location:** Lateral to the pharynx. Shaped like an inverted pyramid.
*   **Compartments:** Divided by the Styloid process.
    *   **Pre-styloid:** Contains fat, lymph nodes, internal maxillary artery. Abscess here causes **Trismus** and pushes tonsil medially.
    *   **Post-styloid:** Contains the **Carotid Sheath** (ICA, IJV, CN IX, X, XI, XII, Sympathetic chain). Abscess here causes cranial nerve palsies and Horner's syndrome.

**3. [[Peritonsillar Space]]**
*   **Location:** Between the tonsillar capsule and the superior constrictor muscle.
*   **Clinical:** Site of **[[Quinsy]]** (Peritonsillar abscess). Features include "Hot potato voice", trismus, and uvular deviation.

## Deglutition (Swallowing)
The pharynx plays a crucial role in the pharyngeal phase of swallowing (involuntary).
1.  Soft palate rises to close the nasopharynx (prevents nasal regurgitation).
2.  Larynx elevates; vocal cords adduct; epiglottis deflects to protect the airway.
3.  Pharyngeal constrictors contract (peristalsis) to propel bolus.
4.  Cricopharyngeus (UES) relaxes to allow food into the esophagus.
