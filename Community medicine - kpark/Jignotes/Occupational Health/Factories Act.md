# [[Occupational Health]] > Factories Act

### The Factories Act, 1948

The [[Factories Act, 1948]] is a comprehensive piece of social legislation in India that aims to ensure the health, safety, and welfare of workers in factories. It came into force on **April 1, 1949**.

### **Scope and Definition**
*   **Factory Definition:** Any premises where a manufacturing process is carried on involving:
    *   **10 or more workers** (if working with the aid of **power**),.
    *   **20 or more workers** (if working **without** the aid of power),.
*   **Exclusions:** Mines, Armed Forces of the Union, and Railway running sheds,.

---

### **Key Provisions**

#### **1. Health Measures (Chapter III)**
To prevent industrial health hazards, the act mandates:
*   **Cleanliness:** Daily removal of dirt; floors washed weekly; painting/varnishing every 5 years.
*   **Disposal of Wastes:** Effective arrangements for waste and effluent disposal.
*   **Ventilation & Temperature:** Adequate circulation of fresh air and comfortable temperature.
*   **Dust & Fumes:** Effective exhaust measures to prevent inhalation.
*   **Overcrowding:** Minimum space of **500 cubic feet (14.2 cubic meters)** per worker is mandatory to prevent overcrowding,.
*   **Lighting:** Sufficient natural or artificial lighting; prevention of glare.
*   **Drinking Water:** Wholesome water at convenient points. If **>250 workers**, cool water must be provided in hot weather.
*   **Latrines & Urinals:** Separate for males and females. Sanitary type with glazed tiles.

#### **2. Safety Measures (Chapter IV)**
*   **Fencing:** Machinery must be properly fenced/encased.
*   **Prohibition:** Young persons cannot work on dangerous machines.
*   **Protective Gear:** Provision of goggles for eye protection, precautions against dangerous fumes/explosives.
*   **Safety Officer:** Appointment is mandatory in factories employing **1000 or more workers**,.

#### **3. Welfare Measures (Chapter V)**
*   **Washing Facilities:** Adequate and separate for men/women.
*   **Sitting Facilities:** For workers obliged to work in a standing position.
*   **First Aid:** At least one first-aid box for every **150 workers**. An ambulance room (with medical staff) is required if **>500 workers**.
*   **Canteens:** Mandatory if employing **>250 workers**,.
*   **Shelters/Rest Rooms:** Required if >150 workers.
*   **Crèches:** Suitable room for children <6 years required if employing **>30 women workers**,.
*   **Welfare Officer:** Appointment mandatory if employing **500 or more workers**.

#### **4. Employment of Young Persons & Women**
*   **Prohibition:** Employment of children **<14 years** is strictly prohibited,.
*   **Adolescents (15–18 years):**
    *   Must be certified fit by a **Certifying Surgeon**,.
    *   Can work only between **6 AM and 7 PM**.
    *   Maximum working hours: **4.5 hours/day**,.
*   **Women:** No woman is allowed to work between **7 PM and 6 AM**.

#### **5. Working Hours for Adults**
*   **Weekly Hours:** Maximum **48 hours/week**,.
*   **Daily Hours:** Maximum **9 hours/day**.
*   **Rest Interval:** At least 30 minutes rest after 5 hours of continuous work.
*   **Weekly Holiday:** One full day off per week (usually Sunday).
*   **Leave with Wages:** 1 day for every 20 days worked (Adults); 1 day for every 15 days (Children).

### **Notifiable Diseases (Schedule III)**
Under the Act, specific occupational diseases must be notified by the factory manager to the authorities. There are **29 notifiable diseases** listed in the schedule,.
*   **Examples:** [[Lead poisoning]], Silicosis, [[Anthracosis]], [[Byssinosis]], Asbestosis, Noise-induced hearing loss, Toxic anemia,.

---

### **Mnemonic for Worker Number Thresholds**

**"30 Moms Cry, 150 for Aid, 250 Eat, 500 Fare, 1000 Stay Safe"**

*   **30** Moms = **Crèche** (>30 women workers)
*   **150** for Aid = **First Aid Box** (1 per 150 workers)
*   **250** Eat = **Canteen** (>250 workers)
*   **500** Fare = **Welfare Officer** (>500 workers) / Ambulance Room
*   **1000** Stay Safe = **Safety Officer** (>1000 workers)

---

### **Clinical Relevance**
*   **Certifying Surgeon:** Appointed under the Act to examine young persons, workers in hazardous processes, and supervise cases of illness due to the nature of work.
*   **Occupational History:** Doctors must take a detailed occupational history for patients presenting with respiratory or dermatological issues to rule out notifiable diseases like [[Pneumoconiosis]].
*   **Pre-placement Examination:** The Act emphasizes medical checks (Ergonomics) to fit the worker to the job.
