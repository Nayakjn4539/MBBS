# [[OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH]] > Define "Factory" under Indian Factories Act 1948. Write in brief the provisions recommended in Indian Factories Act 1948 to protect health of the workers.

### [[The Factories Act, 1948]]

### 1. Definition of "Factory"
According to the [[Factories Act, 1948]], a **Factory** is defined as any premises including the precincts thereof:

*   Wherein **10 or more workers** are working, or were working on any day of the preceding twelve months, and in any part of which a manufacturing process is being carried out **with the aid of power**.
*   Wherein **20 or more workers** are working, or were working on any day of the preceding twelve months, and in any part of which a manufacturing process is being carried out **without the aid of power**.

**Note:** It does *not* include a mine subject to the operation of the Mines Act, 1952, or a mobile unit belonging to the armed forces of the Union, a railway running shed or a hotel, restaurant or eating place.

---

### 2. Provisions for Health (Chapter III)
The Act prescribes detailed instructions in **Sections 11 to 20** to ensure the environmental sanitation and health of workers.

**1. Cleanliness (Section 11):**
*   The factory must be kept clean and free from effluvia arising from any drain or privy.
*   Accumulated dirt must be removed daily.
*   Floors should be washed at least **once a week** with disinfectant.
*   Inside walls/partitions/ceilings must be repainted/varnished once in **5 years** (washable water paint once in **3 years**) and white-washed once in **14 months**.

**2. Disposal of Wastes and Effluents (Section 12):**
*   Effective arrangements must be made for the treatment and disposal of wastes and [[industrial effluents]] generated during the manufacturing process.

**3. Ventilation and Temperature (Section 13):**
*   Provision for adequate circulation of fresh air.
*   Temperature must be maintained at a level comfortable for workers to prevent injury to health. Hot parts of machinery should be insulated.

**4. Dust and Fumes (Section 14):**
*   Effective measures to prevent inhalation and accumulation of dust/fumes.
*   Exhaust appliances should be installed as near as possible to the point of origin of the dust/fume.

**5. Artificial Humidification (Section 15):**
*   If humidity is increased artificially, the water used must be from a public supply or purified drinking water source. It must not be injurious to health.

**6. Overcrowding (Section 16):**
*   To prevent overcrowding, there must be at least **[[14.2 cubic meters]] (500 cubic feet)** of space for every worker employed.
*   Space more than **4.2 meters (14 feet)** above the floor level is *not* taken into account for this calculation.

**7. Lighting (Section 17):**
*   Sufficient and suitable lighting (natural or artificial) must be provided.
*   Glazed windows/skylights must be kept clean.
*   Prevention of **glare** and formation of shadows that cause eye strain.

**8. Drinking Water (Section 18):**
*   Wholesome drinking water must be provided at convenient points.
*   Points must be marked "Drinking Water" in local language.
*   Water points must be at least **6 meters** away from any washing place, urinal, or latrine.
*   **Cool water** must be provided in hot weather if the factory employs more than **250 workers**.

**9. Latrines and Urinals (Section 19):**
*   Sufficient, accessible, and separate enclosed accommodation for males and females.
*   Adequately lighted, ventilated, and maintained in a clean sanitary condition.
*   Sweepers must be employed to keep them clean.

**10. Spittoons (Section 20):**
*   Sufficient number of spittoons must be provided at convenient places.
*   They must be maintained in a clean and hygienic condition.

### Key Statutory Requirements Based on Worker Strength

| Provision | Minimum No. of Workers Required |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cool Drinking Water** | > 250 workers |

### Clinical Relevance
*   **Notifiable Diseases:** Under the [[Factories Act]], distinct occupational diseases (like [[Silicosis]], [[Anthracosis]], [[Byssinosis]], [[Asbestosis]], Lead poisoning) are listed in the **Third Schedule**.
*   It is mandatory for the factory medical officer or any practitioner to notify these diseases to the Chief Inspector of Factories.
*   **Pre-placement & Periodic Examination:** The Act mandates fitness certification for young persons (adolescents 15-18 years) by a **Certifying Surgeon**.
