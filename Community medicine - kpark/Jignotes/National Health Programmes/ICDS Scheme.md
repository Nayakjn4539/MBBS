# Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) Scheme

Launched on **2nd October 1975**, the [[Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS)]] scheme represents one of the world’s largest and most unique programmes for early childhood care and development. It is the foremost symbol of India's commitment to her children.

**Nodal Ministry:** [[Ministry of Women and Child Development]] (Note: Health components are delivered by Ministry of Health & Family Welfare).

### **Objectives**
*To remember the objectives, use the Mnemonic: **M-D-R-C-C*** (Like Multi-Drug Resistant cases need special care, children need ICDS).

1.  **M**ortality reduction: To reduce the incidence of mortality, morbidity, malnutrition and school dropout.
2.  **D**evelopment: To lay the foundation for proper psychological, physical and social development of the child.
3.  **R**eferral/Health status: To improve the nutritional and health status of children in the age-group 0-6 years.
4.  **C**oordination: To achieve effective coordination of policy and implementation amongst the various departments to promote child development.
5.  **C**apability: To enhance the capability of the mother to look after the normal health and nutritional needs of the child through proper nutrition and health education.

---

### **Beneficiaries**
The scheme targets the most vulnerable groups:
1.  **Children:** 0 to 6 years.
2.  **Mothers:** Pregnant women and Lactating mothers.
3.  **Women:** 15–45 years (For Nutrition and Health Education).
4.  **Adolescent Girls:** 11–18 years (Under schemes like [[Kishori Shakti Yojana]] or SAG).

---

### **Package of Services**
The concept is based on the synergy of services; one service supports the other.

| **Service** | **Target Group** | **Service Provided By** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Supplementary Nutrition** | Children < 6 yrs, Pregnant & Lactating Mothers (P&LM) | [[Anganwadi Worker (AWW)]] & Helper |
| **2. Immunization** | Children < 6 yrs, Pregnant Women | ANM / MO (Assisted by AWW) |
| **3. Health Check-up** | Children < 6 yrs, P&LM | ANM / MO / AWW |
| **4. Referral Services** | Children < 6 yrs, P&LM | AWW / ANM / MO |
| **5. Pre-School Non-Formal Education** | Children 3–6 years | [[Anganwadi Worker (AWW)]] |
| **6. Nutrition & Health Education** | Women (15–45 yrs) | AWW / ANM / MO |

> **Note:** The health services (Immunization, Health Check-up, Referral) are delivered by the Health Department (Public Health Infrastructure), while the rest are delivered by the ICDS staff.

### **Infrastructure and Norms**

**The Anganwadi Centre (AWC)**
The AWC is the focal point for the delivery of services. It is the "Heart" of the ICDS system.

**Revised Population Norms for Setting up AWC:**

| **Project Area** | **Population Norm** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Rural / Urban Projects** | • 400 – 800: 1 AWC <br> • 800 – 1600: 2 AWCs <br> • 1600 – 2400: 3 AWCs |
| **Tribal / Riverine / Desert / Hilly Areas** | • 300 – 800: 1 AWC |
| **Mini-Anganwadi Centre** | • 150 – 400 (Rural/Urban) <br> • 150 – 300 (Tribal/Hilly) |

### **Revised Nutritional Norms (Supplementary Nutrition)**
Under the National Food Security Act, the nutritional norms were revised to bridge the calorie and protein gap. Support is provided for **300 days a year**.

| **Category** | **Type of Meal** | **Calories (Kcal)** | **Protein (g)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Children (6 months – 3 years)** | Take Home Ration (THR) | 500 | 12 – 15 |
| **Children (3 – 6 years)** | Morning Snack + Hot Cooked Meal | 500 | 12 – 15 |
| **Severely Malnourished (6m – 6y)** | Take Home Ration + Hot Cooked Meal | 800 | 20 – 25 |
| **Pregnant & Lactating Mothers** | Take Home Ration (THR) | 600 | 18 – 20 |

### **The Anganwadi Worker (AWW)**
*   **Selection:** A woman from the local community (honorary worker).
*   **Education:** Minimum Matriculation (10th Pass).
*   **Training:** 4 months induction training.
*   **Staffing:** 1 AWW and 1 Helper per centre.
*   **Key Function:** She acts as a bridge between the community and the government services. She maintains the **Growth Chart**.

---

### **Important Components & Updates**

**1. Growth Monitoring**
*   Uses **WHO Child Growth Standards (2006)**.
*   [[Mother and Child Protection Card (MCP Card)]] is used.
*   **Interpretation:**
    *   **Green Zone:** Normal
    *   **Yellow Zone:** Moderately Underweight
    *   **Red Zone:** Severely Underweight

**2. POSHAN Abhiyan (National Nutrition Mission)**
*   Launched in 2018.
*   Aims to reduce stunting, undernutrition, anemia, and low birth weight.
*   Target: **Mission 25 by 2022** (Reduce stunting to 25%).
*   Uses technology (ICDS-CAS/Poshan Tracker) for real-time monitoring.

**3. Adolescent Girls Scheme (SAG/Kishori Shakti Yojana)**
*   Targets out-of-school girls (11-14 years).
*   Provides nutrition, life skills, and health education.

**4. Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)**
*   Maternity benefit program implemented through ICDS platform.
*   Cash incentive for first living child.

---

### **Clinical Relevance**
*   **Malnutrition Management:** AWWs identify children in the "Red Zone" (Severe Acute Malnutrition) and refer them to [[Nutrition Rehabilitation Centres (NRC)]] or medical officers.
*   **Immunization:** AWC serves as the venue for the Village Health Nutrition and Sanitation Day (VHND), ensuring vaccine coverage.
