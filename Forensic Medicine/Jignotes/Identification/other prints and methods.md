# [[Identification]] > other prints and methods

### Other Methods of Identification and Prints

Apart from standard dactylography (fingerprints) and DNA profiling, several other biological markers and prints are utilized for establishing **[[identification]]**. These methods are particularly valuable in mass disasters, decomposed bodies, or when primary identifiers are missing.

---

### 1. Cheiloscopy (Lip Prints)
**Definition:** The study of the patterns of grooves and wrinkles on the labial mucosa (lips).
*   **Principle:** The grooves on the lips are unique to an individual and remain permanent throughout life (except in severe burns or trauma).
*   **Classification:** **[[Suzuki’s Classification]]** is the most commonly used method to grade lip prints.

| Type | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Type I** | Complete vertical grooves |
| **Type I'** | Partial vertical grooves |
| **Type II** | Branched grooves |
| **Type III** | Intersected grooves |
| **Type IV** | Reticular grooves |
| **Type V** | Undifferentiated / Irregular pattern |

*   **Collection:** Lip prints can be latent, visible, or plastic. They are often recovered from glasses, cigarette butts, straws, or clothing.
*   **Medicolegal Importance:**
    *   Unique to individuals.
    *   Can identify suspects from trace evidence left at a crime scene (e.g., on a glass).
    *   Useful for gender determination to some extent.

> **Mnemonic for Suzuki's Classification:**
> **V**ery **P**retty **B**irds **I**n **R**ed **U**nderwear
> (**V**ertical, **P**artial vertical, **B**ranched, **I**ntersected, **R**eticular, **U**ndifferentiated)

### 2. Palatoscopy (Rugoscopy)
**Definition:** The study of **[[palatal rugae]]** (plica palatinae) patterns on the anterior part of the hard palate.
*   **Anatomy:** Rugae are anatomical folds of fibrous connective tissue located on the anterior third of the palate behind the incisive papilla.
*   **Formation:** Formed by the 12th–14th week of intrauterine life.
*   **Advantages:**
    *   **Uniqueness:** No two palates are alike.
    *   **Stability:** The pattern remains unchanged throughout life.
    *   **Protection:** The rugae are well-protected by the lips, cheeks, and tongue. They are resistant to decomposition and thermal effects (burns).
*   **Medicolegal Importance:**
    *   Highly useful in identifying **[[mutilated bodies]]**, decomposed remains, and burnt bodies (air crashes) where fingerprints or faces are destroyed.
    *   Can be compared with ante-mortem dental casts (orthodontic records).

---

### 3. Podography (Footprints)
**Definition:** The study of impressions formed by the friction ridges on the soles of the feet.
*   **Principle:** Similar to fingerprints, the ridge patterns on the soles (loops, whorls, arches) are unique and permanent.
*   **Newborn Identification:** Footprints are routinely taken in hospitals for newborn identification as ridges are formed early in intrauterine life.
*   **Forensic Application:**
    *   Latent footprints found at crime scenes (in blood, dust, or mud) can link a suspect to the location.
    *   Useful when the offender is barefoot.

---

### 4. Onychoscopy (Nail Prints)
**Definition:** Identification based on the longitudinal striations present on the nail plate.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   Parallel longitudinal striations exist on the convex surface of fingernails and toenails.
    *   The distribution, number, and depth of these striations are unique to the individual.
    *   The pattern becomes more prominent with age but remains fundamentally unchanged.
*   **Limitations:** Nails grow continuously (approx. 3.2 mm/month) and can be damaged, filed, or lost.

---

### 5. Eye Biometrics (Iris and Retina)
These are advanced biometric methods with high specificity.

#### A. Iris Scan
*   **Principle:** The colored part of the eye (iris) contains a complex trabecular meshwork of connective tissue, crypts, and furrows.
*   **Uniqueness:** The pattern is formed randomly during fetal development and is unique even between **[[identical twins]]** (unlike DNA).
*   **Method:** A high-resolution camera captures the iris pattern using near-infrared light.

#### B. Retinal Scan
*   **Principle:** Analysis of the pattern of blood vessels on the **[[retina]]** (fundus) at the back of the eye.
*   **Uniqueness:** The vascular pattern is unique to each individual.
*   **Method:** Requires the subject to look into a scanning device; less convenient than iris scanning but highly secure.

---

### 6. Skeletal Patterns (Skull & Sinuses)

#### A. Frontal Sinus Patterns
*   **Principle:** The frontal sinuses are irregular, air-filled cavities within the frontal bone.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   The upper margins are scalloped.
    *   The size, shape, and septum placement are individual-specific.
    *   No two pairs of sinuses are the same.
*   **Medicolegal Importance:**
    *   Excellent for identification of skeletal remains (e.g., plane crashes) by comparing ante-mortem and post-mortem **[[radiographs]]** (X-rays).
    *   Female sinuses are generally smaller with more numerous scallops.

#### B. Sutural Patterns
*   **Principle:** The patterns of cranial sutures (joints between skull bones) are highly variable.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   Presence of **[[Wormian bones]]** (sutural bones/islands of bone) within the sutures provides definite points for comparison.
    *   Suture closure patterns can also assist in age estimation.

### 7. Vascular Grooves and Veins
*   **Veins on Hands:** Tamassia suggested that the arrangement of veins on the dorsum (back) of the hands is unique to each individual and can be used for identification.
*   **Vascular Grooves:** Impressions left by blood vessels on bones can sometimes be compared with radiological records.

---

### 8. Ear Prints
*   **Principle:** The morphological features of the external ear (helix, antihelix, tragus, antitragus, concha, and lobule) are unique.
*   **Application:**
    *   **Imprints:** Burglars often press their ears against doors or windows to listen for occupants, leaving latent ear prints (sebum/wax).
    *   **Biometrics:** The shape and dimensions of the ear are used in surveillance systems (Ear identification - FEARID project).

---

### 9. Nose Prints (Poroscopy)
*   **Poroscopy:** The study of the arrangement, size, and shape of sweat pores on the friction ridges.
*   **Nasal Application:** While distinct "nose prints" (like cattle muzzles) are not standard in humans, the pore structure on the nose and the morphological shape of the nose can be identifying features. However, **[[Locard's poroscopy]]** generally refers to sweat pores on fingers/palms.

| Method | Target Tissue | Key Feature | Primary Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cheiloscopy** | Lips | Grooves/Wrinkles | Crime scene trace evidence (glasses/cups) |
| **Rugoscopy** | Hard Palate | Palatal Rugae | Burnt/decomposed bodies (thermal resistant) |
| **Podography** | Foot Sole | Friction Ridges | Newborn ID; barefoot crime scenes |
