# [[HEALTH PROGRAMMES IN INDIA]] > Mention the package of services under RCH programme. Outline as to how the services are provided through different levels of health care facilities available in a block.

### Package of Services under [[Reproductive and Child Health (RCH)]] Programme

The [[RCH Programme]] aims to provide need-based, client-centered, demand-driven, and high-quality integrated services. The package utilizes a **"Lifecycle Approach"** or **"Continuum of Care"** covering all stages of life.

#### A. Major Components of the Package

**1. Maternal Health**
*   **Essential Obstetric Care:**
    *   **Antenatal Care (ANC):** Early registration (within 12 weeks), minimum 4 ANC visits, physical examination (BP, weight), and laboratory tests (Hb, Urine protein/sugar).
    *   **Nutrition:** Supplementation with **Iron and Folic Acid (IFA)** tablets and Calcium.
    *   **Immunization:** Tetanus Toxoid (TT) vaccination (2 doses or 1 booster).
    *   **Intranatal Care:** Promotion of [[Institutional Delivery]] (schemes like [[Janani Suraksha Yojana]] - JSY) and safe home deliveries by Skilled Birth Attendants (SBA) using the **"5 Cleans"**.
    *   **Postnatal Care (PNC):** Minimum 2 visits (now recommended more under HBNC) to monitor bleeding, infection, and breastfeeding.
    *   **Emergency Obstetric Care (EmOC):** Management of complications like hemorrhage, sepsis, eclampsia, and obstructed labor.

**2. Child Health**
*   **Essential Newborn Care:** Resuscitation, prevention of hypothermia (warm chain), prevention of infection, and early initiation of exclusive breastfeeding.
*   **Care of At-Risk Infants:** Management of [[Low Birth Weight (LBW)]] babies and Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC).
*   **Immunization:** Routine immunization against vaccine-preventable diseases under the [[Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)]].
*   **Nutrition:** Vitamin A prophylaxis and management of malnutrition.
*   **Disease Control:** Management of Diarrhea (ORS + Zinc) and Acute Respiratory Infections (ARI/Pneumonia).

**3. Reproductive Health & Family Planning**
*   **Contraception:** Promotion of spacing methods (IUCD, OCPs, Condoms) and terminal methods (Tubectomy, Vasectomy/NSV).
*   **Safe Abortion Services:** [[Medical Termination of Pregnancy (MTP)]] services to reduce maternal morbidity from unsafe abortions.
*   **RTI/STI Control:** Prevention, syndromic management, and referral of Reproductive Tract Infections and Sexually Transmitted Infections.

**4. Adolescent Health**
*   **ARSH (Adolescent Reproductive and Sexual Health):** Counseling, weekly IFA supplementation (WIFS), and menstrual hygiene promotion.

***

### Delivery of Services through Health Care Facilities in a Block

The services are delivered through a hierarchical **"Referral System"** ranging from the village level to the Community Health Centre (CHC) at the block level.

> [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Flowchart showing the hierarchy: Community -> Sub-Centre -> PHC -> CHC

#### 1. Village Level (Community Level)
*   **Key Functionaries:** [[ASHA]] (Accredited Social Health Activist), [[Anganwadi Worker]] (AWW), and Traditional Birth Attendant (Dai).
*   **Services Provided:**
    *   **Surveillance:** Identification of pregnant women and registration.
    *   **Counseling:** Health education on breastfeeding, nutrition, and hygiene.
    *   **Depot Holder:** Distribution of Iron Folic Acid (IFA), ORS packets, condoms, and oral contraceptive pills.
    *   **Events:** Organizing **Village Health and Nutrition Day (VHND)** monthly.
    *   **Referral:** Escorting pregnant women to institutions for delivery.

#### 2. Sub-Centre Level (The Most Peripheral Contact Point)
*   **Population Norm:** 5,000 (Plain) / 3,000 (Hilly/Tribal).
*   **Staff:** Female Health Worker (ANM) and Male Health Worker (MPW).
*   **Services Provided:**
    *   **MCH:** Full antenatal check-ups (urine albumin, Hb estimation), immunization sessions.
    *   **Delivery:** Conduction of normal deliveries (if equipped).
    *   **IUD Insertion:** Insertion of Copper-T.
    *   **Records:** Maintenance of Eligible Couple Register and tracking of dropouts.

#### 3. Primary Health Centre (PHC) Level
*   **Population Norm:** 30,000 (Plain) / 20,000 (Hilly/Tribal).
*   **Staff:** Medical Officer (MO), Pharmacist, Lab Technician, Nurse Midwife.
*   **Services Provided (24x7 Services):**
    *   **Medical Care:** First point of contact with a qualified doctor.
    *   **BEmOC:** Acts as a **Basic Emergency Obstetric Care (BEmOC)** centre.
    *   **Procedures:** MTP services, management of RTI/STI, and neonatal resuscitation.
    *   **Referral:** Refers complicated cases to CHC/FRU.

#### 4. Community Health Centre (CHC) / First Referral Unit (FRU)
*   **Population Norm:** 1,20,000 (Plain) / 80,000 (Hilly/Tribal).
*   **Staff:** Specialists (Obstetrician, Pediatrician, Surgeon, Anesthetist).
*   **Services Provided (CEmOC):** Acts as a **Comprehensive Emergency Obstetric Care (CEmOC)** centre.
*   **Critical Capabilities (FRU Criteria):**
    1.  **Surgical Interventions:** Caesarean Sections.
    2.  **Newborn Care:** Specialized care for sick newborns.
    3.  **Blood Storage:** Blood transfusion facilities.
*   **Functions:** Investigating adverse events and acting as a referral hub for 4 PHCs.

**Comparison of Obstetric Care Levels:**

| Feature | BEmOC (PHC Level) | CEmOC (CHC/FRU Level) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Parenteral Antibiotics** | Yes | Yes |
| **Parenteral Oxytocics** | Yes | Yes |
| **Manual Removal of Placenta**| Yes | Yes |
| **Assisted Delivery (Vacuum)** | Yes | Yes |
| **Surgery (C-Section)** | **No** | **Yes** |
| **Blood Transfusion** | **No** | **Yes** |

### Clinical Relevance: The 5 Cleans
To prevent neonatal tetanus and sepsis during delivery at the peripheral level (home/Sub-centre), the **5 Cleans** must be maintained:
1.  Clean **Hands**
2.  Clean **Surface**
3.  Clean **Blade** (for cutting cord)
4.  Clean **Tie** (for tying cord)
5.  Clean **Stump** (no application on the cord)
