# Spectrum of Iodine Deficiency Disorders (IDD)

Previously, iodine deficiency was equated only with **[[Goitre]]**. However, it is now recognized that iodine deficiency leads to a much wider spectrum of disorders commencing with intrauterine life and extending through childhood to adult life with serious health and social implications.

The term **[[Iodine Deficiency Disorders (IDD)]]** refers to all the effects of iodine deficiency on human growth and development which can be prevented by correction of iodine deficiency.

### 2. Endemic Cretinism

This is the most severe impact of iodine deficiency during fetal development. It is associated with endemic goitre and presents in two distinct types:

**A. Neurological Cretinism**
*   **Hearing/Speech:** **[[Deaf-mutism]]** (most characteristic).
*   **Motor:** Spasticity and rigidity (proximal), mainly affecting lower limbs.
*   **Ocular:** Squint (Strabismus).
*   **Mental:** Mental deficiency.
*   **Gait:** Disorders of stance and gait.

**B. Myxedematous Cretinism**
*   **Growth:** Severe **[[Short Stature]]** (Dwarfism).
*   **Appearance:** Coarse facial features, dry skin.
*   **Hormonal:** Clinical signs of hypothyroidism (Myxedema) without deaf-mutism.
*   **Mental:** Severe mental retardation.

### 3. Clinical Relevance & Impact

*   **Brain Damage:** Iodine deficiency is the world's single most significant cause of **[[preventable brain damage]]** and mental retardation.
*   **IQ Loss:** The **[[Intelligence Quotient (IQ)]]** score of children living in an iodine-deficient environment is nearly **13 IQ points less** than those living in iodine-sufficient environments.
*   **Hidden Hunger:** Much of the population is affected by "invisible" effects (reduced intellectual capacity) rather than obvious goitre, often referred to as **[[Hidden Hunger]]**.
*   **Social Impact:** The social impact arises not so much from goitre but from the effect on the **[[Central Nervous System]]**.
