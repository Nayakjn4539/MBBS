# Conjunctivitis

**Definition:** [[Conjunctivitis]] is the inflammation of the [[conjunctiva]] characterized by conjunctival hyperaemia associated with discharge which may be watery, mucoid, mucopurulent, or purulent.

### Classification of Conjunctivitis
Conjunctivitis is broadly classified based on etiology,:
1.  **Infective:**
    *   **Bacterial:** Acute, Hyperacute (Purulent), Chronic, Angular.
    *   **Viral:** Adenoviral, Enteroviral, Herpes Simplex, Molluscum Contagiosum.
    *   **Chlamydial:** Trachoma, Adult Inclusion Conjunctivitis, Neonatal.
    *   **Fungal/Parasitic/Granulomatous.**
2.  **Allergic:**
    *   Simple (Seasonal/Perennial).
    *   Vernal Keratoconjunctivitis (VKC).
    *   Atopic Keratoconjunctivitis (AKC).
    *   Giant Papillary Conjunctivitis (GPC).
    *   Phlyctenular Conjunctivitis.
3.  **Cicatricial:** Ocular Mucous Membrane Pemphigoid, Stevens-Johnson Syndrome.
4.  **Toxic/Chemical:** Drug-induced, irritants.

---

### General Clinical Features
**Symptoms:**
*   **Redness (Hyperemia):** "Pink eye" appearance.
*   **Discharge:** Sticking of eyelids together (especially in the morning).
*   **Foreign Body Sensation:** Grittiness.
*   **Discomfort/Burning.**
*   **Photophobia:** Usually mild; if severe, implies corneal involvement.
*   **Blurred Vision:** Transient, clears on blinking (due to mucus flakes).

**Signs:**
1.  **Conjunctival Congestion:** Superficial, bright red, more marked in fornices, less at limbus (unlike ciliary congestion). Blanching occurs with vasoconstrictors (e.g., Phenylephrine).
2.  **Chemosis:** Edema of the conjunctiva.
3.  **Discharge:** Nature of discharge helps in differential diagnosis.
4.  **Reaction Patterns:**
    *   **[[Papillae]]:** Hypertrophied vascular structures with a central vessel. Seen in **Allergic** and **Bacterial** conjunctivitis,.
    *   **[[Follicles]]:** Lymphoid aggregates (look like sago grains/rice). Avascular center. Seen in **Viral** and **Chlamydial** infections,.
5.  **Membranes:**
    *   **True Membrane:** Bleeds on peeling (e.g., Diphtheria, Beta-hemolytic Streptococcus).
    *   **Pseudomembrane:** Does not bleed on peeling (e.g., Adenovirus, Gonococcus, Autoimmune).

| Feature | Bacterial | Viral | Allergic | Chlamydial |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Discharge** | Mucopurulent/Purulent | Watery/Serous | Ropy/Stringy (White) | Mucopurulent |
| **Reaction** | Papillary | Follicular | Papillary | Follicular |
| **Itching** | Minimal | Minimal | Severe (Hallmark) | Minimal |
| **Nodes** | Occasional | Frequent (Pre-auricular) | Absent | Frequent |

### 1. Bacterial Conjunctivitis

#### A. Acute Mucopurulent Conjunctivitis ("Pink Eye")
*   **Etiology:** *Staphylococcus aureus* (Most common), *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (Pneumococcus), *Koch-Weeks bacillus* (*Haemophilus aegyptius*).
*   **Clinical Features:**
    *   "Fiery red eye".
    *   Flakes of mucopus in fornices/canthi.
    *   Sticking of lid margins (matting of eyelashes) upon waking.
    *   *Pneumococcus* causes petechial [[subconjunctival haemorrhage]].
*   **Treatment:**
    *   Topical broad-spectrum antibiotics (Chloramphenicol, Moxifloxacin, Tobramycin).
    *   Frequent irrigation with saline.
    *   **Do not bandage** (increases temperature and bacterial growth).

#### B. Hyperacute (Purulent) Conjunctivitis
*   **Etiology:** *[[Neisseria gonorrhoeae]]* (Gonococcus).
*   **Clinical Features:**
    *   **Copious purulent discharge** (re-accumulates seconds after cleaning).
    *   Severe lid swelling and chemosis.
    *   Tender **pre-auricular lymphadenopathy**.
    *   **Clinical Implication:** Gonococci can penetrate **intact corneal epithelium**, leading to corneal ulceration and perforation.
*   **Treatment:**
    *   Systemic Ceftriaxone (IM/IV) + Topical antibiotics.
    *   Frequent saline lavage.

#### C. Angular Conjunctivitis
*   **Etiology:** *[[Moraxella axenfeld]]* (diplobacillus),.
*   **Pathology:** Proteolytic enzymes produced by the bacteria macerate the epithelium at the angles.
*   **Clinical Features:** Congestion and excoriation of skin at the **canthi** (angles of the eye).
*   **Treatment:** Zinc boric drops (inhibits proteolytic enzyme) or Tetracycline/Erythromycin ointment.

### 2. Viral Conjunctivitis

#### A. Adenoviral Conjunctivitis
*   **Epidemic Keratoconjunctivitis (EKC):** Serotypes **8, 19, 37**. Highly contagious.
    *   **Signs:** Follicles, pseudomembranes, subepithelial corneal infiltrates (nummular keratitis) causing glare/halos.
*   **Pharyngoconjunctival Fever (PCF):** Serotypes **3, 4, 7**.
    *   **Triad:** Pharyngitis + Fever + Follicular Conjunctivitis.
*   **Treatment:** Mostly supportive (lubricants, cold compresses). Topical steroids only for severe membrane formation or vision-threatening corneal infiltrates.

#### B. Acute Hemorrhagic Conjunctivitis (Apollo Conjunctivitis)
*   **Etiology:** **[[Enterovirus 70]]**, **Coxsackie A24**.
*   **Features:** Short incubation (<24hrs). Profuse watering, **subconjunctival hemorrhages** (frank blood), follicles.
*   **Treatment:** Self-limiting (5-7 days).

---

### 3. Chlamydial Conjunctivitis

#### A. Trachoma (Egyptian Ophthalmia)
*   **Etiology:** *[[Chlamydia trachomatis]]* Serotypes **A, B, Ba, C**.
*   **Vector:** Flies (Musca sorbens).
*   **Pathology:** Chronic keratoconjunctivitis affecting superficial epithelium.
*   **WHO Classification (FISTO):**
    1.  **TF (Trachomatous Inflammation - Follicular):** >5 follicles (>0.5mm) on upper tarsal conjunctiva.
    2.  **TI (Trachomatous Inflammation - Intense):** Thickening obscuring >50% deep tarsal vessels.
    3.  **TS (Trachomatous Scarring):** Fibrous bands (Arlt's line).
    4.  **TT (Trachomatous Trichiasis):** At least one eyelash rubbing the globe.
    5.  **CO (Corneal Opacity):** Visible opacity over pupil.
*   **Clinical Signs:**
    *   **Sago Grain Follicles:** Necrosis/Leber cells.
    *   **[[Arlt's Line]]:** Linear scar in sulcus subtarsalis.
    *   **[[Herbert's Pits]]:** Cicatrized limbal follicles (Pathognomonic).
    *   **Pannus:** Vascularization of upper cornea.
*   **Treatment (SAFE Strategy):**
    *   **S**urgery (for Trichiasis).
    *   **A**ntibiotics (Azithromycin 1g single dose or Tetracycline ointment).
    *   **F**acial Cleanliness.
    *   **E**nvironmental Improvement.

#### B. Adult Inclusion Conjunctivitis
*   **Etiology:** *Chlamydia trachomatis* Serotypes **D to K**.
*   **Source:** Genital secretions (STD). "Swimming pool conjunctivitis".
*   **Signs:** Acute follicular conjunctivitis, **larger follicles in lower fornix** (unlike Trachoma which is upper).
*   **Treatment:** Systemic Azithromycin or Doxycycline.

---

### 4. Allergic Conjunctivitis

#### A. Vernal Keratoconjunctivitis (VKC) / Spring Catarrh
*   **Epidemiology:** Young boys (5-15 yrs), hot/dry climates. Type I Hypersensitivity (IgE mediated).
*   **Symptoms:** **Intense Itching**, ropy/stringy white discharge, photophobia.
*   **Forms:**
    1.  **Palpebral:** **Cobblestone Papillae** (flat-topped, hexagonal) on upper tarsal conjunctiva,.
    2.  **Bulbar:** Gelatinous thickening at limbus, **[[Horner-Trantas Dots]]** (eosinophils + epithelial debris).
    3.  **Mixed.**
*   **Complications:** **[[Shield Ulcer]]** (transversely oval corneal ulcer), Keratoconus.
*   **Treatment:**
    *   Mast cell stabilizers (Sodium Cromoglycate).
    *   Dual action (Olopatadine).
    *   Topical Steroids (for acute exacerbations/shield ulcers) - *Monitor IOP*.

#### B. Phlyctenular Conjunctivitis
*   **Etiology:** Type IV (Delayed) Hypersensitivity to endogenous microbial proteins (e.g., **[[Tuberculosis]]**, **Staphylococcus**).
*   **Clinical Feature:** **Phlycten** (pinkish-white nodule) near the limbus with a leash of blood vessels.
*   **Treatment:** Topical Steroids, treat underlying cause (TB/blepharitis).

---

### 5. Ophthalmia Neonatorum
Conjunctivitis occurring within the first **28 days** (1 month) of life.

| Etiology | Onset | Features | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chemical** | < 24 hours | Mild redness (Silver nitrate) | Self-limiting,. |
| **Gonococcal** | 24 - 48 hours | **Hyperacute**, copious pus, corneal perforation risk | Systemic Ceftriaxone + Saline wash,. |
| **Bacterial** | 5 - 7 days | Mucopurulent | Topical antibiotics. |
| **HSV-2** | 5 - 14 days | Vesicles, keratitis | Systemic/Topical Acyclovir. |
| **Chlamydia** | 1 - 2 weeks | **Most Common**, mucopurulent, *No follicles* (adenoid layer absent in infants) | Oral Erythromycin (prevent pneumonia),. |

**Mnemonic for Onset:**
*   **C**hemical (1 day)
*   **G**onococcal (2 days)
*   **B**acterial (5 days)
*   **H**erpes (1 week)
*   **C**hlamydia (1-2 weeks)
*(Can Go Be Home Child)*
