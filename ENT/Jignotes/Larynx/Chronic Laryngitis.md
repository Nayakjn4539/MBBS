# Chronic Laryngitis

**Definition**
[[Chronic Laryngitis]] is a diffuse or localized inflammatory condition of the laryngeal mucosa that persists for **more than 3 weeks**. It is characterized by irreversible pathological changes in the mucosa, submucosa, and glands of the [[Larynx]].

## Classification
Chronic laryngitis is broadly classified into two categories:

1.  **Chronic Non-Specific Laryngitis**
    *   Chronic Simple (Hyperemic) Laryngitis
    *   Chronic Hypertrophic (Hyperplastic) Laryngitis (Diffuse or Localized)
    *   Atrophic Laryngitis ([[Laryngitis Sicca]])
2.  **Chronic Specific Laryngitis** (Granulomatous diseases)
    *   [[Tuberculosis]]
    *   [[Syphilis]]
    *   [[Scleroma]]
    *   [[Leprosy]]
    *   Fungal Infections ([[Laryngeal Mycosis]])

---

## Etiology and Risk Factors
The condition is multifactorial. Common causes include:

*   **Infection:** Recurrent acute laryngitis, chronic infection in paranasal sinuses ([[Chronic Sinusitis]]), tonsils ([[Chronic Tonsillitis]]), or chest (Bronchitis/Bronchiectasis).
*   **Irritants:** Chronic exposure to cigarette smoke, alcohol, dust, or industrial fumes.
*   **Vocal Abuse:** Faulty voice production, excessive shouting, or "Pharyngeal Neurosis" (constant throat clearing).
*   **Gastroesophageal Reflux ([[Laryngopharyngeal Reflux]] - LPR):** Acid bathing the posterior larynx.
*   **Systemic Disorders:** Diabetes, hypothyroidism, kidney disease.
*   **Mouth Breathing:** Due to nasal obstruction (DNS, polyps).

**Mnemonic for Causes: "V-RIPS"**
*   **V** - Voice Abuse
*   **R** - Reflux (LPR/GERD)
*   **I** - Infections (Sinus/Chest)
*   **P** - Pollution/Occupation
*   **S** - Smoking/Spirits (Alcohol)

## 1. Chronic Non-Specific Laryngitis

### A. Chronic Simple (Hyperemic) Laryngitis
This is a diffuse inflammatory condition symmetrically involving the whole larynx.

*   **Pathology:** Vasodilatation, hyperemia, and sticky mucopurulent exudate.
*   **Symptoms:**
    *   **Hoarseness:** Worse in the morning, improves as the day progresses (initially).
    *   **Hawking:** Constant desire to clear the throat.
    *   **Dry Cough:** Irritating and dry.
    *   **Sore Throat:** Raw sensation/discomfort.
*   **Signs (Laryngoscopy):**
    *   Mucosa is dusky red (hyperemic).
    *   [[Vocal Cords]] lose their pearly white luster and appear dull red/pink.
    *   Edges of cords appear rounded.
    *   Flecks of viscid mucus on cords and interarytenoid region.

### B. Chronic Hypertrophic (Hyperplastic) Laryngitis
This represents an advanced stage where permanent pathological changes occur.

*   **Pathology:**
    *   Hyperemia and edema of submucosa.
    *   **Squamous Metaplasia:** Pseudostratified ciliated epithelium changes to stratified squamous epithelium.
    *   Hyperplasia and Keratinization.
    *   Glandular hypertrophy followed by atrophy.
*   **Types:**
    1.  **Diffuse:** General thickening of laryngeal structures.
    2.  **Localized:** Manifests as discrete lesions like [[Vocal Nodules]], [[Vocal Polyp]], [[Reinke's Edema]], [[Leukoplakia]], or [[Contact Ulcer]].

#### Pachydermia Laryngis (Specific form of Hypertrophic Laryngitis)
*   **Site:** Affects the **posterior part** of the larynx (Interarytenoid region and posterior vocal cords).
*   **Etiology:** Chronic alcoholism, heavy smoking, and GERD.
*   **Signs:**
    *   Heaping up of red/grey granulation tissue in the interarytenoid region.
    *   "Kissing ulcers" or contact ulcers may be seen on vocal processes.
*   **Clinical Implication:** It does not usually undergo malignant change but biopsy is essential to rule out TB or Cancer.

### C. Atrophic Laryngitis (Laryngitis Sicca)
Characterized by atrophy of the laryngeal mucosa and crust formation. Often associated with [[Atrophic Rhinitis]] (Ozena).

*   **Population:** More common in women.
*   **Symptoms:**
    *   Hoarseness (improves temporarily after coughing out crusts).
    *   Foul smell from the mouth (Halitosis).
    *   Dyspnea (if crusts obstruct the airway).
*   **Signs:**
    *   Mucosa is pale, dry, and atrophic.
    *   Greenish/black foul-smelling crusts cover the larynx and trachea.
    *   Removal of crusts reveals bleeding (excoriated) mucosa.

## 2. Chronic Specific Laryngitis (Granulomatous)

### A. Laryngeal Tuberculosis
Almost always secondary to [[Pulmonary Tuberculosis]].

*   **Pathology:** Affects the **Posterior Larynx** most commonly (Interarytenoid fold > Ventricular bands > Vocal cords > Epiglottis).
    *   *Bronchogenic spread:* Sputum pools in the posterior commissure.
*   **Symptoms:**
    *   Weak voice/Hoarseness.
    *   **Severe Odynophagia** (pain on swallowing) - *Cardinal symptom*.
    *   Referred [[Otalgia]].
*   **Signs (Laryngoscopy):**
    1.  **Mouse-Nibbled Appearance:** Ulceration of vocal cords.
    2.  **Turban Epiglottis:** Pseudoedema of the epiglottis.
    3.  **Mamillated Arytenoids:** Swelling of arytenoids.
    4.  **Posterior Commissure:** Pallor and granulation tissue.
*   **Diagnosis:** Sputum for AFB, Chest X-ray, Biopsy.
*   **Treatment:** Anti-Tubercular Therapy (ATT).

### B. Syphilis of Larynx
*   **Stage:** Usually Tertiary Syphilis ([[Gumma]]).
*   **Site:** Affects the **Anterior Larynx** (Epiglottis) > Posterior.
*   **Appearance:** Punched out deep ulcers, wash-leather slough. Painless.

### C. Scleroma of Larynx
*   **Organism:** *Klebsiella rhinoscleromatis*.
*   **Site:** **Subglottic region**.
*   **Signs:** Smooth, red subglottic swelling causing stenosis.
*   **Histology:** [[Mikulicz Cells]] and Russell bodies.

## Differential Diagnosis of Chronic Laryngitis

| Feature | **Tuberculosis** | **Syphilis** | **Carcinoma (Malignancy)** | **Chronic Non-Specific** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Site** | Posterior Larynx (Arytenoids) | Anterior Larynx (Epiglottis) | Any part (Glottis/Supraglottis) | Vocal cords (Diffuse) |
| **Pain** | **Severe Odynophagia** | Painless | Late feature | Discomfort/Rawness |
| **Appearance** | Mouse-nibbled ulcer, Turban epiglottis, Pale | Punched out ulcer, Gumma | Proliferative/Ulcerative growth | Congestion, Edema, Nodules |
| **Mobility** | Impaired early (mechanical) | Normal until late | **Fixation** (Early in glottic Ca) | Usually Normal |
| **Voice** | Weak/Aphonic | Rough/Hoarse | Hoarse | Hoarse |

## Management of Chronic Laryngitis

### 1. Conservative Management
*   **Vocal Hygiene:** strict voice rest (absolute silence for acute exacerbations, relative rest for chronic).
*   **Elimination of Irritants:** Cessation of smoking and alcohol. Avoidance of dust/fumes.
*   **Treat Focal Sepsis:** Management of sinusitis, tonsillitis, and dental sepsis.
*   **Steam Inhalation:** With Tincture Benzoin or Menthol to moisten secretions.
*   **Expectorants:** To loosen viscid mucus (e.g., Guaifenesin).
*   **Anti-Reflux Therapy:** Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) and lifestyle modification for LPR.

### 2. Speech Therapy
*   Essential for functional disorders, nodules, and after surgery to prevent recurrence.
*   Corrects faulty voice production mechanisms.

### 3. Surgical Management
*   **Microlaryngeal Surgery (MLS):**
    *   **Stripping/Decortication:** Removal of hyperplastic/edematous mucosa (for diffuse hypertrophy or Reinke's edema). Done one cord at a time to prevent webbing.
    *   **Excision:** For localized lesions like Polyps or Nodules.
    *   **Biopsy:** Mandatory in cases of suspicious leukoplakia, ulceration, or asymmetrical swelling to rule out [[Carcinoma Larynx]].

### 4. Specific Treatment
*   **Atrophic Laryngitis:** Laryngeal sprays with glucose in glycerin (to prevent crusting), potassium iodide (to increase secretions).
*   **Tuberculosis:** ATT (Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol).
