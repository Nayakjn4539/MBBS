# [[Diseases Of Cornea]] > Corneal Disorders table

### 1. Infective Keratitis (Corneal Ulcers)

This section covers microbial infections leading to [[Corneal Ulcer]] formation.

| Disease/Condition | Defect (Clinical Features/Pathology) | Etiopathogenesis (Cause) | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Bacterial Keratitis]]** | • **Epithelial defect** with underlying stromal infiltration.<br>• **Suppuration** and necrosis.<br>• **[[Hypopyon]]**: Sterile, mobile pus in the anterior chamber.<br>• **Ulcus Serpens**: Serpentine/creeping ulcer (Pneumococcus). | • **Etiology**: *Staphylococcus aureus* (m/c worldwide), *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (m/c in India), *Pseudomonas* (contact lens users).<br>• **Risk Factors**: Trauma, contact lenses, dry eye, steroids.<br>• **Pathogenesis**: Organism adheres to damaged epithelium $\rightarrow$ invades stroma $\rightarrow$ necrosis. | • **Topical Antibiotics**: Fortified drops (e.g., Cefazolin 5% + Tobramycin 1.3%) or fluoroquinolones (Moxifloxacin, Gatifloxacin).<br>• **Cycloplegics**: [[Atropine]] 1% to relieve pain and prevent posterior synechiae.<br>• **Systemic Antibiotics**: For [[N. gonorrhoeae]] or threatened perforation. |
| **[[Fungal Keratitis]] (Mycotic)** | • **Dry-looking ulcer** with grey-white elevated margins.<br>• **Feathery margins** and finger-like extensions.<br>• **Satellite lesions**.<br>• **Immune Ring of Wessley**.<br>• **Hypopyon**: Thick, immobile, non-sterile (fungi penetrate intact Descemet's). | • **Filamentous**: *[[Aspergillus]]* (m/c), *Fusarium* (vegetative matter trauma).<br>• **Yeasts**: *Candida* (immunocompromised/chronic steroid use).<br>• **Pathogenesis**: Fungi proliferate in stroma with less inflammatory destruction initially compared to bacteria. | • **Filamentous**: Topical **[[Natamycin]]** 5% (DOC).<br>• **Yeasts**: Topical **Amphotericin B** 0.15% or Voriconazole.<br>• **Contraindication**: **Corticosteroids** are strictly contraindicated. |
| **[[Viral Keratitis]] (HSV)** | • **Epithelial**: Punctate keratitis $\rightarrow$ **Dendritic ulcer** (branching pattern with terminal bulbs) $\rightarrow$ **Geographic ulcer**.<br>• **Stromal**: [[Disciform Keratitis]] (central stromal edema, no necrosis) or Necrotizing stromal keratitis.<br>• **Decreased Corneal Sensation**. | • **Agent**: [[Herpes Simplex Virus]] (HSV-1).<br>• **Pathogenesis**: Reactivation of latent virus in Trigeminal ganglion. Epithelial disease is active replication; Disciform is Type IV hypersensitivity. | • **Epithelial**: Topical **[[Acyclovir]]** 3% ointment or Ganciclovir gel. Debridement.<br>• **Stromal (Disciform)**: Topical **Corticosteroids** (to suppress inflammation) *PLUS* Antiviral cover (to prevent epithelial recurrence). |
| **[[Herpes Zoster Ophthalmicus]] (HZO)** | • **Pseudodendrites** (tapered ends, no terminal bulbs).<br>• **Nummular Keratitis** (coin-shaped subepithelial deposits).<br>• **Hutchinson’s Sign**: Vesicles on tip of nose (nasociliary nerve involvement). | • **Agent**: [[Varicella Zoster Virus]] (VZV).<br>• **Pathogenesis**: Reactivation in the Gasserian ganglion involving the Ophthalmic division ($V_1$) of CN V. | • **Systemic**: Oral Acyclovir (800mg 5x/day) or Valacyclovir/Famciclovir within 72 hours.<br>• **Topical**: Lubricants, antibiotics for secondary infection, steroids for stromal involvement. |
| **[[Acanthamoeba Keratitis]]** | • **Severe Pain** out of proportion to signs.<br>• **Radial Keratoneuritis** (infiltrates along nerves).<br>• **Ring Abscess** (late sign).<br>• Pseudodendrites. | • **Agent**: *Acanthamoeba* (protozoa).<br>• **Risk Factors**: **Contact lens** wear (homemade saline, swimming), trauma with contaminated water/mud.<br>• **Pathogenesis**: Trophozoites penetrate epithelium. | • **Biguanides**: **PHMB** (0.02%) or **Chlorhexidine** (0.02%).<br>• **Diamidines**: Propamidine isethionate (Brolene).<br>• **Note**: Treatment is prolonged (months). |

**Clinical Implication:**
*   **Hypopyon Differences:** Bacterial hypopyon is usually sterile and mobile. Fungal hypopyon is often thick, immobile, and may contain fungal elements.
*   **Steroid Warning:** Never use steroids in pure epithelial HSV keratitis (dendritic ulcer) or Fungal keratitis; it worsens the disease ("Geographic ulcer" in HSV).

> [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Dendritic Ulcer vs Pseudodendrite
*   *Dendritic (HSV):* True branching, terminal bulbs, stains with Fluorescein.
*   *Pseudodendrite (HZO/Acanthamoeba):* Painted-on appearance, no terminal bulbs, stains poorly/negative staining.

### 2. Corneal Dystrophies

These are bilateral, hereditary, non-inflammatory, and progressive disorders affecting specific layers.

| Dystrophy | Layer Affected | Defect / Findings | Etiopathogenesis / Stain | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Cogan's Dystrophy]]** (EBMD) | Epithelium / Basement Membrane | • **Map-Dot-Fingerprint** lines.<br>• **Recurrent Corneal Erosions** (painful upon waking). | • Most common anterior dystrophy.<br>• Poor adhesion of epithelium. | • Hypertonic saline drops.<br>• Bandage Contact Lens.<br>• **PTK** (Phototherapeutic Keratectomy). |
| **[[Reis-Bucklers Dystrophy]]** | Bowman's Layer | • Geographic, honeycomb, or fish-net opacities.<br>• Severe recurrent erosions. | • AD Inheritance.<br>• Mutation in **TGFBI** gene. | • Superficial Keratectomy.<br>• **PTK**.<br>• Lamellar Keratoplasty. |
| **[[Lattice Dystrophy]]** | Stroma | • Refractile, branching **lattice lines** (spider-web).<br>• Central haze later. | • **Amyloid** deposits.<br>• Stain: **Congo Red** (Apple-green birefringence). | • **Keratoplasty** (DALK or PK) when vision drops.<br>• High recurrence rate in grafts. |
| **[[Granular Dystrophy]]** | Stroma | • Discrete, crumb-like white opacities ("bread crumbs").<br>• Clear intervening stroma (initially). | • **Hyaline** deposits.<br>• Stain: **Masson Trichrome** (Red). | • PTK (if superficial).<br>• **Keratoplasty** (DALK or PK). |
| **[[Macular Dystrophy]]** | Stroma | • Diffuse clouding with focal grey-white spots.<br>• Extends to **Limbus** (others spare limbus).<br>• Thinned cornea. | • **Mucopolysaccharide** (Glycosaminoglycan).<br>• Stain: **Alcian Blue** / Colloidal Iron.<br>• **Autosomal Recessive** (others are AD). | • **Penetrating Keratoplasty** (PK). |
| **[[Fuchs Endothelial Dystrophy]]** | Endothelium | • **Cornea Guttata** (beaten metal appearance).<br>• Stromal edema $\rightarrow$ Epithelial bullae (**[[Bullous Keratopathy]]**).<br>• Morning blur (worse on waking). | • Loss of endothelial pumps ($Na^+/K^+$ ATPase).<br>• **Polymegathism** and **Pleomorphism** of cells.<br>• Common in females. | • Hypertonic saline (5% NaCl).<br>• **DSEK/DMEK** (Endothelial Keratoplasty) is the gold standard. |

**Mnemonic for Stromal Dystrophies:**
"**M**arilyn **M**onroe **A**lways **G**ets **H**er **M**en in **L**. **A**. **C**ity"
*   **M**acular = **M**ucopolysaccharide (**A**lcian blue)
*   **G**ranular = **H**yaline (**M**asson trichrome)
*   **L**attice = **A**myloid (**C**ongo red)

### 3. Ectatic and Degenerative Disorders

Conditions involving thinning, bulging, or deposits due to age/environment.

| Disease | Defect / Clinical Signs | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Keratoconus]]** | • Conical protrusion of cornea.<br>• **[[Munson's Sign]]** (V-shape lower lid on downgaze).<br>• **Vogt’s Striae** (stress lines).<br>• **Fleischer’s Ring** (Iron at base of cone).<br>• **Scissoring Reflex** on retinoscopy. | • Non-inflammatory thinning.<br>• Associations: Down syndrome, VKC, Eye rubbing, Marfan’s. | • **Early**: Rigid Gas Permeable (RGP) contact lenses.<br>• **Progression**: **C3R** (Collagen Cross-Linking).<br>• **Late**: Intacs (rings) or **Keratoplasty** (DALK/PK). |
| **[[Keratoglobus]]** | • Globular protrusion of the *entire* cornea.<br>• Generalized thinning (periphery to center). | • Congenital/Hereditary.<br>• Associated with Ehlers-Danlos, Blue Sclera. | • Protection from trauma (prone to rupture).<br>• Scleral contact lenses.<br>• Tectonic keratoplasty (difficult). |
| **[[Arcus Senilis]]** | • Grey-white lipid ring in periphery.<br>• **Lucid interval of Vogt** (clear zone between opacity and limbus). | • Lipid deposition in stroma.<br>• Age-related (normal >60y).<br>• If <40y (Arcus Juvenilis) $\rightarrow$ Check lipid profile. | • No treatment required (does not affect vision). |
| **[[Band Shaped Keratopathy]]** | • Horizontal grey-white band across interpalpebral zone.<br>• "Swiss-cheese" holes (nerves penetrating Bowman’s). | • **Calcium** deposition in Bowman’s layer.<br>• Causes: Chronic Uveitis (JIA), Silicone oil, Hypercalcemia, Phthisis bulbi. | • **Chelation**: Scraping epithelium + **EDTA** (Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid).<br>• PTK. |
| **[[Pterygium]]** | • Triangular fibrovascular growth onto cornea.<br>• Destroys Bowman’s layer.<br>• **Stocker’s Line** (Iron deposit at head). | • UV radiation (sunlight), dust, dry heat.<br>• Elastotic degeneration of conjunctiva. | • Surgical excision with **Conjunctival Autograft** (CAG) + fibrin glue/sutures (low recurrence).<br>• Mitomycin C (to prevent recurrence). |
| **[[Mooren's Ulcer]]** | • Peripheral ulcerative keratitis.<br>• Painful, progressive excavation with overhanging edge. | • Autoimmune/Idiopathic.<br>• Type 1 (Old, Unilateral, benign). Type 2 (Young, Bilateral, aggressive). | • Topical Steroids.<br>• Immunosuppressants.<br>• Conjunctival resection (peritomy). |

### 4. Immune-Mediated and Miscellaneous Disorders

| Disease | Defect / Signs | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Phlyctenular Keratoconjunctivitis]]** | • **Phlycten**: Pinkish-white nodule near limbus.<br>• May migrate onto cornea (fascicular ulcer). | • Type IV Hypersensitivity to endogenous microbial proteins (e.g., **[[Tuberculosis]]**, *Staph. aureus*, worms). | • Topical Steroids.<br>• Antibiotics.<br>• Treat systemic cause (e.g., Mantoux test, deworming). |
| **[[Vernal Keratoconjunctivitis]]** (VKC) | • **Shield Ulcer** (Transverse/oval ulcer in upper cornea).<br>• **Vernal Plaques** (mucus/fibrin coating).<br>• **Pseudogerontoxon** (Cupid's bow opacity). | • Type I & IV Hypersensitivity (Allergic).<br>• Boys, hot climates (Spring Catarrh). | • Mast cell stabilizers (Olopatadine).<br>• Topical Steroids (pulsed).<br>• Shield ulcer: Debridement + scrape plaque. |
| **[[Neurotrophic Keratopathy]]** | • Persistent epithelial defect $\rightarrow$ Ulceration.<br>• **Painless** (Anesthetic cornea). | • Damage to **CN V** (Trigeminal).<br>• Causes: Herpes (HSV/HZO), Diabetes, acoustic neuroma surgery, Leprosy. | • Lubricants (preservative-free).<br>• Tarsorrhaphy (lid closure).<br>• Amniotic membrane graft. |
| **[[Keratomalacia]]** | • Dull, lusterless cornea (Xerosis).<br>• Colliquative necrosis (melting).<br>• Bilateral. | • **Vitamin A Deficiency**.<br>• Common in malnourished children. | • Systemic **Vitamin A** (Oral/IM) immediately.<br>• Lubricants/Antibiotics.<br>• Treat malnutrition. |
