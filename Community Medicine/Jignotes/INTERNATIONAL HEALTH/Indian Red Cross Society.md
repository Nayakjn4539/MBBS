# [[INTERNATIONAL HEALTH]] > Indian Red Cross Society

### Indian Red Cross Society (IRCS)

The [[Indian Red Cross Society]] is a premier voluntary, humanitarian organization in India, established in **1920** under the Indian Red Cross Society Act. It is a part of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement.

> [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Red Cross Emblem (Red Cross on a white background)

#### Historical Background
*   **Founder of Red Cross:** [[Henry Dunant]], a Swiss businessman, founded the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) after witnessing the suffering at the Battle of Solferino (1859).
*   **Establishment in India:** The Indian branch was established in **1920** by an Act of the Indian Legislature.
*   **Network:** It has a network of over **400 branches** across India.

#### Objectives (The 3 Pillars)
The primary objectives of the society can be remembered using the Mnemonic **IMP**:
1.  **I**mprovement of health.
2.  **M**itigation of suffering.
3.  **P**revention of disease.

#### Principal Activities and Functions

**1. Disaster Relief Work**
*   The society plays a crucial role in [[Disaster Management]].
*   During natural calamities like floods, earthquakes, droughts, and epidemics, it mobilizes resources to aid victims.
*   **Supplies provided:** Milk powder, medicines, vitamins, clothes, blankets, and other essential items.

**2. Services to Armed Forces**
*   Care for the sick and wounded members of the armed forces is a primary obligation.
*   **Red Cross Home:** A specialized hospital in **Bangalore** is maintained for permanently disabled ex-servicemen. It is a pioneer institution of its kind in Asia.
*   It provides amenities like newspapers, periodicals, and musical instruments to military hospitals.

**3. Maternal and Child Health (MCH) Services**
*   The society has done pioneering work in establishing **Maternity and Child Welfare Centers**.
*   Many centers are directly administered or affiliated with the Red Cross.
*   It has a **Bureau of Maternity and Child Welfare** that provides technical advice and financial aid to establish model centers.

**4. Blood Banking and First Aid**
*   **Blood Banks:** The society runs blood banks in various states and actively promotes voluntary blood donation.
*   **First Aid Training:** Through the **St. John Ambulance Association** (which is part of the Red Cross), it trains lakhs of people in first aid, home nursing, and allied subjects.

**5. Family Planning**
*   Several state branches run [[Family Planning]] clinics under the auspices of the Indian Red Cross.

**6. Junior Red Cross**
*   This is the youth wing of the society involving boys and girls.
*   **Activities:** Village upliftment, first aid, anti-epidemic work, and building an international fraternity of youth to promote friendliness.

#### Comparison: International vs. Indian Red Cross

| Feature | International Red Cross (ICRC) | Indian Red Cross Society (IRCS) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Founded** | 1863 (First Geneva Convention 1864) | 1920 |
| **Founder** | [[Henry Dunant]] | Established by Indian Legislature Act |
| **Headquarters** | Geneva, Switzerland | New Delhi, India |
| **Scope** | Global humanitarian aid in war/peace | National humanitarian aid & health promotion |

#### Clinical and Public Health Relevance
*   **Disaster Response:** Medical officers often coordinate with IRCS during mass casualty incidents for supplies and volunteer support.
*   **Blood Safety:** IRCS constitutes a major source of safe blood in India; physicians rely on these banks for transfusion needs.
*   **Community Training:** It is a key resource for training paramedical staff and laypeople in First Aid and Home Nursing.
