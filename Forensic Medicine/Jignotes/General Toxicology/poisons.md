# [[General Toxicology]] > poisons

### **Introduction to Toxicology and Poison**

**Definition:**
*   **[[Toxicology]]**: The science dealing with properties, actions, toxicity, fatal dose, detection, estimation, and treatment of poisons.
*   **[[Poison]]**: A substance (solid, liquid, or gas) which, if introduced into the living body or brought into contact with any part thereof, will produce ill-health or death by its constitutional or local effects or both.
*   **Forensic Toxicology**: Deals with the medical and legal aspects of the harmful effects of chemicals on human beings.

> **Concept Note:** The definition of poison is often vague because a substance harmless in small quantities (e.g., salt) can be a poison in massive doses, and beneficial drugs can be toxic in overdose. Paracelsus stated: *"Sola dosis facit venenum"* (**The dose makes the poison**).

### **Classification of Poisons**

Poisons are primarily classified based on their **mode of action** or **site of action**. This is the standard classification used for academic and medicolegal purposes.

#### **1. Corrosives**
These cause local tissue destruction (chemical burns) at the site of contact (skin, mucosa).
*   **Strong Acids:**
    *   **Mineral/Inorganic:** [[Sulfuric acid]] (H₂SO₄), [[Nitric acid]] (HNO₃), Hydrochloric acid (HCl).
    *   **Organic:** [[Carbolic acid]] (Phenol), [[Oxalic acid]], Acetic acid, Salicylic acid.
*   **Strong Alkalis:** Caustic soda (NaOH), Caustic potash (KOH), Ammonia.
*   **Metallic Salts:** Zinc chloride, Ferric chloride, Potassium cyanide.

#### **2. Irritants**
These cause inflammation, congestion, and pain at the site of contact or absorption.
*   **Agricultural:** Organophosphates, Carbamates, Chlorinated hydrocarbons.
*   **Inorganic:**
    *   **Non-metallic:** [[Phosphorus]], Iodine, Chlorine, Bromine.
    *   **Metallic:** [[Arsenic]], Antimony, Copper, Lead, Mercury, Thallium.
    *   **Mechanical:** Powdered glass, diamond dust, chopped hair.
*   **Organic:**
    *   **Vegetable (Plant):** [[Castor]] (Ricinus), [[Croton]] (Jamalgota), [[Abrus precatorius]] (Rati), Calotropis, Semecarpus (Marking nut).
    *   **Animal:** [[Snake venom]], Scorpion venom, Cantharides, Bees/Wasps.

#### **3. Systemic (Neurotic) Poisons**
These act chiefly on the nervous system.
*   **Cerebral (Acting on Brain):**
    *   **Somniferous:** Opioids (Opium, Morphine).
    *   **Inebriants:** Alcohols ([[Ethanol]], Methanol), Anesthetics (Ether, Chloroform), Sedatives/Hypnotics (Barbiturates, Benzodiazepines).
    *   **Deliriants:** [[Datura]], Belladonna, [[Cannabis]], Cocaine.
    *   **Stimulants:** Amphetamines, Caffeine.
*   **Spinal (Acting on Spinal Cord):**
    *   **Excitatory:** [[Strychnine]] (Nux vomica).
    *   **Depressant:** Gelsemium.
*   **Peripheral (Acting on Nerve Endings):**
    *   [[Curare]], Conium (Hemlock).

#### **4. Cardiovascular (Cardiac) Poisons**
These act primarily on the heart.
*   [[Aconite]] (Monkshood).
*   Nicotine (Tobacco).
*   Oleander (Nerium odorum, Cerbera thevetia).
*   Digitalis (Foxglove).
*   Quinine.

#### **5. Asphyxiants**
These cause tissue hypoxia or respiratory failure.
*   **Simple/Inert:** Nitrogen, Carbon dioxide (CO₂).
*   **Chemical/Systemic:** [[Carbon Monoxide]] (CO), Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN), Hydrogen Sulphide (H₂S).

#### **6. Miscellaneous**
*   Food poisoning, [[Botulism]].

### **Important Toxicological Concepts**

#### **Types of Poisoning Exposure**
1.  **Acute:** Excessive dose, rapid onset, severe symptoms.
2.  **Chronic:** Small doses over a long period, insidious onset, gradual deterioration.
3.  **Subacute:** Mixture of acute and chronic features.

#### **Factors Modifying Action of Poison**
1.  **Dose:** Generally, toxicity increases with dose (Concept of *Fatal Dose* vs *Therapeutic Dose*).
2.  **Form:** Gases/vapors act fastest > Liquids > Solids (Fine powder acts faster than coarse lumps).
3.  **Route of Administration:** Inhaled (Fastest) > IV > IM > Oral > Skin (Slowest).
4.  **Tolerance:** The ability to endure large doses of a poison (acquired or natural) which would be fatal to a non-tolerant person. Commonly seen with Opium, Alcohol, Tobacco.
5.  **Idiosyncrasy:** An abnormal/exaggerated response to a small (even therapeutic) dose of a drug due to inherent sensitivity (e.g., Penicillin, Aspirin).
6.  **Synergism:** When two poisons increase the toxicity of each other (e.g., Alcohol + Barbiturates).

### **Autopsy Findings: Clues for Identification**

During a [[Medicolegal Autopsy]], specific signs can point towards the type of poison used.

#### **1. Odor/Smell (Detected upon opening body/skull)**
*Open the cranial cavity first to detect volatile odors before they dissipate.*

| **Odor** | **Suspected Poison** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bitter Almonds** | [[Cyanide]] |
| **Garlic** | [[Phosphorus]], [[Arsenic]], [[Organophosphates]] (due to vehicle), Aluminum Phosphide |
| **Rotten Eggs** | Hydrogen Sulphide (H₂S) |
| **Fruity/Sweet** | Ethanol, Chloroform, Diabetic Ketoacidosis |
| **Burnt Rope** | [[Cannabis]] |
| **Acrid/Pungent** | Chloral hydrate, Paraldehyde |
| **Shoe Polish** | Nitrobenzene |
| **Kerosene** | Organophosphates (solvent smell) |

#### **2. Color of Postmortem Staining (Hypostasis)**

| **Color** | **Suspected Poison/Cause** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cherry Red / Pink** | [[Carbon Monoxide]] (Carboxyhemoglobin) |
| **Brick Red / Bright Red** | [[Cyanide]] (Cyanmethemoglobin), Cold exposure |
| **Chocolate Brown** | Nitrites, Aniline, Chlorates (Methemoglobinemia) |
| **Dark Brown / Yellow** | [[Phosphorus]], Copper |
| **Blue / Green** | Hydrogen Sulphide (H₂S) |
| **Black** | Opium (rarely) |

#### **3. Specific Internal Findings**
*   **Subendocardial Hemorrhages:** Commonly seen in the left ventricle in **Arsenic** poisoning (also in Mercury, Phosphorus).
*   **Luminous GI Content:** White Phosphorus.
*   **Leathery Stomach Mucosa:** Carbolic acid (Phenol) causes hardening/tanning.
*   **Corrosion/Perforation:** Strong mineral acids (H₂SO₄).

### **Medicolegal Aspects**

#### **Ideal Suicidal vs. Ideal Homicidal Poison**

| **Feature** | **Ideal Suicidal Poison** | **Ideal Homicidal Poison** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Availability** | Easily available | Easily available |
| **Cost** | Cheap | Cheap |
| **Taste/Odor** | Pleasant/Tasteless | Odorless, Tasteless |
| **Solubility** | Soluble in water/miscible with food | Highly soluble in food/drink without color change |
| **Toxicity** | Highly toxic (small dose fatal) | Highly toxic |
| **Symptoms** | Painless death | Mimics natural disease (e.g., Cholera, Heart attack) |
| **Examples** | [[Organophosphates]], Barbiturates, Opiates | [[Thallium]], [[Arsenic]], Aconite, Fluoride |

#### **Duties of a Doctor in Suspected Poisoning**
1.  **Medical Duty:** Stabilize the patient (ABCD: Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Depression of CNS). Save life first!.
2.  **Legal Duty:**
    *   Assist police in determining manner of death.
    *   Preserve gastric lavage, vomitus, and excreta for analysis.
    *   **Inform Police:** Mandatory under **Sec 39 CrPC** for homicidal poisoning.
    *   **Dying Declaration:** Arrange for it if the patient is critical.
    *   **Documentation:** Maintain detailed records (MLC register).
    *   **Do NOT issue Death Certificate** if poisoning is suspected; inform police for inquest.

#### **Preservation of Viscera (Chemical Analysis)**
In fatal cases, the following must be preserved in saturated saline (common salt) or rectified spirit (except in alcohol poisoning):
1.  **Stomach & Contents** (Entire).
2.  **Small Intestine** (Proximal 30 cm) & Contents.
3.  **Liver** (500g or half).
4.  **Kidneys** (Half of each).
5.  **Blood** (Peripheral vein, min 10ml, usually with Sodium Fluoride).
6.  **Urine** (30-50 ml).
*Additional specific organs (e.g., Brain for volatile poisons, Bone for chronic arsenic/lead).*

### **Mnemonics for Revision**

*   **Classification of Neurotic Poisons:** **CSP**
    *   **C**erebral (Opium, Alcohol)
    *   **S**pinal (Strychnine)
    *   **P**eripheral (Curare)
*   **Smell of Cyanide:** **"Bitter Almonds contain Cyanide"**
*   **Colors of PM Staining:**
    *   **C**arbon Monoxide = **C**herry Red.
    *   **C**yanide = **C**rimson/Brick Red.
    *   **N**itrites = **N**utella color (Chocolate/Brown).
