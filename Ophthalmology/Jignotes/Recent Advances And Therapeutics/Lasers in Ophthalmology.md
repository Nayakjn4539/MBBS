# [[Recent Advances And Therapeutics]] > Lasers in Ophthalmology

### **General Principles of Lasers**

The word **LASER** stands for **L**ight **A**mplification by **S**timulated **E**mission of **R**adiation. Lasers produce a beam of light that has three specific properties:
1.  **Monochromatic:** Single wavelength (single color).
2.  **Coherent:** Waves are in phase with each other in space and time.
3.  **Collimated:** Rays are parallel with minimal divergence.

The production involves a "gain medium" (crystal, gas, or liquid) stimulated to a state of **Population Inversion**, where atoms decay to a lower energy level, releasing photons.

### **Mechanisms of Laser-Tissue Interaction**

The effect of a laser on the eye depends on the wavelength, power density, and duration of exposure.

| Mechanism | Principle | Laser Types | Clinical Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Photocoagulation** | **Thermal Effect:** Light absorbed by pigments ([[Melanin]], [[Hemoglobin]], [[Xanthophyll]]) raises tissue temperature (10–20°C rise), causing protein denaturation and coagulation. | [[Argon Laser]], [[Diode Laser]], [[Krypton Laser]], Frequency-doubled [[Nd:YAG Laser]],. | • Scar formation (Retina)<br>• Vessel occlusion<br>• Tissue atrophy |
| **Photodisruption** | **Mechanical/Ionizing Effect:** High power density creates a "Plasma" state $\rightarrow$ acoustic shock waves $\rightarrow$ mechanical tissue breakdown,. | [[Nd:YAG Laser]], [[Femtosecond Laser]],. | • Cutting tissues (Capsule, Iris)<br>• Creating planes (Cornea) |
| **Photoablation** | **Chemical Effect:** High energy UV photons break molecular bonds directly without thermal damage,. | [[Excimer Laser]]. | • Sculpting corneal tissue (Refractive surgery) |
| **Photochemical** | **Photoradiation:** Light activates a photosensitizer drug to produce cytotoxic free radicals. | Diode (689 nm) + [[Verteporfin]]. | • Treating [[Choroidal Neovascularization]] (CNVM) via [[Photodynamic Therapy]] (PDT) |

**Clinical Implication:**
*   **Melanin** absorbs broad spectrum (visible to infrared).
*   **Hemoglobin** absorbs blue, green, and yellow well.
*   **Xanthophyll** (macular pigment) absorbs blue light; therefore, blue lasers are avoided near the fovea to prevent damage.

### **Specific Lasers in Ophthalmology**

#### **1. Argon Laser**
*   **Medium:** Argon gas.
*   **Wavelength:** Blue (488 nm) and Green (514 nm),.
*   **Mechanism:** [[Photocoagulation]] (Thermal).
*   **Clinical Applications:**
    *   **Retina:** [[Pan Retinal Photocoagulation]] (PRP) for [[Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy]], [[Eales Disease]], and [[Central Retinal Vein Occlusion]],.
    *   **Macula:** Focal/Grid laser for [[Diabetic Macular Edema]].
    *   **Glaucoma:** [[Argon Laser Trabeculoplasty]] (ALT) for [[Primary Open Angle Glaucoma]],.
    *   **Iris:** [[Laser Iridotomy]] (though YAG is preferred), [[Laser Iridoplasty]].
    *   **Retinal Breaks:** Barrage laser to seal holes.

#### **2. Nd:YAG Laser**
*   **Full Name:** Neodymium-Doped Yttrium Aluminum Garnet.
*   **Wavelength:** 1064 nm (Infrared/Invisible),.
*   **Mechanism:** [[Photodisruption]].
*   **Clinical Applications:**
    *   **Lens:** [[Posterior Capsulotomy]] for [[Posterior Capsular Opacification]] (After-cataract),.
    *   **Iris:** [[Peripheral Iridotomy]] (PI) for [[Angle Closure Glaucoma]],.
    *   **Vitreous:** Vitreolysis (cutting vitreous bands).
    *   **Pupil:** Membranectomy for pupillary membranes.

> **Note:** **Frequency-doubled Nd:YAG** emits at **532 nm (Green)**. It acts mainly by **photocoagulation** and is often used as a substitute for Argon Green laser,.

#### **3. Diode Laser**
*   **Medium:** Semiconductor Diode crystal.
*   **Wavelength:** 810 nm (Infrared),.
*   **Mechanism:** [[Photocoagulation]].
*   **Clinical Applications:**
    *   **Retina:** Preferred for [[Retinopathy of Prematurity]] (ROP) due to better penetration and less absorption by tunica vasculosa lentis.
    *   **Glaucoma:** [[Cyclophotocoagulation]] (TSCPC) for [[Absolute Glaucoma]] or [[Neovascular Glaucoma]],.
    *   **Tumors:** [[Transpupillary Thermotherapy]] (TTT) for [[Choroidal Melanoma]].
    *   **Lacrimal:** Endonasal Laser [[Dacryocystorhinostomy]] (DCR).

#### **4. Excimer Laser**
*   **Name:** Derived from "Excited Dimer".
*   **Medium:** Argon and Fluoride gas mixture,.
*   **Wavelength:** 193 nm (Ultraviolet),.
*   **Mechanism:** [[Photoablation]] (Tissue modeling without heat).
*   **Clinical Applications:**
    *   **Refractive Surgery:** [[Photorefractive Keratectomy]] (PRK) and [[LASIK]] (Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis),.
    *   **Therapeutic:** [[Phototherapeutic Keratectomy]] (PTK) for superficial corneal pathologies like [[Band Shaped Keratopathy]] or [[Granular Dystrophy]].

#### **5. Femtosecond Laser**
*   **Medium:** Nd:Glass,.
*   **Wavelength:** 1053 nm (Infrared),.
*   **Pulse Duration:** $10^{-15}$ seconds (one-quadrillionth of a second).
*   **Mechanism:** [[Photodisruption]] (Creates plasma and micro-cavitation bubbles with extreme precision and minimal collateral damage).
*   **Clinical Applications:**
    *   **Refractive:** [[Femto-LASIK]] (Flap creation), [[SMILE]] (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction),.
    *   **Cataract:** [[Femtosecond Laser Assisted Cataract Surgery]] (FLACS) - used for corneal incisions, [[Capsulotomy]], and nuclear fragmentation,.
    *   **Cornea:** Creating tunnels for [[Intracorneal Ring Segments]] (ICRS) and cuts for [[Keratoplasty]],.

### **Laser Safety Classifications**

Most ophthalmic lasers fall into the high-risk category.

*   **Class 1:** Eye safe (e.g., laser pointers).
*   **Class 2:** Visible lasers; blink reflex protects the eye.
*   **Class 3:** Moderate risk; direct viewing is hazardous.
*   **Class 4:** High risk; causes serious skin and eye injury. **Almost all therapeutic ophthalmic lasers (Argon, YAG, Excimer) are Class 4.**

**Safety Measures:**
*   Surgeons must wear protective goggles filtering the specific wavelength in use.
*   Slit lamps have inbuilt filters.
