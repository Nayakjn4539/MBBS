# [[General Orthopaedics]] > Orthoses in Orthopedics

### Definition and Concept

An **[[Orthosis]]** (Plural: Orthoses) is an external device applied to a part of the body to stabilize, control, limit, or immobilize the body part, prevent deformity, correct deformity, or assist function. 

*   **Difference from [[Prosthesis]]:**
    *   **[[Prosthesis]]:** Replaces a missing body part (e.g., artificial leg).
    *   **[[Orthosis]]:** Supports an existing body part (e.g., caliper, brace, splint).

An **Orthotist** is a person qualified to measure, design, and fit all types of orthoses.

### Functions of Orthoses

1.  **Relief of Pain:** By limiting motion or offloading stress (e.g., [[Lumbar corset]] for backache).
2.  **Support:** Supporting weakened or paralyzed muscles (e.g., [[KAFO]] in [[Poliomyelitis]]).
3.  **Stabilization:** Stabilizing unstable joints (e.g., [[Knee immobilizer]]).
4.  **Immobilization:** Keeping joints in a functional position to allow healing (e.g., Casts/splints for fractures).
5.  **Prevention of Deformity:** Preventing contractures (e.g., Splinting in nerve injuries).
6.  **Correction of Deformity:** Applying corrective forces (e.g., [[CTEV]] splints, [[Milwaukee brace]] for scoliosis).
7.  **Functional Assistance:** Assisting movement through energy-storing devices like springs (e.g., [[Dynamic splints]]).

### Classification

*   **Static Orthoses:** Rigid devices that support the limb and prevent movement (e.g., [[Cock-up splint]]).
*   **Dynamic Orthoses:** Allow or assist movement, often using springs or elastics to replace paralyzed muscles (e.g., [[Knuckle bender splint]]).

### Regional Orthoses and Indications

#### 1. Spinal Orthoses

**A. Cervical Spine**
*   **[[Four-post collar]]:** For neck immobilization.
*   **[[SOMI brace]] (Sterno-Occipito-Mandibular Immobilizer):** For cervical spine injuries; provides rigid immobilization.
*   **[[Philadelphia collar]]:** Semirigid support.

**B. Thoracolumbar Spine**
*   **[[Taylor's brace]]:** For dorso-lumbar immobilization (e.g., TB spine, fractures).
*   **[[ASH brace]] (Anterior Spinal Hyperextension brace):** For stable compression fractures; prevents flexion.
*   **[[Milwaukee brace]] (CTLSO):** For high thoracic [[Scoliosis]] or curves with apex above T8. Consists of a pelvic mold, uprights, and a neck ring.
*   **[[Boston brace]]:** Underarm brace for lower [[Scoliosis]] curves.
*   **[[Lumbar corset]] / [[Lumbosacral belt]]:** For [[Low backache]], IVDP, Spondylolisthesis.

#### 2. Lower Limb Orthoses

**A. Footwear Modifications**
*   **[[Thomas heel]]:** Heel extended anteriorly on the medial side. **Indication:** [[Flat foot]] (supports medial arch).
*   **[[Metatarsal bar]]:** Bar placed behind metatarsal heads. **Indication:** [[Metatarsalgia]] (offloads pressure from heads).
*   **[[Arch support]] (Valgus insole):** **Indication:** Flat foot.
*   **[[Heel pad]]:** **Indication:** [[Calcaneal spur]], [[Plantar fasciitis]].
*   **Medial/Lateral Raise:**
    *   Medial raise for [[Genu valgum]].
    *   Lateral raise for [[Genu varum]].

**B. Limb Orthoses**
*   **[[AFO]] (Ankle Foot Orthosis):** For [[Foot drop]] (supports foot in dorsiflexion), medial/lateral instability.
*   **[[KAFO]] (Knee Ankle Foot Orthosis):** Commonly known as "Calipers". Used in [[Poliomyelitis]], quadriceps paralysis, unstable knee.
*   **[[HKAFO]]:** For extensive paralysis (e.g., Paraplegia).
*   **[[Dennis Brown splint]]:** For correction of [[CTEV]] (Clubfoot).
*   **[[Bohler-Braun splint]]:** Used for fracture femur traction/elevation.

#### 3. Upper Limb Orthoses

| Nerve Injury/Condition | Splint/Orthosis | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Radial nerve palsy]]** | **[[Cock-up splint]]** (Dynamic or Static) | Keeps wrist and MCP joints extended. |
| **[[Ulnar nerve palsy]]** | **[[Knuckle bender splint]]** | Prevents hyperextension at MCP joints (corrects clawing). |
| **[[Median nerve injury]]** | **[[Opponens splint]]** | Maintains thumb in abduction and opposition. |
| **[[Brachial plexus injury]]** | **[[Aeroplane splint]]** | Keeps shoulder abducted (prevents adduction contracture). |
| **[[Volkmann's Ischemia]]** | **[[Volkmann's splint]]** | Prevents contracture. |
| **[[Mallet finger]]** | **[[Mallet finger splint]]** | Keeps DIP joint in hyperextension. |

### Specific Important Splints

#### [[Thomas Splint]]
Originally designed by **Hugh Owen Thomas** for [[Tuberculosis of Knee]]. Now widely used for **[[Fracture Shaft of Femur]]**.

*   **Structure:**
    *   **Ring:** Well-padded, oval/circular. Placed at an angle of **120Â°** to the inner bar.
    *   **Side Bars:** Inner bar is shorter; Outer bar is longer (to accommodate the greater trochanter).
    *   **Distal End:** Bars converge to form a 'W' shape (for attachment of traction).
*   **Uses:**
    *   Transport splint for femur fractures.
    *   Fixed traction (using the 'W' end).
    *   Sliding traction (when suspended).

**Clinical Implication:** Ensure proper ring size to prevent pressure sores in the perineum or ischial tuberosity.

#### [[Bohler-Braun Splint]]
A metallic frame with pulleys.
*   **Uses:** Comminuted fracture of the femur, fracture tibia.
*   **Pulleys:**
    *   Pulley A: Proximal (for femur traction).
    *   Pulley B: Distal (for tibia traction).
    *   Pulley C: For foot drop prevention.
*   **Mechanism:** Uses gravity as counter-traction (no need to elevate the foot end of the bed).

### Complications of Orthoses

1.  **Pressure Sores:** Most common complication due to poor fit or lack of padding (especially over bony prominences).
2.  **Nerve Compression:** e.g., Peroneal nerve compression by AFO or casts.
3.  **Muscle Wasting:** Prolonged use of rigid orthoses leads to disuse atrophy.
4.  **Stiffness:** Joints immobilized for long periods become stiff.
5.  **Edema:** Tight strapping can obstruct venous return.

### Mnemonics

*   **Splints for Nerve Injuries:**
    *   **R**adial -> **R**aise the wrist -> **Cock-up** splint.
    *   **U**lnar -> **U**nbend the knuckles -> **Knuckle bender** splint.
    *   **M**edian -> **M**ove the thumb -> **Opponens** splint.
