# [[Diseases Of Retina]] > Retinal Disorders table

### **Diseases of the Retina: Comprehensive Overview**

The retina is the neurosensory layer of the eye responsible for converting light into neural signals. Its pathologies range from vascular disorders to hereditary dystrophies and detachments. Below is a structured classification and management protocol for major retinal disorders.

---

### **1. Retinal Vascular Disorders**

These disorders primarily involve the blood vessels of the retina, leading to ischemia, leakage, or neovascularization.

| Disease | Defect/Clinical Features | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Diabetic Retinopathy]] (DR)** | **Non-Proliferative (NPDR):** [[Microaneurysms]], Dot/Blot hemorrhages, Hard exudates, [[Cotton Wool Spots]].<br>**Proliferative (PDR):** [[Neovascularization]] (NVD/NVE), Vitreous hemorrhage, Tractional RD.<br>**Maculopathy:** [[Clinically Significant Macular Edema]] (CSME). | Chronic hyperglycemia leads to loss of **pericytes** (earliest change) and endothelial damage $\rightarrow$ capillary occlusion (ischemia) and leakage.<br>Ischemia triggers release of **[[VEGF]]** causing neovascularization. | **Systemic:** Glycemic & BP control.<br>**Macular Edema:** Intravitreal [[Anti-VEGF]] (Ranibizumab/Bevacizumab) or Steroids.<br>**PDR:** [[Panretinal Photocoagulation]] (PRP) to convert hypoxic retina to anoxic.<br>**Surgery:** Pars Plana [[Vitrectomy]] for non-clearing hemorrhage or TRD. |
| **[[Hypertensive Retinopathy]]** | **Grades (Keith-Wagner):**<br>I: Arteriolar attenuation.<br>II: AV nicking (Salus/Bonnet/Gunn signs).<br>III: Flame hemorrhages, Cotton wool spots, Copper wiring.<br>IV: **[[Papilledema]]**, Silver wiring, Macular Star. | Systemic hypertension causes vasoconstriction (autoregulation) followed by arteriosclerosis (thickening of vessel wall) and leakage due to breakdown of blood-retina barrier. | **Systemic:** Control of blood pressure.<br>**Ocular:** Observation; lesions usually regress with BP control. Treat complications like BRVO/CRVO if they arise. |
| **[[Central Retinal Artery Occlusion]] (CRAO)** | Sudden, painless, profound vision loss.<br>**Fundus:** Pale/white retina with **[[Cherry Red Spot]]** at macula.<br>Arteries are thread-like; Cattle-traking of blood column. | Embolism (Cholesterol/Hollenhorst plaque, Calcific) or Thrombosis (Atherosclerosis).<br>Retinal distinct layers cloud due to edema; fovea remains red as it is supplied by choroid. | **Ocular Emergency:**<br>1. Ocular massage (to dislodge embolus).<br>2. Lower IOP (Paracentesis, Acetazolamide, Mannitol).<br>3. Vasodilators (Isosorbide dinitrate, Carbogen inhalation).<br>Prognosis is poor. |
| **[[Central Retinal Vein Occlusion]] (CRVO)** | Sudden painless vision loss.<br>**Fundus:** "Tomato splash" appearance, diffuse hemorrhages, dilated tortuous veins, disc edema, Cotton wool spots. | Virchow's Triad: Vessel wall damage, Stasis, Hypercoagulability.<br>Two types:<br>1. **Ischemic:** Poor vision, APD positive, risk of **[[Neovascular Glaucoma]]** (100-day glaucoma).<br>2. **Non-Ischemic:** Better prognosis. | **Macular Edema:** Intravitreal Anti-VEGF or Steroids (Ozurdex implant).<br>**Ischemia/Neovascularization:** PRP to prevent neovascular glaucoma.<br>**Systemic:** Treat HTN, DM, hyperlipidemia. |
| **[[Retinopathy of Prematurity]] (ROP)** | Proliferation of vascular tissue in immature retina.<br>**Stages:**<br>1. Demarcation Line<br>2. Ridge<br>3. Extraretinal fibrovascular proliferation<br>4. Subtotal RD<br>5. Total RD. | Affects preterm infants (<32 weeks, <1500g).<br>Exposure to high **Oxygen** causes vasoconstriction $\rightarrow$ return to room air causes relative hypoxia $\rightarrow$ VEGF surge $\rightarrow$ abnormal vessel growth. | **Screening:** 4 weeks after birth or 31 weeks post-conceptional age.<br>**Treatment:** Laser photocoagulation (Gold standard) for "Threshold Disease" or "Type 1 Pre-threshold".<br>Anti-VEGF agents.<br>Surgery for RD. |
| **[[Eales' Disease]]** | Recurrent **[[Vitreous Hemorrhage]]** in young adult males.<br>Sudden painless vision loss/floaters. | Idiopathic peripheral periphlebitis (inflammation of vein walls). Strongly associated with hypersensitivity to **[[Tuberculosis]]** (Mycobacterium tuberculosis protein). | **Medical:** Systemic Steroids, Antitubercular therapy (if indicated).<br>**Laser:** Photocoagulation of ischemic areas.<br>**Surgery:** Vitrectomy for non-resolving hemorrhage. |
| **[[Coats' Disease]]** | Unilateral leukocoria, exudative RD in young boys. | Congenital retinal telangiectasia leading to massive subretinal exudation (cholesterol crystals). | Laser photocoagulation or Cryotherapy to ablating leaking vessels.<br>Surgery for RD. |

> **Clinical Implication:** **CRVO** is the most common retinal vascular cause of blindness after Diabetic Retinopathy. Always check IOP in the fellow eye, as **POAG** is a risk factor for CRVO.

### **2. Macular Disorders**

Disorders affecting the central vision, color vision, and contrast sensitivity.

| Disease | Defect/Clinical Features | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Central Serous Chorioretinopathy]] (CSCR)** | Sudden onset central scotoma, metamorphopsia, micropsia.<br>**Fundus:** Serous detachment of neurosensory retina at macula. | Dysfunction of **[[Retinal Pigment Epithelium]]** (RPE) pump and hyperpermeable choroid.<br>Risk Factors: Type A personality, **Steroid** use, Stress, H. pylori.<br>**FFA:** Ink blot or Smoke-stack pattern. | **Observation:** Most resolve spontaneously in 3-4 months.<br>**Stop Steroids.**<br>**Laser:** Focal laser or Photodynamic Therapy (PDT) with Verteporfin for chronic/recurrent cases. |
| **[[Age-Related Macular Degeneration]] (ARMD)** | **Dry (Non-exudative):** Gradual vision loss. Drusen (yellow deposits), Geographic atrophy.<br>**Wet (Exudative):** Rapid vision loss, metamorphopsia. Choroidal Neovascular Membrane (CNVM). | Degenerative changes in RPE, Bruch's membrane, and choriocapillaris.<br>**Dry:** Atrophy of RPE.<br>**Wet:** VEGF mediated neovascularization leaking fluid/blood. | **Dry:** Antioxidants (AREDS formula - Vit C, E, Zinc, Lutein). Low vision aids.<br>**Wet:** Intravitreal **[[Anti-VEGF]]** (Ranibizumab, Bevacizumab, Aflibercept).<br>PDT/Laser (rarely used now). |
| **[[Cystoid Macular Edema]] (CME)** | Loss of central vision.<br>**FFA:** Flower-petal appearance.<br>**OCT:** Cystic spaces in retina. | Accumulation of fluid in **Outer Plexiform Layer**.<br>Causes (**DEPRIV**): **D**iabetes, **E**pinephrine, **P**ars planitis, **R**P, **I**rvine-Gass (post-cataract sx), **V**ein occlusion. | Topical NSAIDs (Nepafenac/Ketorolac).<br>Topical/Intravitreal Steroids.<br>Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors (Acetazolamide).<br>Anti-VEGF. |
| **[[Macular Hole]]** | Central scotoma, Metamorphopsia.<br>**Watzke-Allen sign positive**. | Tangential vitreous traction on the fovea (Vitreomacular traction). | **Surgery:** Pars Plana Vitrectomy (PPV) + ILM peeling + Gas tamponade (face-down positioning). |

### **3. Hereditary Dystrophies**

Genetic disorders causing progressive vision loss.

| Disease | Defect/Clinical Features | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Retinitis Pigmentosa]] (RP)** | **Nyctalopia** (Night blindness), Tunnel vision (Ring scotoma).<br>**Triad:** Bony spicules, Arteriolar attenuation, Waxy pale disc. | Hereditary (AD, AR, X-linked) dystrophy primarily affecting **Rods** then Cones.<br>**ERG:** Subnormal or extinguished (a-wave absent). | No cure.<br>Low vision aids.<br>Vitamin A palmitate (slows progression).<br>Retinal prostheses (Argus II). |
| **[[Stargardt's Disease]]** | Central vision loss in young age (Juvenile macular dystrophy).<br>**Fundus:** "Beaten bronze" macula, pisciform flecks.<br>**FFA:** "Dark Choroid" sign. | Mutation in **ABCA4 gene** (Autosomal Recessive).<br>Accumulation of **lipofuscin** in RPE. | Low vision aids.<br>Avoid Vitamin A supplements (can worsen lipofuscin accumulation). |
| **[[Best's Disease]]** (Vitelliform Dystrophy) | "Egg-yolk" (Vitelliform) lesion at macula. Usually good vision initially.<br>Later "Scrambled egg" appearance. | Autosomal Dominant.<br>Lipofuscin accumulation in RPE.<br>**EOG:** Abnormal (Arden's ratio < 1.5). ERG is normal. | Supportive.<br>Treat CNVM if it develops. |

> **Mnemonic for Macular Dystrophies:**
> *   **Best's** is **Best** (Better prognosis, EOG abnormal).
> *   **Stargardt's** is **Sad** (Worse prognosis, Dark choroid).

### **4. Retinal Detachment (RD)**

Separation of the neurosensory retina from the Retinal Pigment Epithelium (RPE).

| Type | Etiology/Defect | Clinical Signs | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Rhegmatogenous RD]]** | **Retinal Break** (Tear/Hole) allows liquefied vitreous to enter subretinal space. Associated with Myopia, Trauma, Lattice degeneration. | Photopsia (flashes), Floaters, "Curtain falling".<br>Mobile, convex retina.<br>**Tobacco dust** (Shafer's sign) in vitreous. | **Surgery:** Scleral Buckling, Pneumatic Retinopexy, or Pars Plana Vitrectomy (PPV) with gas/silicone oil tamponade. Seal breaks with Cryo/Laser. |
| **[[Tractional RD]]** | Fibrous bands pull retina away from RPE.<br>Causes: PDR, ROP, Trauma. | Concave surface, immobile retina. No breaks usually. | **Surgery:** Vitrectomy to release traction (delamination/segmentation). |
| **[[Exudative RD]]** (Serous) | Fluid accumulation due to breakdown of blood-retina barrier (Inflammation/Tumor).<br>Causes: Choroidal Melanoma, Coats', Harada's, Toxemia of pregnancy. | Shifting fluid (fluid moves with head position).<br>Smooth, convex surface. No breaks. | **Treat underlying cause** (e.g., Corticosteroids for Harada's, Enucleation/Radio for tumors). **No surgery** for the detachment itself. |

### **5. Retinal Tumors**

| Disease | Defect/Clinical Features | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Retinoblastoma]]** | Most common intraocular malignancy in children.<br>**Leukocoria** (White reflex), Squint.<br>**Endophytic** (grows into vitreous) or **Exophytic** (subretinal). | Mutation of **RB1 gene** (13q14).<br>HPE: **Flexner-Wintersteiner Rosettes** (pathognomonic).<br>Calcification seen on CT/USG. | **Small:** Focal therapy (Laser/Cryo).<br>**Medium/Large:** Chemoreduction (Carboplatin, Etoposide, Vincristine) + Focal therapy.<br>**Advanced:** **Enucleation**.<br>**Extraocular:** Chemotherapy + Radiotherapy. |
