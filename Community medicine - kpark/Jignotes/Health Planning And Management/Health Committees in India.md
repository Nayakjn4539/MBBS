### **Health Committees in India**

Health planning in India has been largely guided by a series of expert committees appointed by the Government of India. These committees reviewed the existing health situation and recommended measures for further action.

### **1. Bhore Committee (1946)**
*   **Also known as:** [[Health Survey and Development Committee]]
*   **Chairman:** Sir Joseph Bhore
*   **Key Concept:** "Comprehensive Health Care" (Integration of preventive and curative services).
*   **Major Recommendations:**
    *   **Short Term Measure:** One [[Primary Health Centre]] (PHC) for 40,000 population.
    *   **Long Term Measure (3 Million Plan):**
        *   Primary Health Units (75 beds) for 10,000–20,000 population.
        *   Secondary Health Units (650 beds).
        *   District Health Units (2,500 beds).
    *   **Social Physician:** Major changes in medical education to prepare "Social Physicians" (3 months training in PSM).
    *   **Inter-sectoral Coordination:** Improvement in non-health sectors like water, sanitation, and nutrition.

### **2. Mudaliar Committee (1962)**
*   **Also known as:** [[Health Survey and Planning Committee]]
*   **Chairman:** Dr. A.L. Mudaliar
*   **Context:** Appointed to assess the performance of the Bhore Committee recommendations.
*   **Major Recommendations:**
    *   **Consolidation:** Strengthening existing PHCs before opening new ones.
    *   **Population Norm:** 1 PHC per 40,000 population (max).
    *   **All India Health Service:** Constitution of an All India Health Service on the pattern of IAS.
    *   Strengthening of District Hospitals.

### **3. Chadha Committee (1963)**
*   **Purpose:** Arrangements for the **Maintenance Phase** of the [[National Malaria Eradication Programme]] (NMEP).
*   **Major Recommendations:**
    *   **Vigilance:** Malaria vigilance operations should be the responsibility of general health services (PHCs).
    *   **Basic Health Worker (BHW):** 1 BHW per 10,000 population.
    *   **Multipurpose duties:** BHWs to look after malaria + vital statistics + family planning.

### **4. Mukherjee Committee (1965 & 1966)**
*   **1965 Committee:** Appointed to review the Family Planning performance.
    *   **Key finding:** BHWs could not handle multiple burdens (Malaria + Family planning).
    *   **Recommendation:** **Delink** Malaria activities from Family Planning. Separate staff for Family Planning.
*   **1966 Committee:**
    *   **Recommendation:** Details of [[Basic Health Service]] to be provided at the Block level.

### **5. Jungalwalla Committee (1967)**
*   **Also known as:** [[Committee on Integration of Health Services]]
*   **Focus:** Integration of health services and service conditions of doctors.
*   **Major Recommendations (The "Unified" Approach):**
    *   **Unified Cadre**
    *   **Common Seniority**
    *   Recognition of extra qualifications
    *   **Equal pay for equal work**
    *   No private practice for government doctors.

### **6. Kartar Singh Committee (1973)**
*   **Also known as:** Committee on [[Multipurpose Workers]] under Health and Family Planning.
*   **Major Recommendations:**
    *   **Multipurpose Workers (MPW):** Introduced the concept of MPWs.
    *   **Designation Changes:**
        *   ANMs $\rightarrow$ Female Health Workers.
        *   Malaria Surveillance Workers/Vaccinators $\rightarrow$ Male Health Workers.
    *   **Sub-centre Norm:** 1 Sub-centre for 3,000–3,500 population staffed by 1 Male & 1 Female Health Worker.
    *   **Supervision:** 1 Health Supervisor (Male/Female) for every 3–4 Health Workers.

### **7. Shrivastava Committee (1975)**
*   **Also known as:** Group on **Medical Education and Support Manpower**.
*   **Major Recommendations:**
    1.  Creation of **Para-professional/Semi-professional** health workers from the community (e.g., School teachers, Postmasters).
    2.  Establishment of the **ROME Scheme** ([[Reorientation of Medical Education]]).
    3.  Development of a **Referral Services Complex**.
    4.  Establishment of a Medical and Health Education Commission.
    *   *Result:* Launch of the **Rural Health Scheme** (1977) and [[Village Health Guides]] (VHG) scheme.

### **8. Krishnan Committee (1983)**
*   **Focus:** Urban Health.
*   **Recommendation:** **Urban Revamping Scheme** (Health Posts for slums).

### **9. Bajaj Committee (1986)**
*   **Also known as:** Expert Committee for **Health Manpower Planning**, Production and Management.
*   **Major Recommendations:**
    *   Formulation of National Medical & Health Education Policy.
    *   Establishment of an **Educational Commission for Health Sciences (ECHS)**.
    *   Vocationalization of education at 10+2 levels for paramedical training.

### **10. High Level Expert Group (HLEG) (2011)**
*   **Context:** Constituted by Planning Commission for the 12th Five Year Plan.
*   **Chairman:** Dr. K. Srinath Reddy.
*   **Key Concept:** [[Universal Health Coverage]] (UHC).
*   **Key Targets/Recommendations:**
    *   **Bachelor of Rural Health Care (BRHC):** 3-year course to create a cadre of Community Health Officers.
    *   **Public Health Expenditure:** Increase to **2.5% of GDP** (by 2017).
    *   **Ban on Private Practice:** For government doctors (with compensation).
    *   **Target Norms:**
        *   2 Beds per 1000 population.
        *   3 Nurses per 1 Doctor.
        *   1 Doctor per 1000 population.
