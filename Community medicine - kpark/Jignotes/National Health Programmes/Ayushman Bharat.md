# [[National Health Programmes]] > Ayushman Bharat

# [[Ayushman Bharat Programme]]

**Launch Date:** Announced February 2018.
*   **Health and Wellness Centre (HWC):** First inaugurated on **14th April 2018** at Bijapur, Chhattisgarh.
*   **PM-JAY:** Launched on **23rd September 2018**.

**Vision:** To achieve [[Universal Health Coverage]] (UHC) by moving from a sectoral/segmented approach to a comprehensive need-based health care service.

The programme has two interconnected pillars:
1.  **Health and Wellness Centres (HWCs)**
2.  **Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)**

---

### 1. [[Health and Wellness Centres]] (HWCs)

These are the foundation of India's health system, delivering **Comprehensive Primary Health Care (CPHC)**.

*   **Goal:** To transform 1.5 lakh existing Sub-centres and Primary Health Centres (PHCs) into HWCs to bring healthcare closer to people's homes,.
*   **Staffing Pattern:**
    *   Led by a **[[Community Health Officer]] (CHO)**: A BSc/GNM Nurse or Ayurveda Practitioner trained in a 6-month bridge course in primary care and public health.
    *   Supported by Multi-purpose Workers (Male & Female) and [[ASHA]]s.
*   **Scope:** Shift from selective care (RCH + communicable diseases) to **Comprehensive Primary Health Care** covering NCDs, mental health, ENT, ophthalmology, oral health, geriatric care, and palliative care.
*   **Wellness Component:** Promotion of healthy lifestyle through Yoga sessions, physical activities, and "Eat Right" campaigns.
*   **Time to Care:** Goal is to reduce travel time for care to **< 30 minutes**.

#### The 12 Expanded Service Packages at HWCs,:
| Category | Services Included |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Maternal & Child** | 1. Care in pregnancy and childbirth <br> 2. Neonatal and infant health care services <br> 3. Childhood and adolescent health care services <br> 4. Family planning, contraceptive services, and other reproductive health care |
| **Communicable** | 5. Management of communicable diseases (National Health Programmes) <br> 6. General out-patient care for acute simple illnesses and minor ailments |
| **NCDs & Chronic** | 7. Screening, prevention, control, and management of [[Non-Communicable Diseases]] (NCDs) |
| **Speciality** | 8. Basic [[Oral Health]] care <br> 9. Screening and basic management of [[Mental Health]] ailments <br> 10. Care for common Ophthalmic and ENT problems |
| **Geriatric/Trauma** | 11. Elderly and palliative health care services <br> 12. Emergency medical services including burns and trauma |

### 2. [[Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana]] (PM-JAY)

This is the **National Health Protection Scheme (NHPS)** component, currently the world's largest government-funded healthcare programme.

*   **Coverage:** Provides health insurance cover of **Rs. 5 Lakhs per family per year** for **secondary and tertiary care** hospitalization.
*   **Beneficiaries:**
    *   Targets approx. **10.74 Crore** poor and vulnerable families (approx. **50 Crore** individuals).
    *   **Rural:** Based on deprivation categories (D1, D2, D3, D4, D5, D7) of **Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011**,.
    *   **Urban:** Based on 11 defined occupational categories.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **Cashless & Paperless:** Access to services at point of care,.
    *   **No Cap:** No restriction on family size, age, or gender.
    *   **Pre-existing Diseases:** Covered from day one.
    *   **Portability:** Benefits are portable across the country in any empanelled hospital (public or private).
    *   **Expenses Covered:** Includes 3 days of pre-hospitalization and 15 days of post-hospitalization expenses (diagnostics, medicines).
    *   **Packages:** Covers 1,354+ medical and surgical packages (e.g., Bypass, Knee replacement).
*   **Relationship with RSBY:** PM-JAY subsumes the earlier [[Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana]] (RSBY).

---

### Implementation & IT Support

*   **Nodal Agency:** National Health Authority (NHA).
*   **Ayushman Mitra:** Dedicated staff in hospitals to assist patients and coordinate with the scheme.
*   **ABHA ID:** Creation of unique health IDs for centralized medical records.
*   **Call Centre:** 14555 helpline for citizens.

---

### Mnemonic for PM-JAY Features
**"5 C's of PM-JAY"**
*   **C**ashless (and Paperless)
*   **C**overage (Rs. 5 Lakhs)
*   **C**ap-less (No cap on family size/age)
*   **C**onditions (Pre-existing covered)
*   **C**ountry-wide (Portability)

---

### Clinical Relevance
*   **Referral Pathway:** The HWCs act as the first point of contact (gatekeepers). Patients requiring specialized care are referred to CHCs/District Hospitals or empanelled hospitals under PM-JAY, ensuring a **continuum of care** from primary to tertiary levels.
*   **NCD Screening:** Massive screening for Hypertension, Diabetes, and cancers (Oral, Breast, Cervix) is initiated at the HWC level, reducing the burden of advanced disease at tertiary centres.
