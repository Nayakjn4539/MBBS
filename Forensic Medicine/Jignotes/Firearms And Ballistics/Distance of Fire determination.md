# [[Firearms And Ballistics]] > Distance of Fire determination

### Distance of Fire Determination (Range of Fire)

The determination of the range of fire is a crucial aspect of [[Forensic Ballistics]]. It relies on the detection and analysis of the discharge products (flame, smoke, unburnt powder) found on the target (skin or clothing) around the entry wound.

The components emerging from the muzzle travel to different distances:
1.  **Flame:** Travels the shortest distance.
2.  **Smoke (Soot):** Travels further than flame.
3.  **Unburnt Powder particles:** Travel further than smoke.
4.  **Projectile (Bullet/Pellet):** Travels to the target.

---

### 1. Rifled Firearms (Revolvers, Pistols, Rifles)

The range is classified based on the presence of discharge residues around the [[entry wound]].

#### A. Contact Shot (Muzzle against the target)
*   **Distance:** 0 cm (Touch).
*   **Tight Contact (Hard Contact):** Muzzle pressed firmly against skin.
    *   **Wound Shape:** often **Stellate/Cruciate** (irregular/torn) due to gases expanding subcutaneously, especially over bony areas like the forehead or sternum.
    *   **Muzzle Impression:** An abrasion corresponding to the muzzle rim may be seen (Recoil Abrasion/Copy Abrasion).
    *   **Internal Findings:** The wound track and surrounding tissues appear **Cherry Red/Pink** due to the formation of Carboxyhemoglobin (CO from combustion gases).
    *   **Residues:** No external blackening/tattooing (all go inside the wound). [[Soot]] and powder are found **inside** the wound track.
*   **Loose Contact:** Muzzle touches skin lightly or is very close.
    *   **Findings:** A ring of soot (blackening) is seen around the wound. Singeing of hair may occur.

#### B. Close Range (Flame Range)
*   **Distance:** Within the reach of the flame (approx. **3 to 6 inches** / **8 to 15 cm**).
*   **Findings:**
    *   **Burning/Singeing:** Heat effects on skin and hair. Hair looks clubbed/curled.
    *   **Scorching:** Parchment-like discoloration of skin.
    *   **Smoke/Soot:** Heavy deposit may be present.

#### C. Near Range (Smoke Range)
*   **Distance:** Within the reach of smoke but outside flame range (approx. **12 to 18 inches** / **30 to 45 cm**).
*   **Findings:**
    *   **[[Blackening]] (Smudging/Fouling):** Deposition of carbon soot.
    *   **Characteristic:** Superficial deposit; **can be wiped off** with a wet cloth.

#### D. Intermediate Range (Powder Range)
*   **Distance:** Within reach of unburnt powder particles (approx. **2 to 3 feet** / **60 to 90 cm**).
*   **Findings:**
    *   **[[Tattooing]] (Stippling/Peppering):** Unburnt or semi-burnt powder grains penetrate the epidermis and get embedded in the dermis.
    *   **Characteristic:** These are antemortem injuries (punctuate abrasions). They **cannot be wiped off**.
    *   **Medicolegal Importance:** Proves the victim was alive when shot (vital reaction).

#### E. Distant Range
*   **Distance:** Beyond the range of powder deposition (usually **> 1 meter**).
*   **Findings:**
    *   **Absence:** No burning, blackening, or tattooing.
    *   **Entry Wound:** Circular/Oval defect with:
        1.  **[[Abrasion Collar]]:** Rim of abraded skin due to bullet friction and spinning.
        2.  **[[Grease Collar]] (Dirt Collar):** Black ring on the inner edge of abrasion collar due to lubricant/lead from the bullet.

### 2. Shotguns (Smooth Bore Weapons)

Range is determined primarily by the **dispersion of pellets** (lead shots) and the effects of wads.

#### A. Contact Wounds
*   **Appearance:** Single, circular wound. Size equal to the bore of the gun.
*   **Tissue Damage:** Massive internal destruction (mincing of tissues) due to expanding gases.
*   **Color:** Cherry red discoloration of underlying tissues.

#### B. Close Range (Up to 1 meter)
*   **< 15-30 cm:** Single circular hole. No satellite pellets.
*   **30-90 cm (1-3 feet):** Single hole with **irregular/scalloped margins** (Rat-hole appearance).
*   **Wad Marks:** In close range, the wad may enter the wound or leave a bruise/abrasion near the entry (e.g., Plastic wads may leave a Maltese Cross abrasion).

#### C. Distant Range (> 1 meter)
*   **Dispersion:** The shot mass begins to spread (fan out).
*   **Satellite Holes:** Individual pellets separate from the main mass and create small independent holes around the central defect.
*   **Rule of Thumb for Range Estimation:**
    *   **Metric:** Range in **meters** $\approx$ $\frac{1}{3}$ of the dispersion (spread) of pellets in **cm**.
    *   **Imperial:** Range in **yards** $\approx$ Dispersion (spread) of pellets in **inches**.
    *   *Example:* If the spread of pellets is 10 inches, the range is approximately 10 yards.

| Range | Wound Characteristic |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Contact** | Single round wound, muzzle impression, massive destruction |
| **< 1 Meter** | Single wound with scalloped margins ("Rat-hole"), wad entry |
| **1 - 3 Meters** | Central hole with satellite pellets increasing in number |
| **> 4 Meters** | No central hole; individual pellet holes only |

> **Note:** The spread is affected by the **Choke** of the gun. A full choke gun has less spread (tighter pattern) than a cylinder bore gun at the same distance.

### 4. Medico-Legal Aspects & Autopsy Findings

*   **Clothes:** Clothing acts as a filter. It can stop soot and powder. If the body is clothed, the skin may only show the bullet hole while the clothes show blackening/tattooing. Clothes must be examined and preserved.
*   **Suicide vs. Homicide:**
    *   **Suicide:** Almost always **Contact** or **Close** range (within arm's reach).
    *   **Homicide:** Can be any range, but **Distant** shots are strongly suggestive of homicide (unless a mechanism to fire from a distance is found).
*   **[[Back Spatter]]:** In contact shots (especially head), blood and tissue may be sucked back into the gun barrel due to gas expansion and vacuum creation. This links the weapon to the victim.
*   **Kennedy Phenomenon:** Surgical alteration of the wound can make it impossible to determine the range or distinguish entry from exit wounds.
*   **Chemical Tests:** IR photography or chemical tests (Sodium Rhodizonate for lead, Walkerâ€™s test for nitrites) can detect invisible residues to estimate range.

### Mnemonics

*   **Order of Reach:** **F**ire **S**mokes **P**eople **B**adly
    *   **F**lame (Shortest)
    *   **S**moke
    *   **P**owder (Tattooing)
    *   **B**ullet (Furthest)

*   **Blackening vs Tattooing:**
    *   **B**lackening = **B**athed (Can be washed/wiped off).
    *   **T**attooing = **T**rapped (Embedded in skin, cannot be wiped).
