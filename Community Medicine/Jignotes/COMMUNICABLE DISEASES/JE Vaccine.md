# Japanese Encephalitis (JE) Vaccine

### **Overview**
Japanese Encephalitis (JE) is a mosquito-borne viral disease and a leading cause of viral encephalitis in Asia. Since there is no specific antiviral treatment for JE, **vaccination** is the single most effective preventive measure. The vaccine is included in the [[Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)]] of India but is restricted to **endemic districts** only.

---

### **Types of JE Vaccines**

Historically, mouse-brain derived vaccines were used, but they have largely been replaced by safer and more potent cell-culture derived vaccines.

### **Administration in National Immunization Schedule (NIS)**

Under the UIP, the JE vaccine is administered **only in identified endemic districts** (currently >200 districts across states like UP, Assam, West Bengal, Karnataka, etc.).

#### **1. For Children (Pediatric Schedule)**
*   **Target Group:** Children aged 1 to 15 years.
*   **Schedule:** 2 Doses.
    *   **1st Dose:** At **9 completed months** (along with Measles-Rubella 1st dose),.
    *   **2nd Dose:** At **16â€“24 months** (along with DPT/OPV booster),.
*   **Site:**
    *   **Live Vaccine:** Left Upper Arm (Subcutaneous).
    *   **Killed Vaccine (JENVAC):** Anterolateral aspect of mid-thigh (Intramuscular).

> **Note:** If a child misses the schedule, they are eligible to receive the vaccine up to **15 years of age**.

#### **2. For Adults**
*   **Indication:** Adults (15â€“65 years) in **high-burden districts** are vaccinated during special campaigns.
*   **Schedule:** Single dose.

### **Contraindications**

1.  **Age < 6 months:** JE is infrequent in infancy due to maternal antibodies, and the vaccine is not recommended before this age.
2.  **Pregnancy:** Live vaccines are generally contraindicated (unless the risk of infection is very high).
3.  **Anaphylaxis:** History of severe allergic reaction to a previous dose.
4.  **Severe Acute Illness:** Vaccination should be postponed.
5.  **Immunosuppression:** Live vaccine is contraindicated in symptomatic HIV or severe immunodeficiency.

---

### **Adverse Events Following Immunization (AEFI)**

The cell-culture vaccines are generally safe.
*   **Common:** Mild local pain, redness, transient fever, headache, irritability,.
*   **Rare:** Hypersensitivity reactions (urticaria, angioedema).
*   **Obsolete Vaccine Side Effect:** The older mouse-brain vaccine was associated with neurological complications (ADEM), which led to its discontinuation.

### **Clinical Relevance**
*   **Epidemic Control:** In outbreak settings, vaccination campaigns target the susceptible population (1â€“15 years). Vector control (fogging) is supplementary but vaccination is the primary defense.
*   **Herd Immunity:** High coverage is essential because pigs (amplifier hosts) and birds (reservoirs) maintain the virus in the environment; humans are dead-end hosts, so vaccinating humans protects the individual but does not stop the transmission cycle in nature.
