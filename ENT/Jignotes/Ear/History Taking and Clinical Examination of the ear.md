# [[Ear]] > History Taking and Clinical Examination of the ear

# **HISTORY TAKING AND CLINICAL EXAMINATION OF THE EAR**

## **I. DEMOGRAPHIC DATA**
*   **Name, Age, Sex:** Age helps in narrowing differential diagnosis (e.g., [[Acute Otitis Media]] is common in children; [[Presbycusis]] in elderly; [[Otosclerosis]] in 20-40 years).
*   **Occupation:** Crucial for ruling out [[Noise-Induced Hearing Loss]] (factory workers, military) or barotrauma (divers, pilots).
*   **Residence:** High prevalence of [[Chronic Suppurative Otitis Media]] in lower socioeconomic strata and overcrowding.,

---

## **II. CHIEF COMPLAINTS**
*Listed in chronological order of appearance.*
1.  Ear Discharge ([[Otorrhea]])
2.  Hard of Hearing (Deafness)
3.  Ear Pain ([[Otalgia]])
4.  Ringing sensation in ear ([[Tinnitus]])
5.  Dizziness / Spinning sensation ([[Vertigo]]),

---

## **III. HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS (HOPI)**

### **1. Ear Discharge (Otorrhea)**
Ask the following to characterize the pathology:

| **Characteristic** | **Options** | **Clinical Implication / Points Towards** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Acute / Gradual | Acute: [[Acute Otitis Media]] (AOM), Trauma. Gradual: [[Chronic Otitis Media]] (COM). |
| **Duration** | Days to Years | Long duration suggests chronic pathology. |
| **Nature/Color** | **Mucoid** | Tubotympanic (Mucosal) type of CSOM. |
| | **Purulent (Creamy)** | Atticoantral (Squamous) type of CSOM, [[Mastoiditis]]. |
| | **Mucopurulent** | Active Tubotympanic CSOM. |
| | **Serosanguinous** | [[Granular Myringitis]], Trauma, [[Carcinoma Middle Ear]]. |
| | **Watery** | [[Cerebrospinal Fluid Otorrhea]] (Trauma), Eczematous [[Otitis Externa]]. |
| | **Blackish/Grey** | [[Otomycosis]] (Fungal infection). |
| **Odor** | Foul-smelling | Bone destruction (Cholesteatoma/Unsafe CSOM). |
| | Odorless | Mucosal disease (Safe CSOM). |
| **Quantity** | Profuse | Mucosal disease (Tubotympanic). |
| | Scanty | Squamosal disease (Atticoantral). |
| **Blood Stained** | Yes/No | Granulations, Polyp, Malignancy, Trauma. |
| **Aggravating Factors**| URTI / Water entry | Suggests a perforation allowing entry of pathogens. |

### **2. Hard of Hearing (Hearing Loss)**
*   **Onset:** Sudden onset suggests viral neuritis, trauma, or vascular causes. Insidious onset suggests [[Presbycusis]], [[Otosclerosis]], or COM.
*   **Progression:** Fluctuating loss points to [[Meniere's Disease]] or [[Serous Otitis Media]]. Progressive loss points to Otosclerosis.
*   **Paracusis Willisii:** Does the patient hear better in noisy surroundings? (Positive in Otosclerosis).
*   **Autophony:** Does the patient's own voice sound loud/echo? (Seen in [[Patulous Eustachian Tube]]).

### **3. Ear Pain (Otalgia)**
*   **Nature:**
    *   *Throbbing:* Acute boil ([[Furuncle]]) or AOM.
    *   *Deep boring:* Severe, disturbing sleep (suggests [[Malignant Otitis Externa]] or Complications).
    *   *Pricking:* Acute Suppurative Otitis Media.
*   **Aggravating Factors:**
    *   Pain on jaw movement/chewing: [[Furunculosis]] (movement of Temporomandibular joint compresses the canal).
*   **Referred Pain:** If the ear is normal on exam, investigate remote causes based on nerve supply:
    *   **CN V (Auriculotemporal):** Dental issues, TMJ arthritis, Tongue (ant. 2/3) carcinoma.,
    *   **CN IX (Glossopharyngeal):** Tonsillitis, base of tongue malignancy.
    *   **CN X (Vagus):** Laryngeal/Hypopharyngeal carcinoma.
    *   **C2, C3:** Cervical spondylosis.

### **4. Tinnitus**
*   **Pulsatile:** Synchronous with pulse. Suggests vascular tumors like [[Glomus Jugulare]] or [[Glomus Tympanicum]].,
*   **Non-pulsatile (Continuous):** [[Sensorineural Hearing Loss]], Meniere’s disease, Acoustic Neuroma.,
*   **Clicking:** Palatal myoclonus.

### **5. Vertigo**
*   **Episodic (Seconds):** [[Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo]] (BPPV).
*   **Episodic (Minutes to Hours):** Meniere’s Disease.
*   **Aggravated by:** Head movement (BPPV), Loud sounds (Tullio Phenomenon - [[Superior Semicircular Canal Dehiscence]]).

---

## **IV. NEGATIVE HISTORY**
*Used to rule out complications of CSOM.*,
*   **Pain/Swelling behind ear:** Rule out [[Mastoiditis]] or [[Mastoid Abscess]].
*   **Giddiness/Vertigo:** Rule out [[Labyrinthitis]] or Fistula.
*   **Facial asymmetry/deviation:** Rule out [[Facial Nerve Palsy]].
*   **Headache/Vomiting/Neck Rigidity:** Rule out Intracranial complications ([[Meningitis]], [[Brain Abscess]]).
*   **Diplopia/Retro-orbital pain:** Rule out [[Petrositis]] ([[Gradenigo’s Syndrome]]).

---

## **V. PAST & PERSONAL HISTORY**
*   **Past History:** Trauma, Previous ear surgery, Systemic diseases ([[Diabetes Mellitus]] predisposes to Malignant Otitis Externa), Tuberculosis.,
*   **Family History:** Otosclerosis often runs in families.
*   **Personal History:** Smoking, Alcohol, Dietary habits.

---

## **VI. LOCAL EXAMINATION OF EAR**
*Always examine the normal ear first.*

### **1. Pinna and Pre/Post-Auricular Region**
| **Structure** | **Finding** | **Diagnosis/Significance** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pinna Size/Shape** | Microtia / Anotia | Congenital anomalies. |
| | Cauliflower Ear | Hematoma of Pinna (Boxer's ear). |
| **Tragus** | **Tragal Tenderness** | Hallmark of [[Furunculosis]] (Otitis Externa)., |
| **Pre-auricular** | Pit / Sinus | Congenital Pre-auricular sinus (may get infected). |
| | Lymph Nodes | Enlarged in Otitis Externa. |
| **Post-auricular** | Swelling/Abscess | Mastoiditis. |
| | Scar | Previous Mastoidectomy. |
| | Obliteration of Groove | Furunculosis (groove obliterated); Mastoiditis (groove preserved/ironed out). |
| **Mastoid** | **Tenderness** | Elicited at 3 points: Cymba concha (Antrum), Tip, Posterior border (Emissary vein). Indicates Acute Mastoiditis., |

### **2. External Auditory Canal (EAC)**
*   **Wax:** Brown/Black mass.
*   **Discharge:** Describe color and consistency.
*   **Debris:** "Wet newspaper" appearance suggests **Otomycosis** (Fungal).
*   **Swelling:** Generalized narrowing (Otitis Externa); Localized swelling (Furuncle).
*   **Granulations:** Red fleshy mass. If at the bony-cartilaginous junction in a diabetic/elderly, suspect [[Malignant Otitis Externa]].
*   **Bony Growths:**
    *   *Exostosis:* Multiple, bilateral, sessile (Surfer's ear).
    *   *Osteoma:* Single, unilateral, pedunculated.

### **3. Tympanic Membrane (TM)**
> [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Draw the TM divided into 4 quadrants - Pars Tensa and Pars Flaccida
*   **Color:**
    *   *Pearly Grey:* Normal.
    *   *Red/Congested:* Acute Otitis Media (AOM), Glomus tumor (Rising Sun sign).,
    *   *Blue:* Hemotympanum, Secretory Otitis Media.
    *   *Flamingo Pink:* Active Otosclerosis (Schwartze Sign).
*   **Position:**
    *   *Retracted:* Foreshortened malleus, prominent lateral process, absent cone of light. Points to [[Eustachian Tube Dysfunction]].,
    *   *Bulging:* Acute Otitis Media (pus under tension).
*   **Perforation:** Describe Type and Site.
    *   *Central Perforation:* Surrounded by TM remnant on all sides. Seen in Tubotympanic (Safe) CSOM.
    *   *Marginal Perforation:* Annulus is eroded. Seen in Atticoantral (Unsafe) CSOM.
    *   *Attic Perforation:* Seen in Pars Flaccida. Indicates [[Cholesteatoma]].

## **VII. FUNCTIONAL EXAMINATION**

### **1. Tuning Fork Tests (TFT)**
*Ideally using a 512 Hz Tuning fork.*

| **Test** | **Procedure** | **Interpretation** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rinne's Test** | Compare Air Conduction (AC) vs Bone Conduction (BC). | **Positive (AC>BC):** Normal or SNHL.<br>**Negative (BC>AC):** Conductive Hearing Loss (CHL).<br>**False Negative:** Severe unilateral SNHL (dead ear)., |
| **Weber's Test** | Fork on forehead/vertex. Check lateralization. | **Central:** Normal.<br>**Lateralized to Disease Ear:** CHL.<br>**Lateralized to Better Ear:** SNHL., |
| **ABC Test** | Absolute Bone Conduction. Compare patient's BC with examiner's (normal). | **Same as examiner:** Normal or CHL.<br>**Reduced:** Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SNHL). |
| **Gelle’s Test** | Fork on mastoid + Siegel’s speculum pressure. | **Positive:** Decreased hearing with pressure (Normal/SNHL).<br>**Negative:** No change (Fixed Stapes/Otosclerosis)., |

**Mnemonic for Weber:** **SOCS** = **S**NHL **O**pposite side, **C**onductive **S**ame side.

### **2. Fistula Test**
*   **Method:** Apply pressure on tragus or use Siegel’s speculum.
*   **Positive Sign:** Nystagmus and vertigo induced by pressure.
*   **Indication:** Erosion of Lateral Semicircular Canal (Labyrinthine Fistula) usually by Cholesteatoma.,
*   **Hennebert's Sign:** False positive fistula test (seen in Congenital Syphilis, Meniere's).,

---

## **VIII. FACIAL NERVE EXAMINATION**
*   Ask patient to: Frown, close eyes tightly, show teeth, blow out cheeks.
*   Check for deviation of angle of mouth, inability to close eye (Bell's phenomenon).
*   **Grading:** House-Brackmann Grading System (I to VI).

---

## **IX. DIAGNOSIS (FORMAT)**
*Write the diagnosis in the following order:*
1.  **Laterality:** Right / Left / Bilateral.
2.  **Pathology:** Chronic Otitis Media / Otosclerosis.
3.  **Type:** Tubotympanic (Mucosal) / Atticoantral (Squamous).
4.  **Stage:** Active / Inactive / Quiescent.
5.  **Functional Status:** With Conductive / Sensorineural Hearing Loss.
6.  **Complications:** With / Without complications (e.g., Mastoiditis).
