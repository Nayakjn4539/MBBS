# [[Hospital Waste Management]] > Biomedical Waste Management Rules

### Biomedical Waste Management Rules (Color Coding)

Biomedical waste (BMW) management in India is currently governed by the **Biomedical Waste Management Rules, 2016** (superseding the 1998 rules), notified by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change. The core principle is **[[Segregation at source]]**.

#### General Principles
1.  **Segregation:** Waste must be separated at the point of generation into color-coded bags/containers.
2.  **Storage:** Untreated biomedical waste should not be stored beyond **48 hours**.
3.  **Labeling:** Bags must carry the **[[Biohazard Symbol]]** and Cytotoxic Hazard Symbol (if applicable).
4.  **Transport:** Dedicated vehicles must be used to transport waste to the Common Biomedical Waste Treatment Facility ([[CBWTF]]).

---

#### Color Coding and Disposal Categories (Schedule I & II)

The rules categorize waste into four main color categories: **Yellow, Red, White, and Blue**.

##### 1. YELLOW Category
*   **Container Type:** Non-chlorinated yellow plastic bags.
*   **Types of Waste:**
    *   **[[Human Anatomical Waste]]:** Human tissues, organs, body parts, fetus below viability.
    *   **Animal Anatomical Waste:** Experimental animal carcasses, body parts, organs.
    *   **Soiled Waste:** Items contaminated with blood/body fluids (e.g., cotton, dressings, plaster casts, beddings contaminated with blood).
    *   **Expired/Discarded Medicines:** Pharmaceutical waste, cytotoxic drugs (separate bag).
    *   **Chemical Waste:** Discarded disinfectants, X-ray developer fluid.
    *   **Chemical Liquid Waste:** Liquid from labs/washing/cleaning (requires pre-treatment).
    *   **Microbiology/Biotech Waste:** Cultures, stocks, live/attenuated vaccines.
*   **Treatment/Disposal:**
    *   **[[Incineration]]** (High-temperature dry oxidation).
    *   **Plasma Pyrolysis**.
    *   **Deep Burial** (Only in rural/remote areas where CBWTF is not available).

> **Mnemonic:** **Y**ellow = **Y**ucky stuff (Flesh, Pus, Blood, Expired meds) or **Y**ellow = **J**ala do (Incinerate/Burn).

##### 2. RED Category
*   **Container Type:** Non-chlorinated red plastic bags or containers.
*   **Types of Waste (Recyclable):**
    *   Wastes generated from disposable items such as **tubing**, **bottles**, **intravenous tubes and sets**.
    *   **Catheters**, **urine bags**.
    *   **Syringes** (without needles and fixed needle syringes).
    *   **Gloves**.
    *   **Vacutainers** (without blood).
*   **Treatment/Disposal:**
    *   **[[Autoclaving]]** / Microwaving / Hydroclaving.
    *   Followed by **Shredding** or Mutilation (to prevent reuse).
    *   Sent for **Recycling** (Energy recovery/Road making).
    *   *Note: Chlorinated plastics should not be incinerated as they release dioxins/furans.*

> **Mnemonic:** **R**ed = **R**ecyclable (Plastics/Rubber) or **R**ough treatment (Autoclave & Shred).

##### 3. WHITE (Translucent) Category
*   **Container Type:** Puncture-proof, leak-proof, tamper-proof containers.
*   **Types of Waste (Sharps):**
    *   **Needles**, syringes with fixed needles.
    *   Needles from needle tip cutter or burner.
    *   **Scalpels**, **blades**.
    *   Any contaminated sharp object that may cause puncture/cuts.
*   **Treatment/Disposal:**
    *   **Autoclaving** or Dry Heat Sterilization.
    *   Followed by **Shredding** or Mutilation.
    *   **Encapsulation** in metal containers or concrete.
    *   Final disposal in iron foundries or sanitary landfill.

> **Mnemonic:** **W**hite = **W**eapon (Sharps that can hurt/puncture).

##### 4. BLUE Category
*   **Container Type:** Cardboard boxes with blue colored marking.
*   **Types of Waste:**
    *   **Broken or discarded Glassware** (e.g., medicine vials, ampoules).
    *   **Metallic Body Implants** (e.g., plates, screws, orthopedic implants).
*   **Treatment/Disposal:**
    *   Disinfection (Sodium Hypochlorite) / Autoclaving / Microwaving.
    *   Followed by **Recycling**.

> **Mnemonic:** **B**lue = **B**roken Glass / **B**ody Implants.

#### Clinical Relevance
*   **Occupational Safety:** Proper segregation prevents **[[Needle Stick Injuries]]** among healthcare workers and rag pickers.
*   **Infection Control:** Reduces the risk of transmission of blood-borne pathogens like **HIV**, **Hepatitis B**, and **Hepatitis C**.
*   **Environmental Protection:** Prevents toxic emissions (like dioxins) caused by burning chlorinated plastics (Red category waste).
*   **Cytotoxic Drugs:** Expired cytotoxic drugs are kept in **Yellow** bags but must be incinerated at higher temperatures (>1200Â°C) or returned to the manufacturer.
