# Proptosis (Exophthalmos)

### Definition
*   **Proptosis**: Forward displacement or protrusion of the eyeball beyond the orbital margin.
*   **Exophthalmos**: Often used synonymously with proptosis, but clinically the term is specifically reserved for proptosis associated with [[Thyroid Eye Disease]] (Graves' ophthalmopathy).
*   **Normal Values**:
    *   Distance between the lateral orbital rim and corneal apex is typically **10–21 mm**.
    *   A difference of **>2 mm** between the two eyes is considered significant,.

---

### Classification and Causes

Proptosis is classified based on laterality, onset, and dynamic characteristics.

#### 1. Etiological Classification (Unilateral vs. Bilateral)

| Type | Common Causes |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Unilateral Proptosis** | • **Inflammatory**: [[Orbital cellulitis]] (Most common cause in children), [[Panophthalmitis]], [[Pseudotumour]], [[Cavernous sinus thrombosis]] (early stage).<br>• **Vascular**: [[Orbital varix]], [[Carotico-cavernous fistula]] (CCF).<br>• **Tumors**: [[Dermoid cyst]], [[Lacrimal gland tumours]], [[Optic nerve glioma]], [[Meningioma]], [[Rhabdomyosarcoma]] (children), [[Cavernous haemangioma]] (most common benign tumor in adults).<br>• **Mucoceles**: Frontal or Ethmoidal sinus mucocele.<br>• **Thyroid**: [[Thyroid ophthalmopathy]] (Most common cause in adults). |
| **Bilateral Proptosis** | • **Endocrine**: [[Thyroid Eye Disease]] (Most common cause).<br>• **Developmental**: [[Craniofacial dysostosis]] (e.g., [[Crouzon syndrome]], [[Oxycephaly]]).<br>• **Inflammatory**: [[Cavernous sinus thrombosis]] (late stage), [[Mikulicz's syndrome]].<br>• **Systemic/Tumors**: [[Lymphoma]], [[Leukaemia]] (Chloroma), Metastatic [[Neuroblastoma]] (children), [[Histiocytosis]]. |

#### 2. Based on Direction of Displacement
*   **Axial Proptosis**: The eye is pushed directly forward. Indicates a space-occupying lesion within the muscle cone (**Intraconal**).
    *   *Causes*: [[Optic nerve glioma]], [[Optic nerve sheath meningioma]], [[Cavernous haemangioma]], [[Thyroid ophthalmopathy]].
*   **Eccentric (Abaxial) Proptosis**: The eye is displaced forward and to one side. Indicates a lesion outside the muscle cone (**Extraconal**).
    *   *Displacement Down and In*: [[Lacrimal gland tumours]], [[Dermoid cyst]].
    *   *Displacement Down and Out*: [[Ethmoidal mucocele]], [[Frontal mucocele]], [[Osteoma]].
    *   *Displacement Upwards*: Maxillary sinus tumors.

#### 3. Based on Onset and Dynamics
*   **Acute Proptosis** (Sudden onset):
    *   [[Orbital emphysema]] (fracture of medial orbital wall).
    *   [[Orbital haemorrhage]] (retrobulbar hematoma).
    *   Rupture of ethmoidal mucocele.
*   **Intermittent Proptosis**: Appears/disappears or increases with head posture/valsalva.
    *   [[Orbital varix]] (Most common; increases on bending forward/valsalva due to lack of valves in orbital veins),.
    *   Periodic orbital edema.
    *   Highly vascular tumors.
*   **Pulsatile Proptosis**: Visible or palpable pulsation of the globe.
    *   [[Carotico-cavernous fistula]] (CCF) - associated with bruit and thrill.
    *   Deficient orbital roof (transmission of cerebral pulsations): [[Neurofibromatosis type 1]], [[Meningocele]], Post-traumatic/surgical hiatus,.
    *   Saccular aneurysm of ophthalmic artery.

#### 4. Pseudoproptosis
Appearance of protrusion without true forward displacement.
*   **Causes**:
    *   [[Buphthalmos]] (Enlarged eyeball).
    *   High axial [[Myopia]].
    *   [[Lid retraction]] (Thyroid eye disease).
    *   [[Enophthalmos]] of the contralateral eye.
    *   Asymmetry of orbits,.

### Clinical Evaluation

#### 1. History
*   **Onset**: Sudden (hemorrhage, inflammation) vs. Gradual (tumors).
*   **Pain**:
    *   *Painful*: [[Orbital cellulitis]], [[Pseudotumour]], Malignant lacrimal tumors, [[Cavernous sinus thrombosis]].
    *   *Painless*: [[Thyroid ophthalmopathy]], Benign tumors ([[Cavernous haemangioma]]), [[Lymphoma]].
*   **Progression**: Rapid (Rhabdomyosarcoma in children) vs. Slow.
*   **Aggravating factors**: Increases on bending/coughing ([[Orbital varix]]).

#### 2. Inspection
*   **Direction**: Axial vs. Eccentric.
*   **Lids**: Retraction, lag (Thyroid), Ptosis (Tumors, Neurogenic).
*   **Conjunctiva**: Chemosis, congestion (Thyroid, CCF), corkscrew vessels.
*   **Inflammatory signs**: Redness, warmth (Cellulitis).

#### 3. Palpation
*   **Resistance to Retropulsion**:
    *   *Firm/Hard*: Solid tumors (Malignancy).
    *   *Soft/Compressible*: Vascular lesions, cysts.
*   **Orbital Rim**: Check for steps/fractures.
*   **Pulsation**: Check for transmitted pulsations.

#### 4. Measurement (Exophthalmometry)
*   **[[Hertel’s exophthalmometer]]**:
    *   Most common and preferred instrument.
    *   Uses mirrors to measure the distance from the lateral orbital rim to the corneal apex.
    *   Measures both eyes simultaneously (binocular).
*   **[[Luedde’s exophthalmometer]]**:
    *   Simple plastic ruler; measures one eye at a time (uniocular).
    *   Easier for children but less accurate,.

#### 5. Auscultation
*   Check for **Orbital Bruit** (heard in [[Carotico-cavernous fistula]]) over the globe, temple, or forehead,.

#### 6. Specific Tests
*   **Ocular Movements**: Restriction indicates thyroid myopathy (fibrosis) or neurological involvement/entrapment (fracture).
*   **Forced Duction Test**: Differentiates restrictive vs. paralytic causes.
*   **[[Transillumination]]**: Helpful for anterior cystic lesions.
*   **Fundus Examination**: Look for [[Optic disc edema]] (compression), [[Optic atrophy]], [[Choroidal folds]], [[Opticociliary shunts]] (Meningioma).

### Investigations

| Modality | Indication/Findings |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Plain X-ray** | • **Bone destruction**: Malignancy.<br>• **Hyperostosis**: [[Meningioma]].<br>• **Enlarged Optic Foramen**: [[Optic nerve glioma]].<br>• **Calcification**: [[Retinoblastoma]], [[Meningioma]]. |
| **[[Ultrasonography]] (USG)** | • Initial screening; differentiates cystic vs. solid.<br>• **A-scan**: Reflectivity patterns.<br>• **B-scan**: Topography, muscle thickness (Thyroid), "T-sign" in posterior scleritis. |
| **[[CT Scan]]** | • **Investigation of Choice (IOC)** for bony lesions, trauma ([[Blow out fracture]]), and calcification.<br>• Delineates location (intraconal/extraconal) and extension into sinuses/cranium. |
| **[[MRI]]** | • **IOC** for soft tissue lesions, optic nerve tumors, and orbital apex lesions.<br>• Superior resolution for intracranial extension. |
| **Carotid Angiography** | • Digital Subtraction Angiography (DSA) is the Gold Standard for [[Carotico-cavernous fistula]] (CCF) and vascular malformations. |
| **Biopsy** | • **[[Fine needle aspiration biopsy]] (FNAB)**: For suspected metastasis or unresectable tumors.<br>• **Incisional/Excisional Biopsy**: Definitive histopathology. |
| **Systemic Tests** | • **Thyroid Profile**: T3, T4, TSH (Thyroid eye disease).<br>• **Casoni’s Test**: Hydatid cyst.<br>• **Blood Counts**: Leukemia/Lymphoma. |

### Clinical Implications
*   **Emergency**: Acute proptosis with visual loss requires immediate decompression (e.g., [[Retrobulbar haemorrhage]]).
*   **Thyroid**: Most common cause of unilateral *and* bilateral proptosis in adults.
*   **Children**: [[Orbital cellulitis]] is the most common cause; [[Rhabdomyosarcoma]] is the most common primary malignancy (rapid onset).
