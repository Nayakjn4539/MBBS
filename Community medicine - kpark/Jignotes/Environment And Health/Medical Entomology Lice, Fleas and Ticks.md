# [[Environment And Health]] > Medical Entomology Lice, Fleas and Ticks

### **I. LICE (PEDICULUS)**

Lice are wingless ectoparasites. They are [[dorsoventrally flattened]] with clawed legs adapted for clinging to hair or clothing.

#### **1. Types of Human Lice**
| Feature | **Head Louse** (*Pediculus capitis*) | **Body Louse** (*Pediculus corporis*) | **Crab/Pubic Louse** (*Phthirus pubis*) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Habitat** | Scalp hair | Seams of clothing | Pubic/Perineal hair |
| **Appearance** | Darker, smaller | Lighter, larger | Square body, "Crab-like" |
| **Disease Vector** | **No** (Major nuisance) | **YES** (Major vector) | **No** (Causes pediculosis pubis) |
| **Egg Laying** | Glued to hair | Glued to clothes | Cemented to pubic hair |

#### **2. Life Cycle**
*   **Metamorphosis:** Incomplete/Gradual (3 stages).
*   **Stages:** [[Egg (Nit)]] $\rightarrow$ Nymph $\rightarrow$ Adult.
*   **Duration:** Cycle takes about **15-17 days**.
*   **Lifespan:** Adults live for 30–50 days.

#### **3. Diseases Transmitted (By Body Louse)**
*   **Mode:** Not by bite, but by scratching **infected feces** or crushing lice into abraded skin.
*   **Diseases:**
    1.  **Epidemic Typhus** (Agent: *Rickettsia prowazekii*)
    2.  **Relapsing Fever** (Agent: *Borrelia recurrentis*)
    3.  **Trench Fever** (Agent: *Rickettsia quintana*)
    4.  **Dermatitis** (Pediculosis - secondary infection)

> **Mnemonic for Lice Diseases:**
> **REL**ay **T**he **E**pidemic
> *   **R** - **R**elapsing fever
> *   **E** - **E**pidemic typhus
> *   **L** - **L**ouse
> *   **T** - **T**rench fever
> *   **E** - **E**xcoriation (Dermatitis)

#### **4. Control Measures**
*   **Insecticides:**
    *   **1% Malathion powder:** Chemical of choice.
    *   **Permethrin/Lindane:** For head lice.
*   **Hygiene:** Daily bath, washing clothes in hot water, autoclaving bedding.
*   **Treatment:** [[Benzyl benzoate]] emulsion (25%) application.

### **II. FLEAS (XENOPSYLLA)**

Fleas are **laterally compressed**, wingless insects with powerful hind legs for jumping. They are temporary ectoparasites.

#### **1. Important Species**
*   *Xenopsylla cheopis* (Oriental Rat Flea): **Most efficient vector** for plague.
*   *Xenopsylla astia*: Less efficient vector.
*   *Pulex irritans*: Human flea.
*   *Tunga penetrans*: Sand flea/Chigoe (Causes localized necrosis/ulcers).

#### **2. Life Cycle**
*   **Metamorphosis:** Complete (4 stages).
*   **Stages:** Egg $\rightarrow$ Larva $\rightarrow$ Pupa $\rightarrow$ Adult.
*   **Duration:** ~3 weeks to months depending on conditions.

#### **3. Mechanism of Transmission: [[Blocked Flea]]**
*   Plague bacilli multiply in the flea's stomach and block the **proventriculus**.
*   The flea gets hungry (blood cannot enter stomach) $\rightarrow$ Bites frantically.
*   During biting, it **regurgitates** bacilli-laden blood back into the host.
*   **Clinical Relevance:** A partially blocked flea is more dangerous than a totally blocked flea because it lives longer and bites more people.

#### **4. Flea Indices (Surveillance)**
Crucial for predicting [[Plague]] outbreaks.
*   **General Flea Index:** Average number of fleas of all species per rat.
*   **Specific Flea Index (Cheopis Index):** Average number of *X. cheopis* per rat.
    *   **Critical Value:** > 1 indicates potential explosiveness of Plague.

#### **5. Diseases Transmitted**
1.  **Bubonic Plague** (*Yersinia pestis*)
2.  **Endemic (Murine) Typhus** (*Rickettsia typhi*)
3.  **Chiggerosis**
4.  **Hymenolepis diminuta** (Rat tapeworm)

#### **6. Control Measures**
*   **Insecticides:** 10% DDT dust, BHC, Malathion or Carbaryl insufflated into rat burrows.
*   **Rodent Control:** Must be done **AFTER** or **ALONG WITH** flea control (killing rats first releases hungry fleas to bite humans).
*   **Repellents:** DEET, Benzyl benzoate.

### **III. TICKS**

Ticks are blood-sucking arachnids (8 legs in adults/nymphs).

#### **1. Hard Ticks vs. Soft Ticks**

| Feature | **Hard Ticks** (Ixodidae) | **Soft Ticks** (Argasidae) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scutum (Shield)** | **Present** (Dorsal shield) | **Absent** |
| **Head (Capitulum)** | Visible from above (Anterior) | Invisible from above (Ventral) |
| **Feeding** | Slow feeders (Days) | Fast feeders (Minutes/Night) |
| **Starvation** | Cannot stand starvation | Can survive starvation for years |
| **Example** | *Haemaphysalis*, *Ixodes* | *Ornithodoros* |

#### **2. Diseases Transmitted**

**A. Hard Ticks Transmit:**
1.  **Kyasanur Forest Disease (KFD):** (Vector: *Haemaphysalis spinigera*)
2.  **Tick Typhus:** (Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever)
3.  **Viral Encephalitis**
4.  **Tularemia**
5.  **Tick Paralysis:** (Caused by toxin in saliva, not infectious agent)
6.  **Babesiosis**

**B. Soft Ticks Transmit:**
1.  **Relapsing Fever** (*Borrelia duttoni*)
2.  **Q Fever**

> **Mnemonic for Hard Tick Diseases:**
> **TV KFD** (Watch TV in KFD forest)
> *   **T** - **T**ick Typhus / **T**ularemia
> *   **V** - **V**iral Encephalitis
> *   **KFD** - **K**yasanur **F**orest **D**isease

#### **3. Control Measures**
*   **Chemical:** Acaricides like DDT, Lindane, Malathion.
*   **Environmental:** Filling cracks/crevices (Soft ticks hide here).
*   **Personal:** Protective clothing, impregnating clothes with repellents (Permethrin).
*   **Manual Removal:** Remove gently with forceps without crushing (to prevent release of infective fluids).

### **IV. KEY DISEASE PROFILES (Summary)**

#### **1. Kyasanur Forest Disease (KFD)**
*   **Agent:** KFD Virus (Flavivirus).
*   **Vector:** Hard Tick (*Haemaphysalis spinigera*).
*   **Reservoir:** Rats, Squirrels (Monkeys are amplifying hosts).
*   **Host:** Man is an incidental, **dead-end host**.
*   **Incubation:** 3–8 days.
*   **Control:** Tick control, Vaccination (Killed KFD vaccine).

#### **2. Plague**
*   **Agent:** *Yersinia pestis* (Gram -ve coccobacillus).
*   **Vector:** Rat Flea (*Xenopsylla cheopis*).
*   **Reservoir:** Wild rodents (*Tatera indica*).
*   **Transmission:** Bite of blocked flea (Bubonic), Droplet (Pneumonic).
*   **Control:** DDT dusting (10%), Tetracycline chemoprophylaxis.

#### **3. Epidemic Typhus**
*   **Agent:** *Rickettsia prowazekii*.
*   **Vector:** Body Louse.
*   **Reservoir:** Humans.
*   **Transmission:** Scratching infected louse feces into skin.
