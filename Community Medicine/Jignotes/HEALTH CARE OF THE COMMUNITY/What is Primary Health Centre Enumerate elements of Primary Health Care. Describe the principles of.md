### Primary Health Centre (PHC)

The [[Primary Health Centre]] (PHC) is the cornerstone of rural health services and acts as the **first contact point** between the village community and the Medical Officer. It is established and maintained by the State Governments under the Minimum Needs Programme (MNP).

**Population Norms:**
*   **Plains:** 1 PHC for every **30,000** population.
*   **Hilly/Tribal/Backward Areas:** 1 PHC for every **20,000** population.

**Infrastructure:**
*   **Beds:** Provision for **4â€“6** beds for in-patient care.
*   **Staff:** A typical PHC has a staff of **15 members**, led by a Medical Officer.
*   **Referral:** It acts as a referral unit for **6 Sub-centres** and refers patients to Community Health Centres (CHCs).

### Elements of Primary Health Care

The **Alma-Ata Declaration (1978)** outlined 8 essential components of Primary Health Care.

**Mnemonic: ELEMENTS**

| Letter | Component | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **E** | **E**ducation | Education concerning prevailing health problems and methods of preventing/controlling them. |
| **L** | **L**ocally Endemic Diseases | Prevention and control of locally endemic diseases (e.g., Malaria, TB). |
| **E** | **E**ssential Drugs | Provision of essential drugs. |
| **M** | **M**aternal & Child Health | Maternal and child health care, including family planning. |
| **E** | **E**PI (Immunization) | Immunization against major infectious diseases. |
| **N** | **N**utrition | Promotion of food supply and proper nutrition. |
| **T** | **T**reatment | Appropriate treatment of common diseases and injuries. |
| **S** | **S**afe Water & Sanitation | An adequate supply of safe water and basic sanitation. |

### Principles of Primary Health Care

There are four pillars or principles that support the concept of Primary Health Care.

1.  **[[Equitable Distribution]]:** Health services must be shared equally by all people irrespective of their ability to pay. It aims to redress the imbalance between urban and rural health care by shifting the focus to rural areas where the majority of the population lives.
2.  **[[Community Participation]]:** Involvement of individuals, families, and communities in promoting their own health and welfare. It emphasizes self-reliance and ensures that health programmes are not just government-imposed but accepted by the people (e.g., ASHA, Village Health Guides).
3.  **[[Intersectoral Coordination]]:** Health cannot be improved by the health sector alone. It requires the joint effort of related sectors such as agriculture, animal husbandry, food, industry, education, housing, and public works.
4.  **[[Appropriate Technology]]:** The technology used should be scientifically sound, adaptable to local needs, acceptable to those who apply it, and affordable for the community and country (e.g., ORS, vaccine vial monitors).

### Services Provided at a Primary Health Centre

The PHC delivers comprehensive **primary health care** through the following services:

1.  **Medical Care:**
    *   OPD services (6 hours/day).
    *   24-hour emergency services (management of injuries, poisons, bites).
    *   In-patient services (4-6 beds).
    *   Referral services to CHCs or District Hospitals.
2.  **Maternal and Child Health (MCH) & Family Planning:**
    *   **Antenatal Care:** Registration, check-ups, IFA/TT administration.
    *   **Intranatal Care:** 24-hour delivery services (normal and assisted).
    *   **Postnatal Care:** Nursing mother care, promotion of breastfeeding.
    *   **Newborn/Child Care:** Essential newborn care, immunization, IMNCI.
    *   **Family Planning:** Sterilization (Tubectomy/Vasectomy), IUD insertion, counseling.
    *   **MTP Services:** Safe abortion services.
3.  **Nutrition Services:** Diagnosis of malnutrition/anemia, Vitamin A prophylaxis, coordination with ICDS (Anganwadi).
4.  **School Health Services:** Regular check-ups and treatment for school children.
5.  **Control of Communicable Diseases:** Prevention and control of locally endemic diseases like Malaria, TB (DOTS centre), Leprosy, HIV/AIDS (ICTC), etc..
6.  **Vital Statistics:** Collection and reporting of births and deaths.
7.  **Health Education:** Behaviour Change Communication (BCC) regarding hygiene, nutrition, and lifestyle.
8.  **Basic Laboratory Services:** Routine blood, urine, stool tests, sputum microscopy for TB, malaria slides, rapid pregnancy tests.
9.  **National Health Programmes:** Implementation of all relevant national health programmes.

### Role of Medical Officer in PHC

The **Medical Officer (MO)** is the captain of the health team at the PHC and provides leadership.

1.  **Curative/Clinical Functions:**
    *   Organizes and conducts Outpatient (OPD) clinics.
    *   Manages in-door patients and attends to emergencies.
    *   Refers complicated cases to higher centres.
    *   Attends to medico-legal cases.
    *   Organizes laboratory services.

2.  **Administrative and Supervisory Functions:**
    *   Supervises and guides the Health Assistants and Health Workers.
    *   Visits sub-centres regularly to monitor field activities.
    *   Manages personnel (leave, discipline) and supplies (drugs, equipment).
    *   Maintains records and submits reports to the Chief Medical Officer (CMO).

3.  **Preventive and Promotive Functions:**
    *   Ensures implementation of National Health Programmes (e.g., UIP, RNTCP).
    *   Organizes MCH and Family Planning clinics.
    *   Controls communicable diseases and investigates epidemics.
    *   Promotes environmental sanitation and safe water supply.

4.  **Training and Educational Functions:**
    *   Organizes training programs for health personnel, **ASHAs**, **Dais**, and Anganwadi workers.
    *   Conducts staff meetings (usually monthly) to review progress and solve problems.
    *   Imparts health education to the community.
