### **Introduction to Rural Health Care System in India**

The rural health care delivery system in India is structured as a **3-tier system** based on the recommendations of the **Bhore Committee (1946)** and the **Srivastava Committee (1975)**. It links the community to the national health system.

> Draw a pyramid showing Sub-centre at the base, PHC in the middle, and CHC at the top, leading to District Hospital

### **1. Sub-Centre (SC) / Health & Wellness Centre (HWC)**

*   **Definition:** It is the **most peripheral** and the **first contact point** between the primary health care system and the community.
*   **Administrative Control:** Established by the Central Government (**100% Central assistance**).
*   **Current Status:** Under **Ayushman Bharat**, Sub-centres are being upgraded to [[Health and Wellness Centres]] (HWC) to provide Comprehensive Primary Health Care (CPHC).

#### **Population Norms**
| Terrain Type | Population Covered |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hilly / Tribal / Backward Areas** | **3,000** |
| **Plains** | **5,000** |

#### **Staffing Pattern (Total = 3 to 4)**
According to [[Indian Public Health Standards]] (IPHS):
1.  **Community Health Officer (CHO):** BSc Nursing/Ayurveda practitioner trained in public health (Added in HWC).
2.  **Health Worker Female:** Also known as [[Auxiliary Nurse Midwife]] (ANM).
3.  **Health Worker Male:** [[Multi-Purpose Worker]] (MPW-M).
4.  **Voluntary Worker:** For cleaning/assistance.

#### **Key Functions**
*   **Maternal & Child Health:** Antenatal care, immunization, Vitamin A distribution.
*   **Family Planning:** Distribution of oral pills, condoms, and IUD insertion (Cu-T).
*   **Disease Control:** Collection of blood slides for Malaria, sputum for TB.
*   **Hygiene:** Chlorination of water sources.
*   **Vital Events:** Recording births and deaths.

> **Mnemonic for Sub-centre Norms:**
> **"3 to 5 stay Alive"**
> *   **3**000 (Hilly) to **5**000 (Plains) population.

### **2. Primary Health Centre (PHC)**

*   **Definition:** The first contact point between the village community and a **qualified Medical Officer**.
*   **Referral Link:** One PHC acts as a referral unit for **6 Sub-centres**.
*   **Administrative Control:** Established and maintained by the **State Government** under the Minimum Needs Programme (MNP).
*   **Bed Strength:** **4 to 6 beds** for inpatients.

#### **Population Norms**
| Terrain Type | Population Covered |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hilly / Tribal / Backward Areas** | **20,000** |
| **Plains** | **30,000** |

#### **Staffing Pattern (Total = 15)**
The Medical Officer is the leader of the PHC team.
1.  **Medical Officer:** 1 (MBBS).
2.  **AYUSH Practitioner:** 1 (Desirable under NRHM).
3.  **Pharmacist:** 1
4.  **Nurse Midwife (Staff Nurse):** 1
5.  **Health Worker (Female) / ANM:** 1
6.  **Health Educator / Block Extension Educator:** 1
7.  **Health Assistant (Male):** 1 (Supervises 6 Male HWs at SCs).
8.  **Health Assistant (Female) / LHV:** 1 (Supervises 6 ANMs at SCs).
9.  **Lab Technician:** 1
10. **Clerks/Drivers/Class IV:** Remaining staff.

#### **Functions (The 8 Essential Elements of Primary Health Care)**
1.  **Medical Care:** OPD (4 hours morning, 2 hours evening) and 24-hour emergency services.
2.  **MCH & Family Planning:** Deliveries, Tubectomy (if equipped), MTP services.
3.  **Safe Water & Sanitation.**
4.  **Control of Endemic Diseases.**
5.  **Vital Statistics.**
6.  **Health Education.**
7.  **National Health Programmes implementation.**
8.  **Referral Services.**

> **Clinical Relevance:** The PHC is the lowest level where a doctor is available to certify cases, conduct medico-legal autopsies (in some states), and administer anti-snake venom.

### **3. Community Health Centre (CHC)**

*   **Definition:** Provides **Secondary Health Care**. It serves as the **First Referral Unit (FRU)** for the rural population.
*   **Referral Link:** One CHC covers **4 PHCs**.
*   **Administrative Control:** State Government.
*   **Bed Strength:** **30 beds** (Essential). Can go up to 50-100 beds.

#### **Population Norms**
| Terrain Type | Population Covered |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hilly / Tribal / Backward Areas** | **80,000** |
| **Plains** | **1,20,000** |

#### **Staffing Pattern (Total = 46 to 52)**
Must have **4 Core Specialists**:
1.  **Surgeon**
2.  **Physician**
3.  **Obstetrician & Gynaecologist**
4.  **Paediatrician**

*Plus:* Anesthetist, Eye Surgeon, Dental Surgeon, Public Health Specialist, and supporting paramedical staff (Nurses, OT Technicians, Radiographers).

#### **Functions**
*   **Specialist Care:** Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, OBG.
*   **Emergency Obstetric Care (EmOC):** Caesarean sections (C-section).
*   **Investigative Facilities:** X-ray, ECG, Laboratory.
*   **Blood Storage:** Functioning as a blood storage unit is a criteria for FRU.
*   **MTP Services:** Safe abortion services.
