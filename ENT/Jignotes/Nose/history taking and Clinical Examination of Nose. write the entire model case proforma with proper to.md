# [[Nose]] > history taking and Clinical Examination of Nose. write the entire model case proforma with proper to

### **CASE PROFORMA: NOSE AND PARANASAL SINUSES**

### **I. BIODATA**
*   **Name, Age, Sex:**
    *   *Clinical Implication:* [[Juvenile Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma]] (JNA) is exclusive to adolescent males. [[Atrophic Rhinitis]] is common in females. [[Ethmoidal Polyps]] are common in adults, while [[Antrochoanal Polyps]] are seen in children/young adults.
*   **Occupation:**
    *   *Clinical Implication:* Woodworkers (hardwood dust) are prone to [[Adenocarcinoma]] of ethmoids. Nickel/chromium workers are prone to [[Squamous Cell Carcinoma]].
*   **Residence:**
    *   *Clinical Implication:* [[Rhinosporidiosis]] is endemic in certain areas (e.g., South India, Sri Lanka).

---

### **II. CHIEF COMPLAINTS**
Record in chronological order with duration. Common complaints include:
1.  Nasal obstruction
2.  Nasal discharge
3.  Epistaxis (Bleeding from nose)
4.  Headache/Facial pain
5.  Disturbance of smell (Anosmia/Hyposmia)
6.  Sneezing/Itching
7.  Post-nasal drip
8.  Change in voice.

---

### **III. HISTORY OF PRESENTING ILLNESS (HPI)**

#### **1. Nasal Obstruction**
*   **Laterality:** Unilateral or Bilateral?
    *   *Unilateral:* Foreign body (children), [[Antrochoanal Polyp]], Malignancy, Deviated Nasal Septum (DNS).
    *   *Bilateral:* Allergic rhinitis, [[Ethmoidal Polyps]], Adenoids, Septal hematoma/abscess.
*   **Onset:** Sudden (Foreign body, Trauma) vs. Gradual (Polyps, Tumors).
*   **Progression:** Progressive (Tumors, Polyps) vs. Stationary (DNS).
*   **Duration/Periodicity:** Constant vs. Intermittent (Allergic rhinitis).
*   **Relation to side:** Shifts with posture (Dependent obstruction) suggests [[Vasomotor Rhinitis]] or hypertrophied turbinates.

#### **2. Nasal Discharge (Rhinorrhea)**
*   **Nature:**
    *   *Watery:* Viral rhinitis, Allergic rhinitis, [[CSF Rhinorrhea]].
    *   *Mucoid:* Chronic rhinitis, Hypertrophic rhinitis.
    *   *Mucopurulent/Purulent:* Bacterial [[Rhinosinusitis]], Foreign body (unilateral foul-smelling in a child is a dictum for FB).
    *   *Blood-stained:* Malignancy, Granulomatous lesions, Trauma.
*   **Consistency:** Thick/viscid (Chronic sinusitis) vs. Thin (Allergy).
*   **Smell:** Foul-smelling ([[Atrophic Rhinitis]] or Foreign body).

#### **3. Epistaxis**
*   **Spontaneous or Traumatic:** (Nose picking, road traffic accidents).
*   **Frequency and Amount:** Scanty vs. Profuse.
*   **Side:** Unilateral vs. Bilateral.
*   **History of Bleeding disorders/Hypertension/Anticoagulants**.

#### **4. Headache and Facial Pain**
*   **Periodicity:**
    *   *Office Headache:* Pain starts in the morning, peaks at noon, subsides by evening. Points to [[Frontal Sinusitis]].
*   **Site of Pain:**
    *   *Cheek/Upper teeth:* [[Maxillary Sinusitis]].
    *   *Between eyes/Retro-orbital:* Ethmoid sinusitis.
    *   *Vertex/Occiput:* Sphenoid sinusitis.

#### **5. Disturbances of Smell**
*   **Anosmia/Hyposmia:** Loss or decreased smell (Polyps, Rhinitis, Trauma).
*   **Cacosmia:** Perception of foul smell (Maxillary sinusitis of dental origin, foreign body).
*   **Parosmia:** Altered perception of smell.

#### **6. Sneezing and Itching**
*   History of paroxysmal sneezing, itching of nose/eyes/palate points towards [[Allergic Rhinitis]].

---

### **IV. PAST HISTORY**
*   History of Trauma (Nasal bone fracture, Septal hematoma).
*   Previous surgeries (Septoplasty, FESS).
*   History of Tuberculosis, Syphilis, Asthma ([[Samter’s Triad]]: Asthma + Aspirin sensitivity + Nasal Polyps).

---

### **V. EXAMINATION**

#### **A. General Examination**
*   **Build/Nutrition:** Weight loss (Malignancy/TB).
*   **Pallor:** Chronic blood loss (Angiofibroma).
*   **Facies:**
    *   *Adenoid Facies:* Open mouth, elongated face, pinched nostrils (Adenoid hypertrophy).
    *   *Frog Face:* Widening of the nasal bridge (Nasal polyposis, Angiofibroma).

#### **B. Local Examination of Nose**

**1. External Nose (Inspection & Palpation)**
*   **Skin:** Redness/Furuncle (Vestibulitis), Butterfly rash (SLE/Lupus), Rhinophyma (Potato nose).
*   **Deformity:**
    *   *Saddle Nose:* Depressed dorsum (Leprosy, Syphilis, Trauma, Septal abscess).
    *   *Hump/Crooked/Deviated Nose:* Trauma or developmental.
*   **Swelling:**
    *   *Root of nose:* Glioma, Encephalocele, Dermoid.
    *   *Side of nose:* Mucoceles, Tumors.
*   **Tenderness:** Fracture, acute inflammation.
*   **Crepitus:** Fracture of nasal bones.

**2. Examination of Vestibule**
*   Tilt the tip of the nose up.
*   Look for: Boils (Furunculitis), Fissures, Crusting, Caudal dislocation of septum.

**3. Anterior Rhinoscopy**
*   **Instrument:** Thudichum’s nasal speculum.
*   **Technique:** Hold in left hand; insert closed, open inside, remove open (to avoid pulling vibrissae).

| Structure | Findings & Clinical Significance |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Nasal Mucosa** | **Pale/Boggy/Blue:** [[Allergic Rhinitis]].<br>**Red/Congested:** Acute Rhinitis/Sinusitis.<br>**Atrophic/Dry:** [[Atrophic Rhinitis]] (Roomy cavity). |
| **Septum** | **Deviated Nasal Septum (DNS):** C-shaped, S-shaped, Spurs.<br>**Perforation:** Trauma, Syphilis (Bony part), TB/Lupus (Cartilaginous part).<br>**Little's Area:** Dilated vessels/crusts (Epistaxis). |
| **Turbinates** | **Hypertrophy:** Mulberry appearance of [[Inferior Turbinate]].<br>**Atrophy:** Small turbinates (Atrophic Rhinitis).<br>**Concha Bullosa:** Pneumatized middle turbinate. |
| **Meatus (Middle)** | **Discharge:** Sinusitis (Maxillary/Frontal/Ant. Ethmoid).<br>**Polyp:** Pale, glistening, insensitive, mobile mass ([[Nasal Polyp]]). |
| **Masses** | **Bleeding Polyp:** [[Rhinosporidiosis]] (Strawberry mass).<br>**Pink/Fleshy:** Malignancy or Inverted Papilloma. |

**4. Probe Test**
*   Used to differentiate a **Polyp** from a **Turbinate**.
    *   *Polyp:* Soft, mobile, insensitive to touch, probe can be passed all around it, does not bleed easily.
    *   *Turbinate:* Hard (bone), fixed, sensitive, probe cannot be passed laterally.
*   > [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Drawing showing probe passing around a polyp vs stopping at turbinate attachment.

**5. Functional Examination of Nose**
*   **Cold Spatula Test (Mist Test):** Place a cold metal tongue depressor below nostrils. Compare fogging size. Reduced fogging = Obstruction.
*   **Cottle’s Test:** Pull the cheek laterally. If breathing improves = Positive test (Indicates valvular obstruction/collapse).

**6. Posterior Rhinoscopy**
*   **Instrument:** St. Clair Thomson post-nasal mirror.
*   **Technique:** Depress tongue, introduce warmed mirror behind uvula without touching posterior wall.
*   **Look For:**
    *   Choanae (Atresia/Polyps).
    *   Posterior end of turbinates (Mulberry hypertrophy).
    *   [[Eustachian Tube]] opening (Torus tubarius).
    *   Fossa of Rosenmuller (Site for [[Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma]]).
    *   Adenoids (Roof of nasopharynx).

---

### **VI. EXAMINATION OF PARANASAL SINUSES (PNS)**

**1. Inspection**
*   Swelling/Redness over cheek ([[Maxillary Sinusitis]]) or lid edema ([[Ethmoiditis]]).

**2. Palpation for Tenderness**
*   **Frontal:** Press on the floor of the sinus (supraorbital roof medially).
*   **Ethmoid:** Press on the medial wall of the orbit (near medial canthus).
*   **Maxillary:** Press over the canine fossa.

**3. Transillumination Test**
*   Performed in a dark room using a strong light source.
*   *Maxillary:* Light in mouth; infraorbital crescent glow = Normal. Opaque = Sinusitis/Mass.
*   *Frontal:* Light under orbital rim; forehead glow = Normal.

---

### **VII. DIAGNOSTIC BROCHURE: WHAT FINDINGS POINT TO**

| **Patient Complaint/Sign** | **Points Towards Diagnosis** | **Key Characteristic** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Watery Rhinorrhea + Sneezing** | [[Allergic Rhinitis]] | Pale/Boggy mucosa, "Allergic Salute" crease. |
| **Watery discharge (Unilateral) + Tilted head** | [[CSF Rhinorrhea]] | Increases on bending forward (**Reservoir Sign**), does not stiffen handkerchief. |
| **Unilateral foul-smelling discharge in child** | **Nasal Foreign Body** | **Dictum:** Unilateral foul discharge in a child is FB until proven otherwise. |
| **Facial Pain (Bending forward increases pain)** | **Acute Sinusitis** | Periodicity of headache, tenderness over sinus. |
| **Bilateral Nasal Obstruction + Grape-like masses** | [[Ethmoidal Polyps]] | Associated with Asthma/Aspirin sensitivity ([[Samter's Triad]]). |
| **Unilateral Obstruction + Mass in Choana** | [[Antrochoanal Polyp]] | Arises from maxillary sinus, grows back into nasopharynx. |
| **Profuse Epistaxis in Adolescent Male** | [[Juvenile Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma]] | "Frog Face" deformity, mass in nasopharynx. **Do Not Biopsy**. |
| **Strawberry-like mass (Bleeds on touch)** | [[Rhinosporidiosis]] | History of pond bathing, white dots on surface. |
| **Black Eschar on Turbinates/Palate** | **Invasive Fungal Sinusitis** ([[Mucormycosis]]) | Seen in Diabetics/Immunocompromised. Emergency!. |
| **Hard/Woody External Nose** | [[Rhinoscleroma]] | Hebra nose, Tapir nose. Biopsy: **Mikulicz cells**. |
| **Foul smell (Merciful Anosmia) + Crusts** | [[Atrophic Rhinitis]] | Roomy nasal cavity, atrophy of turbinates. |
| **Saddle Nose Deformity** | **Septal Destruction** | Trauma, Syphilis, TB, Wegener’s Granulomatosis. |
