### Definition of Social Security

**Social security** is defined as the security that society furnishes, through appropriate organization, against certain risks to which its members are exposed. These risks essentially include contingencies against which the individual of small means cannot effectively provide by his own ability or foresight alone, or even in private combination with his fellows.

The risks usually covered include:
*   Sickness
*   Maternity
*   Invalidity
*   Old age
*   Death
*   Unemployment

### Benefits Available Under [[ESI Act, 1948]]

The **Employees' State Insurance (ESI) Act, 1948** is a pioneering measure of social security in India. It provides protection to employees against loss of wages due to sickness, disablement, or death due to employment injury.

There are **7 Types of Benefits** provided under the Act:

**Mnemonic:** **S**ick **M**en **M**ay **D**ie **D**aily **F**or **R**ehabilitation

1.  **M**edical Benefit
2.  **S**ickness Benefit
3.  **M**aternity Benefit
4.  **D**isablement Benefit
5.  **D**ependant’s Benefit
6.  **F**uneral Expenses
7.  **R**ehabilitation Allowance

*(Note: There is also an unemployment allowance known as **Rajiv Gandhi Shramik Kalyan Yojana**).*

#### Summary Table of Cash Benefits

| Benefit Type | Description & Rate | Duration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sickness Benefit** | Periodical cash payment during certified sickness. Rate: ~70% of average daily wages. | Max 91 days in any 2 consecutive benefit periods. |
| **Extended Sickness Benefit** | For long-term diseases (e.g., TB, Cancer, Leprosy). Rate: 80% of wages. | Up to 2 years. |
| **Maternity Benefit** | Payable for confinement/miscarriage. Rate: 100% (Full wages). | **26 weeks** for confinement (updated). |
| **Disablement Benefit** | **Temporary:** 90% of wages.<br>**Permanent:** Life pension based on loss of earning capacity. | Temporary: Till recovery.<br>Permanent: Life long. |
| **Dependant’s Benefit** | Pension to dependants if worker dies due to employment injury. Rate: 90% of wages. | Shared among dependants. |
| **Funeral Expenses** | Cash payment to the eldest surviving member. | Max **Rs. 15,000/-**. |

### Medical Benefits

The **Medical Benefit** is the most important benefit under the ESI Scheme. Unlike other benefits which are mostly in cash, this is a benefit in **Kind**.

**Key Features:**
*   **Full Medical Care:** It consists of comprehensive medical care including hospitalization, free of cost, to the insured person and their family members.
*   **No Ceiling:** There is **no ceiling on expenditure** for the treatment of an insured person.
*   **Eligibility:** It is available from day one of entering insurable employment.

**Package of Services:**
The medical care provided is comprehensive and includes:
*   Out-patient care (OPD).
*   Supply of drugs and dressings.
*   Specialist services (all branches).
*   Pathological and radiological investigations.
*   **Domiciliary services** (Home visits by doctors).
*   Antenatal, natal, and postnatal services.
*   Immunization and Family Planning services.
*   Ambulance services.
*   In-patient treatment (Hospitalization).

**Systems of Delivery:**
The medical benefit is administered through two main patterns:

1.  **Direct Pattern:**
    *   In areas with a concentration of **1000 or more** employee family units.
    *   Services are provided through **ESI Dispensaries** and **ESI Hospitals** staffed by full-time medical and paramedical personnel.

2.  **Indirect Pattern (Panel System):**
    *   Used where the scattered population does not justify a separate dispensary.
    *   Registered Medical Practitioners are designated as **Insurance Medical Practitioners (IMPs)**.
    *   They provide treatment at their private clinics and are paid a capitation fee.

**Extension of Benefits:**
*   **Family:** Medical care is extended to the family members of the insured person.
*   **Retired/Disabled:** On payment of a nominal fee (Rs. 10/month), retired insured persons and permanently disabled workers can continue to avail medical care.
