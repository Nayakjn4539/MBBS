### CARE - India
**(Cooperative for Assistance and Relief Everywhere)**

**CARE** is one of the world's largest independent, non-profit, and non-sectarian international relief and development organizations. It was founded in North America in 1945 in the wake of the Second World War.

#### **1. Operations in India**
*   **Inception:** CARE began its operations in India in **1950**.
*   **Partnership:** It works in close partnership with the Government of India, State Governments, and Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs).
*   **Coverage:** Currently, it has projects in states like Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Orissa, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, and West Bengal.

#### **2. Evolution of Objectives**
CARE's focus in India has shifted over the decades to align with national needs:

| Time Period | Primary Focus |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Till 1980s** | The primary objective was to provide food for children in the age group of **6–11 years**. The largest CARE activity was the [[Mid-day meal scheme]] for primary school children. |
| **Mid-1980s Onwards** | The focus shifted to providing food support in the [[Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS)]] program and developing programs in health and income supplementation. |

#### **3. Key Health and Nutrition Projects**
CARE-India has been instrumental in implementing various projects focusing on women and child health:
*   **Integrated Nutrition and Health Project (INHP)**.
*   **Better Health and Nutrition Project**.
*   **Anemia Control Project**.
*   **Child Survival Project**.
*   **Improving Women's Health Project**.
*   **Improved Health Care for Adolescent Girls' Project**.
*   **Improving Women's Reproductive Health and Family Spacing Project**.
*   **Konkan Integrated Development Project**.

#### **4. Other Areas of Assistance**
Apart from health and nutrition, CARE has provided help in sectors such as:
*   Agriculture.
*   Medicine.
*   Education.
*   Emergency aid and long-term development assistance.
