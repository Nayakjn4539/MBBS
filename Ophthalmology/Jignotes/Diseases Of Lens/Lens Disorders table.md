### Lens Disorders and Diseases

> [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Cross-section of the eye showing lens anatomy (Capsule, Cortex, Nucleus) and various cataract morphologies (Cuneiform, Cupuliform, Nuclear Sclerosis)

#### I. Acquired Cataracts (Age-Related and Secondary)

| Disease / Defect | Clinical Features & Defect Description | Etiopathogenesis | Management / Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Senile [[Cortical Cataract]]** | **Cuneiform:** Wedge-shaped radial spokes from periphery to center (look black against red reflex).<br>**Cupuliform:** Posterior subcapsular opacity.<br>**Hydration:** Water clefts and vacuoles. | **Mechanism:** Decrease in proteins/amino acids + Increase in Na+ leads to hydration and coagulation of proteins.<br>Senile degeneration of cortical fibers. | **Surgery:** [[Phacoemulsification]] or SICS (Small Incision Cataract Surgery) with [[IOL Implantation]]. |
| **Senile [[Nuclear Cataract]]** | **Nuclear Sclerosis:** Hardening and yellowing/browning of the nucleus ([[Cataracta Brunescens]], Nigra, Rubra).<br>**"Second Sight":** Improvement in near vision due to myopic shift (Index Myopia). | **Mechanism:** Accumulation of insoluble proteins, pigments (urochrome/melanin), and dehydration/compaction of the nucleus. | **Surgery:** [[Phacoemulsification]] (Note: Nucleus is harder, requiring more energy). |
| **[[Morgagnian Cataract]]** | **Hypermature Cataract:** Cortex liquefies and becomes milky white.<br>**Sunken Nucleus:** Brownish nucleus settles at the bottom. | Long-standing mature cataract where cortical fibers disintegrate and liquefy. | **Surgery:** Urgent removal to prevent complications like [[Phacolytic Glaucoma]] or [[Phacoanaphylactic Uveitis]].<br>**Caution:** Risk of "Argentinian Flag Sign" (capsular tear) during capsulorhexis. |
| **[[Diabetic Cataract]]** | **Snowflake Cataract:** Bilateral, subcapsular, white punctate opacities (pathognomonic).<br>**Presenile Cataract:** Early onset nuclear sclerosis or cortical changes. | **Mechanism:** Hyperglycemia $\rightarrow$ saturation of hexokinase $\rightarrow$ activation of **Sorbitol Pathway** (Aldose Reductase) $\rightarrow$ accumulation of sorbitol $\rightarrow$ osmotic overhydration. | **Control:** Strict glycemic control (may reverse early hydration).<br>**Surgery:** [[Cataract Extraction]] (Risk of progression of diabetic retinopathy post-op). |
| **[[Complicated Cataract]]** | **Polychromatic Lustre:** Iridescent sheen at the posterior pole.<br>**Breadcrumb Appearance:** Posterior subcapsular opacities. | Secondary to ocular diseases: [[Uveitis]] (most common), [[Retinitis Pigmentosa]], High [[Myopia]], Glaucoma. Disturbance in lens nutrition. | **Treat Primary Cause:** Control uveitis (steroids).<br>**Surgery:** Under steroid cover; guarded prognosis due to retinal status. |
| **[[Traumatic Cataract]]** | **Rosette Cataract:** Flower-petal shaped opacity (usually posterior cortex/subcapsular).<br>**Vossius Ring:** Pigment imprint from iris on anterior lens capsule. | **Blunt Trauma:** Contusion injury.<br>**Penetrating Trauma:** Capsule rupture $\rightarrow$ aqueous inhibition $\rightarrow$ rapid opacification. | **Surgery:** Removal of cataract + IOL. Management of associated injuries (e.g., [[Hyphema]], [[Iridodialysis]]). |
| **[[Toxic Cataract]]** | **Steroid Induced:** Posterior Subcapsular Cataract (PSC).<br>**Miotics:** Anterior subcapsular vacuoles.<br>**Copper (Chalcosis):** Sunflower Cataract. | Long-term use of systemic/topical corticosteroids, miotics (echothiophate), amiodarone, chlorpromazine. | **Prevention:** Stop/reduce drug dosage.<br>**Surgery:** If vision is significantly impaired. |
| **[[Glassblower's Cataract]]** | **True Exfoliation:** Peeling of the anterior lens capsule.<br>Posterior polar opacity. | Prolonged exposure to **Infrared (Heat) Radiation**. | **Prevention:** Protective goggles.<br>**Surgery:** Cataract extraction. |
| **[[Electric Cataract]]** | Punctate subcapsular opacities maturing rapidly. | Passage of powerful electric current (lightning/high voltage). | **Surgery.** |
| **[[After Cataract]] (PCO)** | **Elschnig's Pearls:** Cluster of swollen epithelial cells.<br>**Soemmering's Ring:** Thick ring of cortical fibers behind iris. | Proliferation of residual lens epithelial cells on the posterior capsule after cataract surgery. | **Laser:** [[Nd:YAG Laser Capsulotomy]] (Photodisruption). |

**Clinical Implication:** In **Morgagnian Cataract**, the liquefied cortex can leak lens proteins causing **Phacolytic Glaucoma** (secondary open-angle glaucoma due to macrophage blockage of trabecular meshwork).

#### II. Metabolic and Syndromic Cataracts

| Disease / Defect | Specific Lens Defect | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Galactosemia]]** | **Oil Droplet Cataract:** Central refraction opacity. | **Deficiency:** GPUT (Galactose-1-phosphate uridyl transferase) or Galactokinase.<br>Accumulation of galactitol. | **Dietary:** Remove galactose/lactose (milk). Reversible if detected early.<br>**Surgery:** If irreversible opacity forms. |
| **[[Wilson's Disease]]** | **Sunflower Cataract:** Copper deposition under anterior/posterior capsule (Greenish/Reddish). | Inborn error of copper metabolism (Hepatolenticular degeneration). | **Chelation:** Penicillamine.<br>**Surgery:** If vision affected. |
| **[[Hypocalcemia]]** | Punctate subcapsular opacities or lamellar cataract. | **Hypoparathyroidism** / Tetany causing ionic imbalance. | **Medical:** Treat hypocalcemia (Calcium + Vit D).<br>**Surgery:** If cataract matures. |
| **[[Myotonic Dystrophy]]** | **Christmas Tree Cataract:** Polychromatic, iridescent crystals in cortex. | Autosomal dominant neuromuscular disorder. | **Surgery:** Standard cataract extraction. |
| **[[Lowe's Syndrome]]** | **Posterior Lenticonus:** Bulging of posterior lens surface. | Oculocerebrorenal syndrome (Amino acid metabolism error). | **Surgery:** Lensectomy (Pars Plana usually). |
| **[[Fabry's Disease]]** | Spoke-like cataract. | Deficiency of Alpha-galactosidase A (Glycolipidosis). | **Systemic:** Enzyme replacement.<br>**Ocular:** Usually no visual impairment. |

**Mnemonic:** Causes of **P**osterior **S**ubcapsular **C**ataract $\rightarrow$ **CUP** (Complicated, Uveitis, Pigmentosa-Retinitis).

#### III. Congenital and Developmental Cataracts

| Type | Morphology / Defect | Etiology | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Blue Dot Cataract]]** | **Cataracta Punctata Cerulea:** Multiple bluish dots in peripheral nucleus/cortex. | Most common congenital cataract. Usually stationary/innocuous. | **None:** Usually does not affect vision significantly. |
| **[[Zonular Cataract]]** | **Lamellar:** Opacity affecting a specific zone (fetal nucleus).<br>**Riders:** Spokes running outwards. | Malnutrition (Vit D), Hypocalcemia, Maternal Rubella (7th-8th week). | **Surgery:** If visually significant (danger of amblyopia). |
| **[[Rubella Cataract]]** | Pearly white nuclear cataract. | Maternal Rubella infection (TORCH). | **Surgery:** Lensectomy (Virus may persist in lens for years). |
| **[[Coronary Cataract]]** | Club-shaped opacities in the periphery (corona) appearing at puberty. | Developmental (Adolescence). | **None:** Peripheral, vision usually unaffected. |
| **[[Anterior Polar Cataract]]** | Pyramidal or flat opacity at anterior pole. | Delayed formation of anterior chamber; adherent leukoma. | **Observation:** Usually stationary. |
| **[[Posterior Polar Cataract]]** | Onion-ring/whorled opacity at posterior pole. | Persistent hyaloid artery remnants ([[Mittendorf Dot]]). | **Surgery:** High risk of **Posterior Capsular Rupture (PCR)** during surgery. |

#### IV. Congenital Anomalies of Shape and Position

| Defect | Description | Associations / Etiology | Management |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Ectopia Lentis]] (Subluxation)** | Displacement of lens (partial).<br>**Superotemporal:** [[Marfan's Syndrome]].<br>**Inferonasal:** [[Homocystinuria]]. | Weakness/absence of zonules.<br>Trauma (most common cause).<br>Weill-Marchesani (Anterior/Inferior). | **Optical:** Spectacles/Contact lens (aphakic or phakic correction).<br>**Surgery:** If in AC, causing glaucoma, or uncorrectable vision. |
| **[[Microspherophakia]]** | Small, spherical lens. Increased refractive power (Myopia). | [[Weill-Marchesani Syndrome]], Marfan's, Alport's. | **Glaucoma Mx:** Prone to inverse glaucoma (pupillary block). Mydriatics (not miotics) used for block. |
| **[[Lenticonus]]** | Conical projection of lens surface. | **Anterior:** [[Alport's Syndrome]].<br>**Posterior:** [[Lowe's Syndrome]]. | **Surgery:** If visual axis distorted significantly. |
| **[[Coloboma of Lens]]** | Notch-shaped defect (usually inferior). | Defective development of zonules (flattening of equator). | **None** or Cosmetic contact lens if glare present. |
| **[[Aphakia]]** | Absence of crystalline lens. | Surgical (Post-cataract), Traumatic extrusion, Congenital (Rare). | **Correction:** [[IOL]] (Best), Contact Lens, or High plus spectacles (+10D approx). |

**Clinical Implication:** In **Homocystinuria**, patients are prone to thromboembolism; general anesthesia poses high risk. In **Marfan's**, look for aortic aneurysm/dilation.
