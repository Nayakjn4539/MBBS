### Primary Health Centre (PHC)

The [[Primary Health Centre]] is the basic structural unit of the public health services in developing countries like India. It acts as the **first contact point** between the village community and the Medical Officer.

#### Functions of Primary Health Centre (PHC)
The functions of the PHC cover preventive, promotive, curative, and rehabilitative aspects of health care:

1.  **Medical Care**:
    *   OPD services: 4 hours in the morning and 2 hours in the afternoon/evening.
    *   24-hour emergency services.
    *   In-patient services: Provision of **4–6 beds**.
    *   Referral services to [[Community Health Centre]] (CHC) or District Hospital.

2.  **Maternal and Child Health (MCH) & Family Planning**:
    *   **Antenatal Care:** Early registration, minimum 3-4 checkups, high-risk pregnancy identification.
    *   **Intranatal Care:** 24-hour delivery services (normal and assisted).
    *   **Postnatal Care:** Minimum 2 home visits and 48-hour stay after delivery.
    *   **Newborn Care:** Essential newborn care and resuscitation.
    *   **Family Planning:** Counseling, provision of contraceptives (OCPs, Condoms, IUDs), and permanent methods like [[Tubectomy]] and [[Vasectomy]].

3.  **Prevention and Control of Locally Endemic Diseases**:
    *   Surveillance and management of diseases like [[Malaria]], [[Kala-azar]], Japanese Encephalitis, and Dengue.
    *   Active participation in the **National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme** ([[NVBDCP]]).

4.  **Implementation of National Health Programmes**:
    *   **RNTCP (NTEP):** Function as a Designated Microscopy Centre (DMC) and provide [[DOTS]].
    *   **NLEP:** Diagnosis and management of Leprosy.
    *   **NACP:** Screening, referral to ICTC, and syndromic management of STIs/RTIs.
    *   **IDSP:** Weekly reporting of epidemic-prone diseases.

5.  **Basic Laboratory Services**:
    *   Routine blood tests (Hb, grouping), Urine analysis (albumin, sugar), Sputum microscopy for TB, Peripheral smear for Malaria, Rapid diagnostic tests for pregnancy and Typhoid.

6.  **Health Education**:
    *   Information, Education, and Communication (IEC) activities regarding hygiene, nutrition, and prevention of communicable and non-communicable diseases.

7.  **School Health Services**:
    *   Regular check-ups, treatment of minor ailments, and deworming.

8.  **Training of Health Personnel**:
    *   The Medical Officer trains the Health Assistants, Health Workers (Male/Female), [[ASHA]], and Traditional Birth Attendants (Dais).

9.  **Collection and Reporting of Vital Statistics**:
    *   Recording births, deaths, and reporting to higher authorities.

10. **Water Supply and Sanitation**:
    *   Testing water quality, disinfection of water sources, and promotion of sanitary latrines.

11. **Nutritional Services**:
    *   Coordination with [[ICDS]] (Integrated Child Development Services).
    *   Vitamin A prophylaxis and Iron Folic Acid supplementation.

12. **Mainstreaming of AYUSH**:
    *   Provision of Ayush services alongside allopathic medicine.

**Mnemonic for PHC Functions: "ELEMENTS"**
*   **E**ducation regarding health problems.
*   **L**ocally endemic disease control.
*   **E**xpanded Programme on Immunization ([[Universal Immunization Programme]]).
*   **M**aternal and Child Health Care.
*   **E**ssential Drugs provision.
*   **N**utrition promotion.
*   **T**reatment of common diseases/injuries.
*   **S**afe water and sanitation.

### Sub-Centre (SC)

The [[Sub-Centre]] is the **most peripheral** and first contact point between the primary health care system and the community. It is manned by one **Health Worker Female (ANM)** and one **Health Worker Male**.

#### Services Provided by Sub-Centre
The services are classified into **Essential (Minimum Assured)** and Desirable categories under Indian Public Health Standards (IPHS).

**1. Maternal and Child Health:**
*   **Antenatal:** Early registration (within 12 weeks), minimum 4 ANC checkups, Hb estimation, urine test for albumin/sugar, and administration of TT/Td injections and [[IFA tablets]].
*   **Intranatal:** Promotion of institutional deliveries. Skilled attendance at home deliveries if necessary.
*   **Postnatal:** Minimum 2 postpartum home visits (within 48 hrs and 7th day).
*   **Child Health:** Promotion of exclusive breastfeeding, complementary feeding, and assessment of growth.

**2. Immunization:**
*   Full immunization of all infants and children against vaccine-preventable diseases.
*   Vitamin A prophylaxis.

**3. Family Planning and Contraception:**
*   Education and motivation for family planning.
*   Distribution of oral contraceptive pills, condoms, and emergency contraceptives.
*   [[IUCD]] insertion (if trained) and follow-up.

**4. Prevention and Control of Diseases:**
*   **Malaria:** Collection of blood slides/RDT from fever cases.
*   **Tuberculosis:** Referral of suspected cases; acting as a DOTS provider.
*   **Leprosy:** Referral of suspected cases; follow-up of MDT.
*   **Minor Ailments:** Treatment of fever, diarrhea (ORS distribution), ARI, and worm infestation.

**5. Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs):**
*   Opportunistic screening for Hypertension and Diabetes (using strips/glucometer) for individuals >30 years.
*   Counseling for lifestyle modifications.

**6. Water and Sanitation:**
*   Disinfection of drinking water sources (chlorination).
*   Promotion of sanitation and household toilets.

**7. Vital Events Recording:**
*   Maintenance of Birth and Death registers.

**8. Community Needs Assessment:**
*   Updating the eligible couple register and child register.

**Comparison of Norms**

| Feature | Primary Health Centre (PHC) | Sub-Centre (SC) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level of Care** | Primary | Primary (Peripheral) |
| **Population (Plain)** | 30,000 | 5,000 |
| **Population (Hilly/Tribal)** | 20,000 | 3,000 |
| **Key Staff** | Medical Officer, Pharmacist, Lab Tech, Nurse | ANM (HW-F), HW-M |
| **Bed Strength** | 4 – 6 Beds | None (Observation only) |
| **Funding** | State Government | 100% Central Assistance |

***

**Clinical Relevance:**
The Medical Officer at the PHC plays a dual role: a **clinician** treating patients and a **manager** supervising the health workers at the Sub-Centres. Understanding the functions of the SC is vital for the MO to effectively monitor public health metrics like Immunization coverage and ANC registration in the block.
