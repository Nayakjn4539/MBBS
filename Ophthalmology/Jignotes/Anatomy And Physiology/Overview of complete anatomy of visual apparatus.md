# [[Anatomy And Physiology]] > Overview of complete anatomy of visual apparatus

# Anatomy of the Visual Apparatus

> [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Cross-section of the eyeball showing coats, segments, and chambers

### 1. The Eyeball (Globe)
The eyeball is the primary organ of vision, situated in the anterior part of the bony [[orbit]]. It is a cystic structure kept distended by intraocular pressure.

**General Dimensions:**
*   **Shape:** Aspherical (Oblate Spheroid) - consisting of two modified spheres fused together (anterior smaller sphere is cornea; posterior larger sphere is sclera).
*   **Axial Length (Anteroposterior diameter):** 24 mm (Average).
*   **Horizontal Diameter:** 23.5 mm.
*   **Vertical Diameter:** 23 mm.
*   **Volume:** 6.5 ml.
*   **Weight:** 7 gm.

**Segments and Chambers:**
The lens and iris divide the eye into two segments:
1.  **Anterior Segment:** Includes structures anterior to the [[crystalline lens]].
    *   **Anterior Chamber (AC):** Bounded anteriorly by the cornea and posteriorly by the iris. Depth is approx. 2.5mm in the center. Contains [[aqueous humour]] (0.25 ml).
    *   **Posterior Chamber (PC):** Triangular space bounded anteriorly by the iris and posteriorly by the lens and zonules. Contains aqueous humour (0.06 ml).
2.  **Posterior Segment:** Structures posterior to the lens.
    *   Contains [[vitreous humour]], [[retina]], [[choroid]], and [[optic disc]].

### 2. Coats of the Eyeball
The wall of the eyeball consists of three concentric distinct coats (tunics).

#### A. Fibrous Coat (Outer Layer)
This is the dense, protective outer wall.
*   **[[Cornea]] (Anterior 1/6th):**
    *   Transparent, avascular structure responsible for major refraction (+43D to +45D).
    *   **Layers:** Epithelium, [[Bowman's membrane]], Stroma (90% thickness), [[Dua's layer]] (Pre-Descemet's), [[Descemet's membrane]], Endothelium.
    *   **Nutrition:** via Aqueous humour, limbal capillaries, and tear film.
*   **[[Sclera]] (Posterior 5/6th):**
    *   Opaque, white, fibrous coat. Provides structural integrity.
    *   Thinnest posterior to rectus muscle insertions (0.3mm) and thickest at the posterior pole (1mm).
    *   **Limbus:** The junction where the cornea and sclera meet. Contains stem cells (Palisades of Vogt).

#### B. Vascular Coat (Middle Layer / Uveal Tract)
Highly vascular layer responsible for nutrition.
*   **[[Iris]]:**
    *   Anterior-most part forming a diaphragm with a central aperture ([[pupil]]).
    *   **Muscles:**
        *   **[[Sphincter pupillae]]:** Circular muscle, causes constriction (Miosis), supplied by Parasympathetic (CN III).
        *   **[[Dilator pupillae]]:** Radial muscle, causes dilatation (Mydriasis), supplied by Sympathetic nerves.
*   **[[Ciliary Body]]:**
    *   **Pars Plicata (Anterior):** Contains ciliary processes that secrete aqueous humour.
    *   **Pars Plana (Posterior):** Relatively avascular zone; safe site for surgical entry into vitreous.
    *   **[[Ciliary Muscle]]:** Responsible for [[accommodation]].
*   **[[Choroid]]:**
    *   Posterior-most part, lies between sclera and retina. Highly vascular and pigmented. Supplies nutrition to outer layers of the retina.

#### C. Nervous Coat (Inner Layer)
*   **[[Retina]]:**
    *   Transparent, neurosensory membrane. Extends from [[optic disc]] to [[ora serrata]].
    *   **Layers:** Consists of 10 layers. From outside in: Pigment Epithelium $\rightarrow$ Rods and Cones $\rightarrow$ External Limiting Membrane $\rightarrow$ Outer Nuclear Layer $\rightarrow$ Outer Plexiform Layer $\rightarrow$ Inner Nuclear Layer $\rightarrow$ Inner Plexiform Layer $\rightarrow$ Ganglion Cell Layer $\rightarrow$ Nerve Fiber Layer $\rightarrow$ Internal Limiting Membrane.
    *   **[[Macula Lutea]]:** Area of central vision at the posterior pole.
    *   **[[Fovea Centralis]]:** Central depression in the macula. Contains only cones. Site of highest visual acuity.
    *   **[[Photoreceptors]]:**
        *   **Rods:** Scotopic (night) vision, peripheral vision.
        *   **Cones:** Photopic (day) vision, color vision, central vision.

### 3. Refractive Media
Structures responsible for transmitting and focusing light onto the retina.
1.  **[[Cornea]]:** Main refracting surface.
2.  **[[Aqueous Humour]]:** Clear fluid in AC and PC. Produced by ciliary processes. Drains via [[trabecular meshwork]] and [[uveoscleral outflow]].
3.  **[[Crystalline Lens]]:**
    *   Biconvex, transparent, avascular structure.
    *   Suspended by [[zonules of Zinn]].
    *   Structure: Capsule, Epithelium (anterior only), Cortex, Nucleus.
    *   Function: Accommodation (variable refractive power).
4.  **[[Vitreous Humour]]:**
    *   Transparent gel filling the posterior segment (80% of eye volume).
    *   Composition: 99% water, collagen type II, hyaluronic acid.

### 4. Protective Apparatus (Adnexa)

**[[Eyelids]]:**
*   Mobile tissue curtains protecting the eye.
*   **Layers:** Skin, Subcutaneous tissue, Muscles (Orbicularis oculi, Levator Palpebrae Superioris), Tarsal plate (skeleton of lid), Conjunctiva.
*   **Glands:** [[Meibomian glands]] (oil), Glands of Zeis (sebaceous), Glands of Moll (sweat).

**[[Conjunctiva]]:**
*   Translucent mucous membrane lining the eyelids and covering the sclera.
*   **Parts:** Palpebral (lines lids), Bulbar (covers sclera), Forniceal (junction).
*   Contains **[[Goblet cells]]** which secrete mucin for the tear film.

**[[Lacrimal Apparatus]]:**
*   **Secretory System:**
    *   Main Lacrimal Gland: Located in the superolateral orbit. Reflex secretion.
    *   Accessory Glands (Krause & Wolfring): Basal secretion.
*   **Drainage System:**
    *   Puncta $\rightarrow$ Canaliculi $\rightarrow$ Common Canaliculus $\rightarrow$ [[Lacrimal Sac]] $\rightarrow$ [[Nasolacrimal Duct]] (opens into inferior meatus of nose).

### 5. Motor Apparatus (Extraocular Muscles)

> [!warning] Diagram Alert
> Spiral of Tillaux showing rectus muscle insertions

The eye is moved by 6 extraocular muscles originating mostly from the [[Annulus of Zinn]] at the orbital apex.

| Muscle | Nerve Supply | Primary Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Lateral Rectus]] (LR)** | CN VI (Abducent) | Abduction |
| **[[Medial Rectus]] (MR)** | CN III (Oculomotor) | Adduction |
| **[[Superior Rectus]] (SR)** | CN III (Oculomotor) | Elevation |
| **[[Inferior Rectus]] (IR)** | CN III (Oculomotor) | Depression |
| **[[Superior Oblique]] (SO)** | CN IV (Trochlear) | Intorsion (Depression in adduction) |
| **[[Inferior Oblique]] (IO)** | CN III (Oculomotor) | Extorsion (Elevation in adduction) |

*   **Mnemonic for Nerve Supply:** **LR6 SO4** (Lateral Rectus by 6th, Superior Oblique by 4th, rest by 3rd).

### 6. The Visual Pathway (Neural Apparatus)
The route of visual impulses from the retina to the brain.

1.  **[[Optic Nerve]] (CN II):** Formed by axons of retinal ganglion cells. Divided into Intraocular, Intraorbital, Intracanalicular, and Intracranial parts.
2.  **[[Optic Chiasm]]:** Decussation (crossing) of nasal retinal fibers. Located above the pituitary gland.
3.  **[[Optic Tract]]:** Carries fibers from the ipsilateral temporal retina and contralateral nasal retina.
4.  **[[Lateral Geniculate Body]] (LGB):** Relay center in the thalamus.
5.  **[[Optic Radiations]]:** Geniculocalcarine tract passing through temporal and parietal lobes.
6.  **[[Visual Cortex]]:** Occipital lobe (Area 17, Striate cortex) where image is perceived.

---

### 7. Vascular Supply

**Arterial Supply:**
*   Main source: **[[Ophthalmic Artery]]** (branch of Internal Carotid Artery).
*   **Branches:**
    *   [[Central Retinal Artery]] (CRA): Supplies inner retina.
    *   Short Posterior Ciliary Arteries (SPCA): Supply choroid and optic disc.
    *   Long Posterior Ciliary Arteries (LPCA) & Anterior Ciliary Arteries: Supply iris and ciliary body (form Major Arterial Circle of Iris).

**Venous Drainage:**
*   Retinal blood drains via Central Retinal Vein.
*   Uveal blood drains via 4 **[[Vortex Veins]]**.
*   Ultimately drain into Superior and Inferior Ophthalmic Veins $\rightarrow$ [[Cavernous Sinus]].

---

### Clinical Implications
*   **Glaucoma:** Blockage of aqueous outflow at the angle of the anterior chamber leads to raised IOP and optic nerve damage.
*   **Cataract:** Opacification of the crystalline lens.
*   **Retinal Detachment:** Separation of the neurosensory retina from the pigment epithelium.
*   **Papilledema:** Swelling of the optic disc due to raised intracranial pressure (transmitted via subarachnoid space around the optic nerve).
