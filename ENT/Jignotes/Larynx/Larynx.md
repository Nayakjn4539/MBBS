# The Larynx

## 1. Embryology and Development
*   **Origin:** The larynx develops from the **4th and 6th [[Branchial Arches]]**.
    *   **Laryngeal Lining:** Derived from endoderm of the cephalic part of the foregut,.
    *   **Cartilages:** Derived from mesenchyme.
        *   **Thyroid Cartilage:** 4th arch.
        *   **Cricoid, Arytenoid, Corniculate, Cuneiform:** 6th arch.
        *   **Epiglottis:** Develops from the **[[Hypobranchial Eminence]]** (caudal part of the 4th arch),.
*   **Muscles:**
    *   **Cricothyroid:** 4th arch (supplied by [[Superior Laryngeal Nerve]]).
    *   **All intrinsic muscles:** 6th arch (supplied by [[Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve]]),.
*   **Timeline:** Laryngeal development begins during the 4th week intrauterine. By the 10th week, the laryngeal lumen recanalizes; failure of this leads to [[Laryngeal Web]] or atresia,.

**Clinical Implication:**
*   **Infant Larynx:** Positioned higher (C2-C3) compared to adults (C3-C6). The epiglottis is omega-shaped ($\Omega$), and the cartilages are soft. The **[[Subglottis]]** is the narrowest part in infants, whereas the glottis is the narrowest in adults,,.

## 2. Anatomy
**Location:** Midline of the neck, extending from the root of the tongue to the trachea. In adults, it lies opposite C3–C6 vertebrae.

### A. Skeletal Framework
Composed of 9 cartilages (3 Unpaired, 3 Paired).

| Type | Cartilages | Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Unpaired** | **[[Thyroid Cartilage]]** | Largest; shield-shaped. Forms "Adam's Apple" (angle 90° in males, 120° in females). |
| | **[[Cricoid Cartilage]]** | Signet-ring shaped. The only complete cartilaginous ring in the airway. **Clinical:** Pressure here (Sellick’s maneuver) prevents regurgitation,. |
| | **[[Epiglottis]]** | Leaf-shaped elastic cartilage. Protects airway during swallowing. |
| **Paired** | **[[Arytenoid Cartilage]]** | Pyramidal. Sits on cricoid. Has *vocal process* (anterior) and *muscular process* (lateral). |
| | **[[Corniculate Cartilage]]** | (Santorini) Sits on apex of arytenoids. |
| | **[[Cuneiform Cartilage]]** | (Wrisberg) Located in the **[[Aryepiglottic Fold]]**. |

### B. Laryngeal Joints
1.  **Cricothyroid Joint:** Rotatory movement. Changes tension of vocal cords.
2.  **Cricoarytenoid Joint:** Saddle joint. Allows rotatory and gliding movements (abduction/adduction of cords),.

### C. Laryngeal Cavity (Subdivisions)
Divided by vestibular folds (false cords) and vocal folds (true cords),.

1.  **[[Supraglottis]]**:
    *   Extends from the laryngeal inlet to the ventricle.
    *   Structures: Epiglottis, Aryepiglottic folds, Arytenoids, False vocal cords, Ventricle.
2.  **[[Glottis]]**:
    *   The space between the true vocal cords (Rima Glottidis).
    *   Includes: True vocal cords, Anterior commissure, Posterior commissure.
3.  **[[Subglottis]]**:
    *   Extends from 1 cm below the true vocal cords to the lower border of the cricoid cartilage.

### D. Important Spaces
*   **[[Pre-epiglottic Space]] (Space of Boyer):** Anterior to epiglottis, bounded by thyrohyoid membrane and hyoid bone. **Clinical:** Route for cancer spread.
*   **[[Paraglottic Space]]:** Potential space laterally between thyroid cartilage and mucosa.
*   **[[Reinke's Space]]:** Superficial layer of lamina propria under the vocal cord epithelium. Poor lymphatic drainage. **Clinical:** Site for **[[Reinke's Edema]]**.

### E. Muscles of Larynx
**Intrinsic Muscles:** Responsible for sound production and airway protection,,.

| Function | Muscles | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Abductor** | **[[Posterior Cricoarytenoid]] (PCA)** | *Safety muscle of larynx*. Opens the glottis. |
| **Adductor** | **[[Lateral Cricoarytenoid]] (LCA)** | Closes the glottis. |
| | **Interarytenoid** (Transverse/Oblique) | Closes posterior commissure. |
| | **[[Thyroarytenoid]]** | Adducts. |
| **Tensor** | **[[Cricothyroid]]** | Lengthens and tenses cords (increases pitch). Only intrinsic muscle outside the larynx,. |
| | **Vocalis** | Internal part of Thyroarytenoid. Increases tension. |
| **Relaxer** | **Thyroarytenoid** | Shortens and relaxes cords. |

**Extrinsic Muscles:** Elevators (Digastric, Stylohyoid, Mylohyoid) and Depressors (Sternothyroid, Omohyoid) of the entire larynx,.

### F. Nerve Supply
Supplied by the **[[Vagus Nerve]] (CN X)**,,.

1.  **[[Superior Laryngeal Nerve]] (SLN):**
    *   **Internal Branch:** Sensory to Supraglottis (above vocal cords). **Clinical:** Loss causes aspiration due to loss of cough reflex.
    *   **External Branch:** Motor to **[[Cricothyroid Muscle]]**.
2.  **[[Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve]] (RLN):**
    *   **Motor:** Supplies **ALL** intrinsic muscles *except* Cricothyroid.
    *   **Sensory:** Sensory to Subglottis (below vocal cords).

### G. Lymphatic Drainage
*   **Supraglottis:** Rich drainage. Drains to Upper Deep Cervical nodes (Level II, III). High risk of metastasis,.
*   **Glottis:** **No lymphatics** (Watershed area). Carcinoma rarely metastasizes early,.
*   **Subglottis:** Drains to Prelaryngeal (Delphian), Pretracheal, and Lower Deep Cervical nodes,.

## 3. Physiology
1.  **Protection of Lower Airways:** The primary function. Sphincteric closure at three levels: Aryepiglottic folds, False cords, and True cords. Cessation of respiration during swallowing,.
2.  **Phonation:** **Aerodynamic Myoelastic Theory**. Subglottic pressure builds up below adducted cords, forcing them open; elasticity and Bernoulli effect bring them back together, creating vibration.
3.  **Respiration:** Vocal cords abduct during inspiration.
4.  **Fixation of Chest:** Adduction of cords fixes intrathoracic pressure to assist in lifting heavy weights, defecation, and childbirth,.

---

## 4. Clinical Examination
*   **[[Indirect Laryngoscopy]] (IDL):**
    *   Patient seated, tongue protruded and held with gauze. Laryngeal mirror (warmed) placed against uvula.
    *   **Structures seen:** Base of tongue, vallecula, epiglottis, aryepiglottic folds, arytenoids, pyriform fossae, ventricular bands, vocal cords, anterior commissure (often difficult to see),.
    *   **Positions:** "Eee" for adduction/tension; deep breath for abduction.
*   **[[Direct Laryngoscopy]] (DLS):**
    *   Done under GA. **Boyce Position** (Flexion at cervical spine, Extension at atlanto-occipital joint – "Sniffing the morning air").
    *   Used for biopsy, foreign body removal, and microsurgery,.
*   **Stroboscopy:** For visualizing mucosal waves.

## 5. Congenital Lesions
*   **[[Laryngomalacia]] (Congenital Laryngeal Stridor):**
    *   **Defect:** Excessive flaccidity of supraglottic larynx (omega-shaped epiglottis, floppy aryepiglottic folds).
    *   **Signs:** Inspiratory stridor (worsens on crying/supine, improves on prone). Normal cry.
    *   **Management:** Usually conservative (resolves by 2 years). Surgery (Supraglottoplasty) if severe,,.
*   **Congenital Subglottic Stenosis:** Diameter <4mm in full-term, <3mm in preterm. Causes biphasic stridor..
*   **Laryngeal Web:** Incomplete recanalization. Weak cry/aphonia at birth. Tx: Surgical division + Keel placement,.

## 6. Infections and Inflammations
### Acute
*   **[[Acute Epiglottitis]]**:
    *   **Organism:** *H. influenzae* type B.
    *   **Signs:** High fever, drooling, "Hot potato" voice, inspiratory stridor, tripod position.
    *   **X-ray:** **Thumb Sign**.
    *   **Management:** Secure airway (Intubation/Tracheostomy), IV Antibiotics (Ceftriaxone). **Contraindication:** Do NOT examine throat with tongue depressor (risk of laryngospasm),.
*   **[[Laryngotracheobronchitis]] (Croup):**
    *   **Organism:** Parainfluenza virus.
    *   **Signs:** Barking cough, hoarseness, biphasic stridor.
    *   **X-ray:** **Steeple Sign** (subglottic narrowing).
    *   **Management:** Humidification, Steroids, Nebulized Adrenaline,.

### Chronic
*   **[[Vocal Nodules]] (Singer's/Teacher's Nodules):**
    *   **Cause:** Voice abuse.
    *   **Site:** Junction of anterior 1/3 and posterior 2/3 of vocal cords. Bilateral and symmetrical.
    *   **Treatment:** Voice rest, Speech therapy. Microsurgery if refractory,.
*   **[[Vocal Polyp]]:**
    *   **Cause:** Sudden shouting/trauma. Unilateral.
    *   **Treatment:** Surgical excision.
*   **[[Reinke's Edema]]:**
    *   **Cause:** Smoking. Bilateral diffuse polyposis (fluid in Reinke's space).
    *   **Treatment:** Smoking cessation, Decortication,.

## 7. Neurological Disorders ([[Vocal Cord Palsy]])
### Unilateral Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve Palsy
*   **Cause:** Thyroid surgery, trauma, malignancy.
*   **Position:** Paramedian.
*   **Symptoms:** Hoarseness (healthy cord compensates over time).
*   **Management:** Conservative/Speech therapy. Injection thyroplasty if no compensation,.

### Bilateral Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve Palsy
*   **Cause:** Thyroidectomy (Total).
*   **Position:** Median/Paramedian.
*   **Symptoms:** **Stridor/Dyspnea** (Airway compromised, voice is usually good).
*   **Management:** Tracheostomy (immediate), Cord lateralization/Arytenoidectomy (later),.

### Superior Laryngeal Nerve Palsy
*   **Defect:** Cricothyroid paralysis.
*   **Signs:** Wavy cord, loss of pitch variation, voice fatigue.
*   **Management:** Speech therapy.

**Positions of Vocal Cords:**
*   **Median:** Midline (Phonation).
*   **Paramedian:** 1.5mm from midline (Unilateral RLN palsy).
*   **Cadaveric (Intermediate):** 3.5mm from midline (Combined RLN + SLN palsy).

## 8. Tumors of Larynx
### Benign
*   **[[Laryngeal Papillomatosis]] (RRP):**
    *   **Cause:** HPV 6 & 11.
    *   **Juvenile Onset:** Multiple, aggressive, risk of airway obstruction.
    *   **Adult Onset:** Solitary, less aggressive.
    *   **Treatment:** Microdebrider excision, CO2 Laser. Avoid radiation (malignant transformation risk),,.

### Malignant ([[Carcinoma Larynx]])
*   **Type:** Squamous Cell Carcinoma (>95%).
*   **Risk Factors:** Smoking, Alcohol.
*   **Subtypes:**
    1.  **Glottic:** Most common. Presents early with **Hoarseness**. Late lymphatic spread. Best prognosis,.
    2.  **Supraglottic:** Presents with dysphagia/throat pain/neck node. Rich lymphatics (early metastasis).
    3.  **Subglottic:** Presents with stridor. Poor prognosis.
*   **TNM Staging (Simplified):**
    *   **Tis:** Carcinoma in situ.
    *   **T1:** Limited to one subsite, normal cord mobility.
    *   **T2:** Extends to adjacent site, impaired mobility.
    *   **T3:** Limited to larynx, **Fixed vocal cord**.
    *   **T4a:** Invades thyroid cartilage/extra-laryngeal tissues,.
*   **Treatment:**
    *   Early (T1/T2): Radiotherapy (preserves voice) or Transoral Laser Microsurgery.
    *   Advanced (T3/T4): Total Laryngectomy +/- Radiotherapy. Organ preservation (Chemo-radiation) for T3,.
