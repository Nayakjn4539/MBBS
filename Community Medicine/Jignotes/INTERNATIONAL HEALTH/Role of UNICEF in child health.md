# [[INTERNATIONAL HEALTH]] > Role of UNICEF in child health

### **[[UNICEF]] (United Nations International Children’s Emergency Fund)**

**Headquarters:** New York, USA
**Regional Office for South East Asia:** New Delhi, India
**Focus:** Shifted from "Emergency" relief (post-WWII) to the **"Whole Child" concept**, ensuring long-term personal development and protection of children,.

---

### **1. The "GOBI-FFF" Strategy (Child Health Revolution)**
UNICEF promotes a low-cost, high-impact strategy known as the GOBI campaign to reduce infant and child mortality,.

| Component | Meaning | Clinical/Public Health Relevance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **G** | **G**rowth Monitoring | Use of [[Growth Charts]] to detect malnutrition early (Growth faltering is the first sign of PEM). |
| **O** | **O**ral Rehydration | Use of [[ORS]] to treat mild/moderate dehydration due to diarrhea (major killer of under-5s). |
| **B** | **B**reastfeeding | Promotion of exclusive breastfeeding (0-6 months) to prevent infection and malnutrition. |
| **I** | **I**mmunization | Protection against the "Big Six" (TB, Polio, Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Measles). |
| **F** | **F**emale Education | Educated mothers have healthier children with better hygiene and nutrition. |
| **F** | **F**amily Spacing | Birth spacing improves maternal health and child survival rates. |
| **F** | **F**ood Supplements | Targeted at pregnant women and beneficiaries of [[ICDS]] to prevent LBW and malnutrition. |

> **Mnemonic:** **GO BIFF** (Go be strong!) - **G**rowth, **O**RS, **B**reastfeeding, **I**mmunization, **F**emale ed, **F**amily spacing, **F**ood.

### **2. Role in Child Health**
*   **Vaccine Support:** UNICEF has provided massive aid for the production of vaccines and sera. It supported India’s [[BCG]] vaccination program from its inception,.
*   **Infrastructure:** Donated plants for the manufacture of Triple Vaccine (DPT), Penicillin, and Iodized Salt.
*   **Primary Health Care (PHC):** Works closely with [[WHO]] to support PHC delivery at the village level, emphasizing immunization and infant care.
*   **Cold Chain:** UNICEF is a key partner in strengthening the **Cold Chain** system (refrigerators, cold boxes) essential for the [[Universal Immunization Programme]].

---

### **3. Role in Child Nutrition**
*   **Applied Nutrition Programme (ANP):** Collaborated with FAO and WHO to help rural populations grow and consume protective foods.
*   **Micronutrient Supplementation:**
    *   **Vitamin A:** Supports the administration of massive doses (200,000 IU) to prevent nutritional blindness ([[Xerophthalmia]]).
    *   **Iodine:** Supports the enrichment of salt with iodine to control [[Endemic Goitre]].
    *   **Iron/Folic Acid:** Provision of supplements to combat anemia in mothers and children.
*   **Dairy Development:** Supplied equipment for modern dairy plants (e.g., in Anand, Mumbai) to ensure milk availability.

---

### **4. Water and Sanitation (WASH)**
*   **Safe Water:** Heavily involved in environmental sanitation programs, specifically providing safe drinking water in rural areas to reduce water-borne diseases like diarrhea and cholera.
*   **Sanitation:** Promotes hygiene education and sanitation facilities in schools and communities.

---

### **5. Education and Social Welfare**
*   **Science Education:** In collaboration with UNESCO, assists in improving science teaching in schools by providing laboratory equipment, books, and AV aids.
*   **Family Welfare:** Supports day-care centers, mothers' clubs, and parental education to improve care within the home.
*   **Urban Basic Services (UBS):** Focuses on upgrading basic services (health, nutrition, water) for children and women in urban slums.

---

### **6. Rights of the Child**
*   UNICEF has moved beyond disease control to advocacy for the **Rights of the Child** (survival, protection, and development).
*   It focuses on children in difficult circumstances, including victims of war, disaster, and exploitation.

---

### **Clinical Relevance**
*   **Baby Friendly Hospital Initiative (BFHI):** UNICEF, along with WHO, promotes the [[BFHI]] to ensure successful breastfeeding practices in healthcare facilities.
*   **Emergency Relief:** In times of war or natural disasters (earthquakes, floods), UNICEF mobilizes immediate relief (milk, medicines, clothes) for affected children.
