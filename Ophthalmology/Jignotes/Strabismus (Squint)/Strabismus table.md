# [[Strabismus (Squint)]] > Strabismus table

### Classification of Strabismus (Squint)

[[Strabismus]] is the misalignment of the visual axes of the two eyes. It is broadly classified into **Apparent (Pseudo)**, **Latent (Phoria)**, and **Manifest (Tropia)**. Manifest squint is further divided into **Comitant** (deviation constant in all gazes) and **Incomitant** (deviation varies).

#### 1. Pseudostrabismus & Latent Strabismus

| Type | Subtype | Defect / Clinical Features | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Pseudostrabismus]]**<br>(Apparent Squint) | **Pseudo-esotropia** | Eyes appear crossed (convergent) but visual axes are parallel.<br>[[Hirschberg test]]: Central corneal reflex. | • **Prominent epicanthal folds** (covers nasal sclera).<br>• Flat/broad nasal bridge.<br>• Small interpupillary distance (IPD).<br>• Negative **[[Angle Kappa]]**. | **No Treatment required.**<br>• Reassurance.<br>• Correction of facial anomaly if severe (cosmetic). |
| | **Pseudo-exotropia** | Eyes appear divergent but axes are aligned. | • **Hypertelorism** (wide IPD).<br>• Positive **[[Angle Kappa]]** (fovea is temporal to optical axis). | **No Treatment.** |
| **[[Heterophoria]]**<br>(Latent Squint) | **Esophoria** | Tendency of eyes to deviate inward.<br>Maintained by **[[Fusion]]**.<br>Manifests on **Cover-Uncover test** or fatigue. | • Convergence excess.<br>• Divergence weakness.<br>• Uncorrected [[Hypermetropia]]. | • Treat refractive error.<br>• **Orthoptic exercises** (improve fusional reserve).<br>• Base-out **[[Prisms]]** (rarely). |
| | **Exophoria** | Tendency to deviate outward.<br>Common in presbyopes/myopes. | • Convergence insufficiency.<br>• Divergence excess. | • **Pencil push-ups** (Convergence exercises).<br>• Base-in prisms.<br>• Surgery (if decompensated/large). |

#### 2. Comitant Strabismus (Manifest/Heterotropia)
*Deviation remains constant in all directions of gaze. Ocular movements are full.*

| Type | Subtype | Defect / Clinical Features | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Esotropia]]**<br>(Convergent Squint) | **Infantile (Congenital) Esotropia** | • Onset < 6 months.<br>• Large angle deviation (>30 PD).<br>• **Cross-fixation** pattern.<br>• Associated with **[[Dissociated Vertical Deviation]] (DVD)** and latent **[[Nystagmus]]**. | • Innervational disturbances.<br>• Defect in fusion faculty.<br>• Usually NOT associated with significant refractive error. | **Surgery (Primary Choice):**<br>• **Bimedial Rectus Recession**.<br>• Or Recession-Resection (R&R) procedure.<br>• Done early (before 2 years) to restore binocularity. |
| | **Accommodative Esotropia** | **1. Refractive:**<br>• Esotropia fully corrected by glasses.<br><br>**2. Non-Refractive:**<br>• Esotropia greater at near than distance.<br>• High **[[AC/A ratio]]**.<br><br>**3. Partially Accommodative:**<br>• Residual squint remains after glasses. | • Uncorrected **[[Hypermetropia]]** (+2D to +7D).<br>• Excessive accommodation leads to excessive convergence.<br>• High AC/A ratio (Convergence per unit accommodation). | • **Full hypermetropic correction** (Spectacles).<br>• **Bifocals** (for high AC/A ratio).<br>• **[[Miotics]]** (e.g., Echothiophate - rarely used now).<br>• **Surgery** for residual non-accommodative element. |
| | **Sensory Esotropia** | Unilateral inward deviation in a poorly seeing eye. | • **Sensory deprivation** (e.g., [[Cataract]], [[Corneal Opacity]], [[Retinoblastoma]]) prevents fusion. | • Treat underlying cause (e.g., cataract surgery).<br>• Cosmetic squint surgery (Recess-Resect). |
| **[[Exotropia]]**<br>(Divergent Squint) | **Congenital Exotropia** | • Onset at birth (Rare).<br>• Large angle constant deviation. | • Innervational/Anatomical defects. | • **Surgery** (Bilateral Lateral Rectus Recession). |
| | **Intermittent Exotropia** | • Deviates when tired, daydreaming, or ill.<br>• **Closes one eye** in bright sunlight.<br>• Good binocular vision when eyes straight. | • **Divergence Excess:** Deviation Distance > Near.<br>• **Convergence Insufficiency:** Deviation Near > Distance.<br>• **Basic:** Distance = Near. | • Correction of [[Myopia]].<br>• **Orthoptics** (Anti-suppression exercises).<br>• **Surgery:**<br> - Bilateral Lateral Rectus Recession (for Divergence excess).<br> - Unilateral R&R (for Basic type). |
| | **Sensory Exotropia** | Constant outward deviation in a blind/poor eye (Adults). | • Long-standing visual loss (disrupts fusion). | • Cosmetic Surgery (Recess-Resect). |
| | **Consecutive Exotropia** | Outward deviation in a previously esotropic eye. | • Spontaneous (long-standing esotropia).<br>• Surgical overcorrection of esotropia. | • Surgery if cosmetically significant. |

#### 3. Incomitant Strabismus: Paralytic
*Angle of deviation varies with gaze. Secondary deviation > Primary deviation.*

| Nerve Palsy | Muscle Affected | Defect / Clinical Signs | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Oculomotor Nerve Palsy]]** (CN III) | SR, IR, MR, IO, LPS, Sphincter pupillae | • **Ptosis**.<br>• Eye is **"Down and Out"**.<br>• Fixed dilated pupil (if compressive).<br>• Loss of accommodation. | • **Vascular:** [[Diabetes]], [[Hypertension]] (Pupil sparing).<br>• **Aneurysm:** PComm artery (Pupil involving).<br>• **Trauma/Tumors**. | • **Medical:** Treat cause (DM/HTN), Prisms for [[Diplopia]].<br>• **Observation:** 6 months for spontaneous recovery.<br>• **Surgery:** If no recovery. Large recession-resection or muscle transposition. |
| **[[Trochlear Nerve Palsy]]** (CN IV) | **Superior Oblique (SO)** | • **Hypertropia** of affected eye.<br>• **Excyclotorsion**.<br>• Head tilt to **opposite shoulder** (Bielschowsky sign +ve).<br>• Diplopia worse on looking down/in. | • **Trauma** (Most common cause of acquired vertical strabismus).<br>• Congenital.<br>• Vascular. | • **Prisms** (for small deviation).<br>• **Surgery:**<br> - Weakening of antagonist (Inferior Oblique).<br> - Tucking of SO tendon.<br> - Harada-Ito procedure (for torsion). |
| **[[Abducens Nerve Palsy]]** (CN VI) | **Lateral Rectus (LR)** | • **Esotropia** (convergent).<br>• Limitation of **Abduction**.<br>• Face turn to **same side**.<br>• Uncrossed diplopia. | • **Raised Intracranial Pressure** (False localizing sign).<br>• **Vascular** (DM/HTN).<br>• Trauma.<br>• Viral infection. | • **Wait & Watch** (6 months), Botox.<br>• **Surgery:**<br> - Vertical Muscle Transposition (Hummelsheim/Jensen procedure) if total palsy.<br> - Recess-Resect if partial palsy. |

> **Mnemonic for CN Innervation:** **LR6 SO4** (Lateral Rectus-6, Superior Oblique-4, Rest-3).

#### 4. Incomitant Strabismus: Restrictive & Special Syndromes
*Forced Duction Test (FDT) is POSITIVE (Mechanical restriction).*

| Syndrome | Defect / Clinical Features | Etiopathogenesis | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Duane Retraction Syndrome]]** (DRS) | • **Retraction of globe** & narrowing of palpebral fissure on **Adduction**.<br>• **Type 1:** Limited Abduction (Most common).<br>• **Type 2:** Limited Adduction.<br>• **Type 3:** Limited Abduction & Adduction. | • **Congenital agenesis** of CN VI nucleus.<br>• LR muscle innervated paradoxically by CN III.<br>• Co-contraction of MR and LR causing retraction. | • **No Treatment** if eyes straight in primary gaze.<br>• **Surgery** only for severe face turn or significant deviation (Recession of recti).<br>• **Resection is CONTRAINDICATED.** |
| **[[Brown Syndrome]]** (SO Tendon Sheath Syndrome) | • Limitation of **Elevation in Adduction**.<br>• Normal elevation in abduction.<br>• Positive FDT on elevating the adducted eye. | • **Short/tight Superior Oblique tendon** or sheath abnormality.<br>• Can be Congenital or Acquired (Inflammation/Trauma - "Clicking" sensation). | • **NSAIDs/Steroid injection** (for acquired/inflammatory).<br>• **Surgery:** Superior Oblique tenotomy/expander (only if significant hypotropia or head posture). |
| **[[Thyroid Eye Disease]]** (Graves' Ophthalmopathy) | • Restrictive myopathy.<br>• **Hypotropia** (IR involved m/c) $\rightarrow$ Defective elevation.<br>• **Esotropia** (MR involved 2nd m/c) $\rightarrow$ Defective abduction. | • Fibrosis and lymphocytic infiltration of EOMs.<br>• Order of muscle involvement: **I'M SL**ow (Inferior, Medial, Superior, Lateral). | • Treat thyroid status.<br>• Systemic Steroids/Radiotherapy.<br>• **Surgery:** Recession of tight muscles (adjustable sutures preferred). |
| **[[Strabismus Fixus]]** | • Both eyes fixed in extreme adduction (convergent). | • Fibrosis of Medial Recti (acquired or congenital). | • Surgery: Large recession of Medial Recti. |

### A and V Pattern Strabismus
*Vertically incomitant horizontal strabismus.*

*   **V-Pattern:** Deviation differs by >15 PD between upgaze and downgaze. (Esotropia worse in downgaze; Exotropia worse in upgaze).
    *   *Cause:* Inferior Oblique overaction.
*   **A-Pattern:** Deviation differs by >10 PD between upgaze and downgaze. (Esotropia worse in upgaze; Exotropia worse in downgaze).
    *   *Cause:* Superior Oblique overaction.

> **Mnemonic for Surgery:** **MALE**
> *   **M**edial rectus moved toward **A**pex of the pattern.
> *   **L**ateral rectus moved toward **E**mpty space (base) of the pattern.
