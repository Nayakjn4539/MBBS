# [[International Health]] > World Health Organization

### World Health Organization (WHO)

**Definition:** The [[World Health Organization (WHO)]] is a specialized, non-political health agency of the United Nations.

*   **Constitution Came into Force:** **7th April 1948**. This date is celebrated annually as [[World Health Day]].
*   **Headquarters:** **Geneva**, Switzerland.
*   **Objective:** "The attainment by all peoples of the highest possible level of health."
*   **Membership:** Open to all countries. Currently has **194 Member States** and 2 Associate Members.

---

### Organizational Structure

The WHO consists of three principal organs:

#### 1. The World Health Assembly (WHA)
*   **Nature:** The supreme governing body, often called the **"Health Parliament"** of nations.
*   **Composition:** Composed of delegates representing all Member States. Each member has one vote.
*   **Meetings:** Meets annually, usually in **May** at Geneva.
*   **Functions:**
    *   Determines international health policies and programmes.
    *   Reviews the work of the past year.
    *   Approves the budget for the following year.
    *   Elects Member States to designate persons to serve on the Executive Board.
    *   Appoints the [[Director-General]] (on nomination by the Executive Board).

#### 2. The Executive Board
*   **Composition:** Consists of **34 members** technically qualified in the field of health.
*   **Representation:** Members are designated by, but do not represent, their governments. At least 3 members are elected from each of the 6 WHO regions.
*   **Term:** Members serve for **3 years**; one-third of the membership is renewed every year.
*   **Meetings:** Meets at least twice a year (usually January and May).
*   **Functions:**
    *   Gives effect to the decisions and policies of the WHA.
    *   Has the power to take emergency action (e.g., during epidemics/earthquakes).

#### 3. The Secretariat
*   **Nature:** The administrative and technical organ.
*   **Leadership:** Headed by the **Director-General (DG)**, who is the chief technical and administrative officer.
*   **Staff:** Comprises international public servants, technical experts, and support staff.
*   **Function:** Provides technical and managerial support to Member States for their national health development programmes.

### Regional Organizations

To meet special health needs of different areas, WHO has established **six regional organizations**. These are integral parts of WHO.

| Region | Abbreviation | Headquarters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **South East Asia** | **SEARO** | **New Delhi (India)** |
| Africa | AFRO | Brazzaville (Congo) |
| The Americas | PAHO/AMRO | Washington D.C. (U.S.A.) |
| Europe | EURO | Copenhagen (Denmark) |
| Eastern Mediterranean | EMRO | Alexandria (Egypt) / Cairo |
| Western Pacific | WPRO | Manila (Philippines) |

**Note:** The Regional Office is headed by a **Regional Director**. There is a **Regional Committee** composed of representatives of Member States in the region which meets once a year.

### Functions of WHO

The first constitutional function is to act as the **directing and coordinating authority** on all international health work.

#### 1. Prevention and Control of Specific Diseases
*   **Communicable Diseases:**
    *   Global eradication of [[Smallpox]] (an outstanding achievement).
    *   Global battle against [[Poliomyelitis]] (Polio eradication).
    *    epidemiological surveillance of diseases subject to [[International Health Regulations]] (Cholera, Plague, Yellow Fever).
    *   Expanded Programme on Immunization (EPI).
*   **Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs):**
    *   Focus on Cancer, Cardiovascular diseases, Diabetes, and Genetic disorders.
    *   Campaigns against smoking and drug addiction.

#### 2. Development of Comprehensive Health Services
*   Promoting **National Health Policy** development.
*   Organizing health systems based on [[Primary Health Care]] (Alma-Ata Declaration).
*   Health manpower development and utilization.
*   Promoting **Appropriate Technology for Health**.

#### 3. Family Health
*   Improvement of quality of life of the family as a unit.
*   Focus areas: [[Maternal and Child Health (MCH)]], Human reproduction, Nutrition, and Health Education.

#### 4. Environmental Health
*   Advising on basic sanitary services and safe water supply (**Water for All**).
*   Protection of air, water, and food quality.
*   Radiation protection and identification of new hazards.

#### 5. Health Statistics and Standards
*   Dissemination of morbidity and mortality statistics.
*   Publication of the **International Classification of Diseases ([[ICD]])**. (Current version: ICD-11).
*   Standardization of diagnostic procedures and biological products (vaccines, sera, drugs).
*   Publication of the **International Pharmacopoeia**.

#### 6. Biomedical Research
*   WHO does not perform research itself but **stimulates and coordinates** research work.
*   Establishes a network of **WHO Collaborating Centres**.
*   **TDR:** Special Programme for Research and Training in Tropical Diseases (Target diseases: Malaria, Schistosomiasis, Trypanosomiasis, Filariasis, Leishmaniasis, Leprosy).

#### 7. Health Literature and Information
*   Acts as a clearing house for information.
*   **Publications:**
    *   *Weekly Epidemiological Record (WER)*
    *   *World Health Statistics Annual*
    *   *Bulletin of the WHO*
    *   *Technical Report Series*

---

### Public Health Relevance
*   **Pandemic Response:** WHO plays a critical role in declaring a **Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC)** (e.g., COVID-19, Ebola) and coordinating the global response.
*   **Standard Setting:** The definitions (e.g., Blindness, Live Birth) and standards (e.g., Drinking water quality, Growth charts) set by WHO are followed by national health programs in India.
*   **Country Support:** WHO provides technical support to the Government of India for programs like [[National Tuberculosis Elimination Programme]] (NTEP) and [[National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme]] (NVBDCP).
