# Corneal Dystrophies

**Definition**: A group of spontaneous, hereditary, bilateral, symmetrical, non-inflammatory, and progressive disorders of the [[cornea]] that usually affect the central area and occur in the absence of vascularization.

### Classification (IC3D - International Committee for Classification of Corneal Dystrophies)
Classification is based on the primary anatomical layer involved:
1.  **Epithelial and Subepithelial Dystrophies**: e.g., Cogan's (Map-Dot-Fingerprint), Meesmann's.
2.  **Bowman Layer Dystrophies**: e.g., Reis-Bucklers, Thiel-Behnke.
3.  **Stromal Dystrophies**: e.g., Granular, Lattice, Macular, Schnyder.
4.  **Descemet Membrane and Endothelial Dystrophies**: e.g., Fuchs’ Endothelial, Posterior Polymorphous.

### Fuchs' Endothelial Corneal Dystrophy (FECD)

**Overview**:
*   A slowly progressive, bilateral condition affecting the [[corneal endothelium]] and [[Descemet's membrane]].
*   **Epidemiology**: More common in elderly females (F:M = 4:1) usually between the 5th and 7th decade.
*   **Inheritance**: Often sporadic, but can be Autosomal Dominant.

**Pathogenesis**:
*   Primary malfunction of the **endothelial Na+/K+ pump** mechanism leading to stromal and epithelial edema.
*   Formation of hyaline excrescences (warts) on Descemet's membrane called **Cornea Guttata**.
*   Endothelial cell density decreases below the critical level (approx <500 cells/mm²) causing corneal decompensation.

**Clinical Stages**:
1.  **Stage of Cornea Guttata**: Central excrescences on Descemet's membrane; asymptomatic. Appearance of "beaten-metal".
2.  **Edematous Stage**: Endothelial decompensation leads to stromal and epithelial edema.
    *   **Clinical Symptom**: **Blurring of vision worse in the morning**. This occurs because evaporation during the day clears the edema caused by eyelid closure (lack of evaporation) during sleep.
3.  **Stage of Bullous Keratopathy**: Formation of epithelial bullae (vesicles) which may rupture causing severe pain, photophobia, and irritation.
4.  **Stage of Scarring**: Subepithelial scar tissue forms; cornea becomes opaque and vascularized (pannus formation).

**Management**:
*   **Medical**:
    *   **Hypertonic saline (5% NaCl)** drops or ointment to dehydrate the cornea.
    *   Warm air (hair dryer) to aid evaporation.
    *   Bandage contact lens for pain relief in bullous keratopathy.
    *   Lowering [[Intraocular Pressure]] (IOP).
*   **Surgical**:
    *   **[[Keratoplasty]]**: Indicated when visual acuity is markedly reduced.
    *   **Endothelial Keratoplasty (DSEK/DSAEK/DMEK)** is preferred over Penetrating Keratoplasty (PK) as it replaces only the diseased posterior layer.

### Stromal Corneal Dystrophies

These are the most clinically significant dystrophies involving the [[corneal stroma]].

**Mnemonic for Stromal Dystrophies:**
*   **M**arilyn **M**onroe **A**lways **G**ets **H**er **M**en in **L**os **A**ngeles **C**ounty.
    *   **M**acular - **M**ucopolysaccharide - **A**lcian Blue (or Acid Schiff/Colloidal Iron).
    *   **G**ranular - **H**yaline - **M**asson Trichrome.
    *   **L**attice - **A**myloid - **C**ongo Red.

#### 1. Granular Corneal Dystrophy (Type 1)
*   **Inheritance**: Autosomal Dominant (AD).
*   **Genetics**: Mutation in **TGFBI gene** (5q31).
*   **Pathology**: Deposition of **Hyaline** material.
*   **Stain**: **Masson Trichrome** (Stains bright red).
*   **Clinical Features**:
    *   Discrete, grayish-white, "crumb-like" or "snowflake" opacities in the anterior stroma.
    *   **Key Feature**: The intervening stroma between opacities is **CLEAR** initially.
    *   Opacities do **not** extend to the limbus (peripheral cornea is spared).
    *   Vision remains good until late stages when opacities coalesce.

#### 2. Lattice Corneal Dystrophy (Type 1)
*   **Inheritance**: Autosomal Dominant (AD).
*   **Genetics**: Mutation in **TGFBI gene** (5q31).
*   **Pathology**: Deposition of **Amyloid**.
*   **Stain**: **Congo Red** (exhibits apple-green birefringence under polarized light).
*   **Clinical Features**:
    *   Refractile, branching, spider-like, filamentous lines (lattice-work) in the stroma.
    *   Central stroma affected; periphery relatively spared.
    *   **Symptom**: frequently presents with **Recurrent Corneal Erosions** causing pain and photophobia.

#### 3. Macular Corneal Dystrophy
*   **Inheritance**: **Autosomal Recessive (AR)** ( *Important Exam Point: The only common stromal dystrophy that is Recessive*).
*   **Genetics**: Mutation in **CHST6 gene** (16q22).
*   **Pathology**: Deposition of **Mucopolysaccharides (Glycosaminoglycans)**.
*   **Stain**: **Alcian Blue** or Colloidal Iron.
*   **Clinical Features**:
    *   **Most severe** of the stromal dystrophies; significant visual impairment occurs early (10-30 years).
    *   Diffuse stromal haze with focal grey-white spots.
    *   **Key Feature**: The intervening stroma is **CLOUDY/HAZY** (unlike granular).
    *   Lesions extend to the **limbus** (peripheral cornea is involved).

### Summary Table: Stromal Dystrophies

| Feature | Granular Dystrophy | Lattice Dystrophy | Macular Dystrophy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inheritance** | Autosomal Dominant (AD) | Autosomal Dominant (AD) | **Autosomal Recessive (AR)** |
| **Deposit Material**| **Hyaline** | **Amyloid** | **Mucopolysaccharide** (GAGs) |
| **Histopath Stain** | **Masson Trichrome** (Red) | **Congo Red** (Green birefringence) | **Alcian Blue** / Colloidal Iron |

| **Intervening Stroma**| **Clear** | Clear initially, haze later | **Cloudy / Hazy** |

| **Gene** | TGFBI (5q31) | TGFBI (5q31) | CHST6 (16q22) |
| **Visual Prognosis** | Good until late | Good until late | **Poor** (severe loss early) |

### Management of Stromal Dystrophies

1.  **Observation**: In early stages if vision is not affected.
2.  **Treatment of Recurrent Erosions**: Lubricants, bandage contact lens, hypertonic saline.
3.  **Phototherapeutic Keratectomy ([[PTK]])**: Using Excimer laser to ablate superficial stromal opacities (especially useful in Granular and Lattice).
4.  **[[Keratoplasty]]**:
    *   **Deep Anterior Lamellar Keratoplasty (DALK)**: Preferred if endothelium is healthy; reduces rejection risk.
    *   **Penetrating Keratoplasty (PK)**: Indicated for deep stromal involvement or when endothelium is affected (often required in Macular dystrophy).
    *   **Recurrence**: Can occur in grafts; Lattice dystrophy tends to recur.
