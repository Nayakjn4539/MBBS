# [[World Health Day]]

### **1. Definition and Origin**
*   **Date:** Observed globally on **7th April** every year.
*   **Significance:** It marks the anniversary of the day the **[[Constitution of WHO]]** came into force in **1948**.
*   **History:** The constitution was drafted by the "Technical Preparatory Committee" in 1946 under the chairmanship of **Rene Sand** and ratified by member states by April 7, 1948.

### **2. Objectives**
*   To focus worldwide attention on a specific aspect or theme of **[[public health]]**.
*   To draw the attention of all member countries towards a topic of global health importance.
*   To mobilize action, awareness, and resources for specific health issues.

### **3. Themes**
A specific theme is chosen each year to highlight a priority area of public health concern. Below are selected themes from recent history:

| Year | Theme |
| :--- | :--- |
| **2008** | Protecting health from [[climate change]] |
| **2009** | Make hospitals safe in emergencies |
| **2010** | Urbanization and Health (1000 cities - 1000 lives) |
| **2011** | [[Antimicrobial resistance]]: No action today, no cure tomorrow |
| **2012** | Ageing and health: Good health adds life to years |
| **2013** | High blood pressure |
| **2014** | [[Vector borne diseases]] (Small bite, big threat) |
| **2015** | Food Safety |
| **2016** | Diabetes (Beat Diabetes) |
| **2019** | [[Universal Health Coverage]] |

> **Note for Students:** In Viva Voce, you may be asked why 7th April is chosen. The specific answer is: *"Because the WHO Constitution came into force on this date in 1948"*.

### **4. Clinical & Public Health Relevance**
*   **Advocacy:** It serves as a launchpad for long-term health advocacy programs (e.g., mental health awareness, immunization drives).
*   **Policy Making:** Governments often time the launch of new health initiatives or policies to coincide with this day to gain maximum public attention.
