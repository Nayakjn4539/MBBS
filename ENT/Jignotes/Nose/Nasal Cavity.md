# [[Nose]] > Nasal Cavity

# Anatomy and Physiology of the Nasal Cavity

## I. Anatomy of the External Nose
The external nose is a pyramidal structure with its root superiorly and base directed downwards. It consists of an osteocartilaginous framework covered by muscles and skin.

### A. Osteocartilaginous Framework
The upper 1/3 is bony, and the lower 2/3 is cartilaginous.

1.  **Bony Part:**
    *   Two **nasal bones** (meet in the midline).
    *   Nasal process of the **frontal bone**.
    *   Frontal process of the **maxilla**.
2.  **Cartilaginous Part:**
    *   **Upper Lateral Cartilages (ULC):** Fuse with the septal cartilage; lower free edge forms the **[[limen nasi]]** or [[nasal valve]].
    *   **Lower Lateral Cartilages (LLC) / Alar Cartilages:** U-shaped; consists of a **medial crus** (forming columella) and **lateral crus** (forming ala).
    *   **Lesser Alar (Sesamoid) Cartilages:** Located above and lateral to alar cartilages.
    *   **Septal Cartilage:** Anterior border supports the dorsum.

### B. Skin Lining
*   **Upper Part:** Thin and freely mobile.
*   **Lower Part (Alar/Tip):** Thick, adherent, and contains numerous **[[sebaceous glands]]**.
    *   *Clinical Implication:* Hypertrophy of these sebaceous glands causes **[[Rhinophyma]]** (potato nose).

---

## II. Anatomy of the Internal Nose
The internal nose is divided into right and left cavities by the **[[nasal septum]]**. Each cavity has two parts:

### 1. Nasal Vestibule
*   The anterior, skin-lined part of the nasal cavity.
*   **Lining:** Stratified squamous epithelium containing hair follicles (**vibrissae**) and sebaceous glands.
*   **Boundary:** Limited superiorly by the **[[limen nasi]]** (nasal valve), which corresponds to the lower margin of the ULC.
*   *Clinical Implication:* Site for furuncles (vestibulitis).

### 2. Nasal Cavity Proper
Extends from the limen nasi to the posterior choanae.
*   **Lining Mucosa:**
    *   **Olfactory Region:** Upper 1/3 (superior turbinate, roof, upper septum). Lined by **[[olfactory epithelium]]** (neuroepithelium).
    *   **Respiratory Region:** Lower 2/3. Lined by **[[Schneiderian membrane]]** (Pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium) with goblet cells.

---

## III. Medial Wall (Nasal Septum)
The septum divides the nose into two chambers. It has three parts:
1.  **Columellar Septum:** Medial crura of alar cartilages covered by skin.
2.  **Membranous Septum:** Double layer of skin between columella and septal cartilage.
3.  **Septum Proper:** Osteocartilaginous framework covered by mucosa.

### Components of Septum Proper
| Component | Structure | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quadrilateral Cartilage** | Hyaline cartilage | Anterior part; wedged between vomer and ethmoid |
| **Perpendicular Plate of Ethmoid** | Bone | Superior part |
| **Vomer** | Bone | Postero-inferior part |
| **Minor Contributions** | Nasal spine of frontal bone, Rostrum of sphenoid, Crests of maxilla/palatine | Periphery |

*   *Clinical Implication:* Deviated Nasal Septum (**[[DNS]]**) often involves the junction of these structures. A persistent **[[septal spur]]** may cause epistaxis. The membranous septum is the site for transfixion incision in septoplasty.

## IV. Lateral Wall of Nose
This is complex and increases the surface area for air conditioning.

### A. Turbinates (Conchae)
Scroll-like bony projections.
1.  **Inferior Turbinate:** Largest; an independent bone.
2.  **Middle Turbinate:** Part of the [[ethmoid bone]].
3.  **Superior Turbinate:** Part of the ethmoid bone.
4.  **Supreme Turbinate:** Occasionally present.

### B. Meatuses and Drainage
The spaces beneath the turbinates are meatuses.

| Meatus | Structures Opening/Draining Here |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Inferior Meatus** | **[[Nasolacrimal Duct]]** (Hasner's Valve). |
| **Middle Meatus** | **Frontal Sinus** (via frontal recess)<br>**Maxillary Sinus** (natural ostium)<br>**Anterior Ethmoid Cells**. |
| **Superior Meatus** | **Posterior Ethmoid Cells**. |
| **Sphenoethmoidal Recess** | **[[Sphenoid Sinus]]** (lies above superior turbinate). |

**Mnemonic for NLD direction:** **DOUBLE** (Downwards, Backwards, Laterally).

### C. Osteomeatal Complex (OMC)
A functional unit in the middle meatus critical for sinus drainage. Blockage here leads to sinusitis.
*   **Components:**
    1.  **[[Uncinate Process]]**: Sickle-shaped bone.
    2.  **[[Ethmoid Bulla]]**: Largest anterior ethmoid cell.
    3.  **[[Hiatus Semilunaris]]**: 2D slit between uncinate and bulla.
    4.  **Infundibulum**: 3D channel where maxillary sinus ostium opens.

## V. Blood Supply
The nose has a rich vascular supply from both External Carotid (ECA) and Internal Carotid (ICA) systems.

### 1. Arterial Supply
*   **ICA Branches:**
    *   **Anterior Ethmoidal Artery:** Supplies anterior superior septum and lateral wall.
    *   **Posterior Ethmoidal Artery:** Supplies superior septum/lateral wall.
*   **ECA Branches:**
    *   **[[Sphenopalatine Artery]] (Branch of Maxillary):** Main artery of the nose (Artery of Epistaxis).
    *   **Greater Palatine Artery:** Supplies posterior septum.
    *   **Superior Labial Artery (Branch of Facial):** Supplies anterior septum.

### 2. Kiesselbach’s Plexus (Little’s Area)
Located on the anteroinferior septum. It is the most common site of **[[epistaxis]]** (90% of cases).
*   **Formed by anastomosis of 4 arteries:**
    1.  Septal branch of Sphenopalatine (ECA)
    2.  Septal branch of Greater Palatine (ECA)
    3.  Septal branch of Superior Labial (ECA)
    4.  Anterior Ethmoidal (ICA)
    *(Note: Posterior ethmoidal artery does not participate)*.

### 3. Venous Drainage
*   **Retrocolumellar Vein:** Common site of venous bleeding in young people.
*   **Woodruff’s Plexus:** Venous plexus posterior to the inferior turbinate; common site for posterior epistaxis in adults.
*   **Danger Area:** Veins drain into the cavernous sinus via ophthalmic veins; risk of cavernous sinus thrombosis from nasal infections.

## VI. Nerve Supply

### 1. Olfactory Nerves (CN I)
Supply the olfactory region (upper 1/3). Nerves pass through the cribriform plate.

### 2. Sensory Supply (General Sensation)
*   **Ophthalmic Division (V1):** Anterior Ethmoidal nerve (supplies anterior septum and lateral wall).
*   **Maxillary Division (V2):** Branches from **[[Sphenopalatine ganglion]]** (supplies posterior septum and lateral wall) and Infraorbital nerve (vestibule).

### 3. Autonomic Supply (Vidian Nerve)
*   **Nerve of Pterygoid Canal ([[Vidian Nerve]]):** Formed by fusion of:
    *   **Greater Superficial Petrosal Nerve (GSPN):** Parasympathetic (Secretomotor/Vasodilator). *Clinical:* Stimulation causes **[[Rhinorrhea]]**.
    *   **Deep Petrosal Nerve:** Sympathetic (Vasoconstrictor). *Clinical:* Stimulation causes decongestion.

## VII. Physiology of the Nose

### 1. Respiration
*   **Pathway:** Main airflow is laminar, passing through the middle part of the nose (parabolic curve).
*   **Nasal Valve:** The narrowest area (limen nasi), offering maximum resistance (50% of total airway resistance).
*   **[[Nasal Cycle]]**: Rhythmic congestion and decongestion alternating between nostrils every 2.5–4 hours, controlled by the autonomic system.

### 2. Air Conditioning
*   **Filtration:** Vibrissae filter coarse particles (>3 µm). Mucus blanket traps fine particles (0.5–3 µm).
*   **Temperature Regulation:** The "Radiator Mechanism" of vascular sinusoids (erectile tissue) warms inspired air to 37°C.
*   **Humidification:** Serous glands saturate air to 75–80% humidity.

### 3. Protection (Mucociliary Clearance)
*   **Mucous Blanket:** Two layers—Superficial viscous layer (Gel) traps debris; Deep serous layer (Sol) allows ciliary movement.
*   **Ciliary Movement:** Cilia beat 10–20 times/sec, moving mucus backward towards the nasopharynx to be swallowed.
*   **Enzymes:** Mucus contains **[[Lysozyme]]** (bacteriolytic) and IgA.

### 4. Olfaction
*   Sniffing directs air to the olfactory cleft (upper nose).
*   Odorants dissolve in mucus and stimulate bipolar olfactory receptors.
*   *Clinical:* **[[Anosmia]]** (loss of smell) can occur in obstructed clefts or nerve damage.

### 5. Vocal Resonance
*   The nose acts as a resonating chamber for nasal consonants (M, N, NG).
*   *Clinical:* Blocked nose causes **Rhinolalia Clausa** (hyponasality); Palatal defects cause **Rhinolalia Aperta** (hypernasality).

### 6. Nasal Reflexes
*   **Sneeze Reflex:** Protective expulsion of irritants.
*   **Nasopulmonary Reflex:** Nasal obstruction leads to increased pulmonary resistance.
