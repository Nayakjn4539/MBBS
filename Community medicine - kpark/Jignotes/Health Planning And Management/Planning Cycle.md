# [[Health Planning And Management]] > Planning Cycle

### **The Planning Cycle in Health Management**

[[Health Planning]] is defined as the orderly process of defining community health problems, identifying unmet needs, surveying resources to meet them, establishing priority goals that are realistic and feasible, and projecting administrative action to accomplish the purpose of the proposed programme.

The **Planning Cycle** is a continuous, dynamic process involving a succession of steps to analyze a system and initiate necessary action.

---

### **Pre-Planning Phase**
Before the actual planning cycle begins, certain **Pre-conditions** must be met for success,:
1.  **Government Interest:** Strong political will and clear directives.
2.  **Legislation:** Necessary laws (e.g., [[MTP Act]], Epidemic Diseases Act) to support health policies.
3.  **Organization for Planning:** A structured body (e.g., [[NITI Aayog]] in India) to prepare the plan.
4.  **Administrative Capacity:** Ability to coordinate and implement the plan at central and state levels.

---

### **Steps of the Planning Cycle**

The cycle consists of **8 major steps**,:

#### **1. Analysis of the Health Situation**
*   This is the first step, also known as [[Community Diagnosis]].
*   It involves the collection, assessment, and interpretation of data to create a clear picture of the health status.
*   **Data required:**
    *   Population demographics (Age/Sex structure).
    *   Morbidity and Mortality statistics.
    *   Epidemiology and geographical distribution of diseases.
    *   Existing medical care facilities and technical manpower.
    *   Training facilities and cultural beliefs/attitudes of the population.

#### **2. Establishment of Objectives and Goals**
*   Without clear targets, there is haphazard activity and waste of funds.
*   **Objectives:** Planned endpoints of all activities; they are precise and concerned with the problem itself.
*   **Goals:** Ultimate desired state towards which objectives are directed; not constrained by time or resources (e.g., Health for All).
*   **Targets:** Discrete activities to measure the attainment of objectives (e.g., [[IMR]] reduction to 25/1000).

#### **3. Assessment of Resources**
*   Implies the evaluation of **Manpower, Money, Materials**, skills, knowledge, and time needed vs. what is available.
*   A balance must be struck between requirements and availability to ensure feasibility.

#### **4. Fixing Priorities**
*   Since resources are always limited, needs must be prioritized.
*   **Criteria for prioritization:**
    *   Magnitude of the problem (Mortality/Morbidity data).
    *   Financial constraints (Cost-effectiveness).
    *   Diseases preventable at low cost.
    *   Political and community interests/pressures.

#### **5. Write-up of Formulated Plan**
*   This is the **Blue-print** for action.
*   It must define the inputs (resources) required for expected outputs (results).
*   It must contain working guidance for execution and a **built-in system of evaluation**.

#### **6. Programming and Implementation**
*   This stage depends on effective organization.
*   **Key components:**
    *   Definition of roles and tasks.
    *   Selection, training, motivation, and supervision of [[Health Manpower]].
    *   Establishing communication channels.
*   *Note:* Shortcomings often appear at this stage due to delays in supplies or inappropriate staff use.

#### **7. Monitoring**
*   **Definition:** The continuous, day-to-day follow-up of activities during implementation to ensure they are proceeding as planned.
*   It involves observing, recording, and reporting.
*   **Goal:** To identify deviations early and take corrective action.

#### **8. Evaluation**
*   **Definition:** Assessing the achievement of the stated objectives of a programme.
*   It measures the final outcome, adequacy, efficiency, and acceptance.
*   It provides **feedback** for the next cycle (Reassessment), allowing for the modification of future plans.

### **Clinical & Managerial Relevance**
*   **District Health Officers:** Use this cycle to create the [[District Health Action Plan]] under [[National Health Mission]].
*   **Hospital Administrators:** Use it to plan new wings (e.g., ICU) or introduce new protocols (e.g., [[Biomedical Waste Management]]).
*   **Resource Allocation:** Helps prevent wastage by ensuring money and manpower are directed toward high-priority health burdens like [[Tuberculosis]] or [[Maternal Mortality]].
