# [[International Health]] > UNICEF and Other Agencies

### **UNICEF (United Nations Children’s Fund)**

**Background & Structure**
*   **Established:** 1946 (originally United Nations International Children’s Emergency Fund).
*   **Headquarters:** [[New York]], USA.
*   **Regional Office (South Central Asia):** [[Kathmandu]], Nepal.
*   **Nobel Peace Prize:** Awarded in 1965.
*   **Core Philosophy:** The **"Whole Child"** concept—focusing not just on immediate health but long-term development.

**Key Functions & Strategies**
1.  **Child Health & Nutrition:** Focuses on reducing infant and child mortality.
2.  **Primary Health Care (PHC):** Supports PHC at the grass-root level.
3.  **Water & Sanitation:** Provision of safe drinking water (e.g., tube wells).
4.  **Education:** Formal and non-formal education support.

**The "GOBI-FFF" Strategy**
UNICEF promotes a "Child Health Revolution" through a low-cost, high-impact strategy known as **GOBI-FFF**.

| Acronym | Component | Clinical/Public Health Relevance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **G** | **G**rowth Monitoring | Use of [[Growth Charts]] to detect malnutrition early. |
| **O** | **O**ral Rehydration | Management of dehydration due to diarrhea ([[ORS]]). |
| **B** | **B**reastfeeding | Promotion of exclusive breastfeeding. |
| **I** | **I**mmunization | Protection against 6 killer diseases (Universal Immunization Programme). |
| **F** | **F**emale Education | Literacy correlates directly with lower IMR/MMR. |
| **F** | **F**ood Supplements | Addressing nutritional gaps (e.g., Vitamin A). |
| **F** | **F**amily Spacing | Birth spacing improves maternal and child survival. |

*Mnemonic for GOBI-FFF:* **GO BI**g **F**or **F**amily **F**uture.

**UNICEF in India**
*   Supports [[ICDS]] (Integrated Child Development Services).
*   Assisted in establishing **Penicillin plant** (Pune) and **DDT plant** (Delhi).
*   Supported the **Amul Dairy Plant** (Anand) for milk conservation.
*   Salt Iodization program for [[Goiter]] control.

### **FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization)**

*   **Established:** 1945.
*   **Headquarters:** [[Rome]], Italy.
*   **Motto:** *Fiat Panis* (Let there be bread).

**Major Functions**
1.  **Raise Nutrition Levels:** Combat malnutrition and hunger.
2.  **Improve Production:** Increase efficiency of farming, fisheries, and forestry.
3.  **Rural Development:** Better the conditions of rural populations.
4.  **Campaign:** Launched the **Freedom from Hunger Campaign (FFHC)** in 1960.

**Clinical/Public Health Linkage**
*   **Food Standards:** Collaborates with WHO to create the [[Codex Alimentarius]] (International food standards to protect consumer health).
*   **Zoonoses Control:** Works on controlling diseases transmitted from animals to humans (e.g., Brucellosis).

*Mnemonic:* **F**ood **A**nd **O**regano $\rightarrow$ Pasta $\rightarrow$ **Rome** (HQ Location).

### **ILO (International Labour Organization)**

*   **Established:** 1919 (affiliated with the League of Nations; later UN).
*   **Headquarters:** [[Geneva]], Switzerland.
*   **Goal:** To promote social justice and improve labor conditions.

**Key Contributions**
1.  **International Labour Code:** Sets minimum standards for working hours, safety, and health policies.
2.  **Occupational Health:** Collaborates with WHO on health and safety in the workplace.
3.  **Living Standards:** Defines basic needs indicators like calorie consumption, housing, and literacy.

### **The Red Cross**

**History & Origin**
*   **Founder:** [[Henry Dunant]] (Swiss businessman).
*   **Inception:** Inspired by the suffering at the **Battle of Solferino (1859)**.
*   **Established:** 1864 (First Geneva Convention).
*   **Indian Red Cross Society:** Established in **1920**.

**Principles**
Humanity, Impartiality, Neutrality, Independence, Voluntary Service, Unity, Universality.

**Functions of Indian Red Cross**
1.  **Disaster Relief:** Immediate aid (food, medicine, shelter) during floods, famines, and earthquakes.
2.  **Blood Banking:** Maintains blood banks and promotes voluntary blood donation.
3.  **First Aid & Nursing:** Training programs (St. John Ambulance Association).
4.  **Maternal & Child Welfare:** Runs bureaus for maternity and child welfare.
5.  **Service to Armed Forces:** Caring for sick/wounded soldiers.
6.  **Rehabilitation:** Runs the **Red Cross Home** in [[Bangalore]] for disabled ex-servicemen.

**Junior Red Cross**
Active student wing focusing on:
1.  Promotion of Health.
2.  Service to the sick and suffering.
3.  International friendliness.
