# [[HEALTH CARE OF THE COMMUNITY]] > Preventive services offered by your Medical College & Hospital

### Preventive Services Offered by Medical College & Hospital

A Medical College Hospital acts as a [[Tertiary Level of Health Care]] facility. While it handles complex curative cases, it also plays a pivotal role in **preventive and promotive health care**, primarily coordinated through the **Department of Community Medicine (PSM)** and integrated into various clinical departments.

These services can be classified as follows:

#### 1. Maternal and Child Health (MCH) Services
Integrated services are provided to reduce maternal and infant mortality.
*   **Antenatal & Postnatal Care:** [[High Risk Pregnancy]] screening, tetanus immunization, and iron-folic acid supplementation.
*   **Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) & JSSK:** Implementation of free delivery and transport services for beneficiaries.
*   **Immunization:**
    *   Running a static immunization center under the [[Universal Immunization Programme]] (UIP).
    *   Providing vaccines like BCG, OPV, Hepatitis B, Pentavalent, Rotavirus, and MR.
    *   Maintenance of the **Cold Chain** (ILRs and Deep Freezers).
*   **Nutritional Services:**
    *   **Growth Monitoring:** Using growth charts to detect [[Protein Energy Malnutrition]] (PEM).
    *   **Nutritional Rehabilitation Centre (NRC):** Managing cases of Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM).

#### 2. Family Welfare and Planning
*   **Post Partum Programme (PPP):** A unique feature of medical colleges to motivate women for contraception during the lying-in period (when they are most receptive).
*   **Contraceptive Services:** Provision of spacing methods (IUCDs, OCPs) and terminal methods (Tubectomy, Vasectomy).
*   **MTP Services:** Safe abortion services under the [[MTP Act]] to prevent maternal morbidity/mortality from unsafe abortions.

#### 3. Communicable Disease Control
Medical colleges act as nodal centers for National Health Programs.
*   **NTEP (TB Control):** Functioning as a [[Designated Microscopy Centre]] (DMC) and DOTS center for diagnosis and treatment. Facilities for CBNAAT/TrueNat for MDR-TB detection,.
*   **NACP (HIV/AIDS):**
    *   **ICTC (Integrated Counselling and Testing Centre):** For HIV screening and pre/post-test counselling.
    *   **PPTCT:** Prevention of Parent to Child Transmission services.
    *   **ART Centers:** Provision of Anti-Retroviral Therapy.
*   **Anti-Rabies Clinic:** Provision of [[Post Exposure Prophylaxis]] (PEP) with ARV and Immunoglobulins for animal bites.

#### 4. Non-Communicable Disease (NCD) & Cancer Screening
*   **NCD Clinics:** Opportunistic screening for [[Hypertension]] and Diabetes Mellitus for patients aged >30 years.
*   **Cancer Screening:**
    *   **Cervical Cancer:** Pap smear and VIA (Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid).
    *   **Breast Cancer:** Mammography and self-examination training.
    *   **Oral Cancer:** Screening for pre-malignant lesions in dental/ENT OPDs.

#### 5. Outreach Services (Field Practice Areas)
This is a distinct function of a medical college to train interns/PGs and serve the community.
*   **Rural Health Training Centre (RHTC):** A peripheral centre delivering comprehensive health care to the rural population.
*   **Urban Health Training Centre (UHTC):** Catering to the health needs of urban slums.
*   **Health Camps:** Multi-specialty camps organized in underserved areas.

#### 6. Special Preventive Clinics
*   **Under-Fives Clinic:** Combining preventive, promotive, curative, and rehabilitative care for children,.
*   **Adolescent Health Clinic:** Addressing nutritional anemia (WIFS), menstrual hygiene, and mental health (AFHC).
*   **Geriatric Clinic:** Addressing age-related disability and degenerative diseases.
*   **Child Guidance Clinic:** For behavioral and developmental problems.

#### 7. Support Services
*   **Health Education (IEC):** Conducted in OPD waiting halls on topics like hygiene, nutrition, and vector control.
*   **Biomedical Waste Management:** Proper segregation and disposal to prevent hospital-acquired infections.

---
### Mnemonic for Preventive Services: "IMAGES"
*   **I** - **I**mmunization (UIP) & Infections (TB/HIV/Rabies)
*   **M** - **M**aternal & Child Health (MCH) / **M**edical Termination of Pregnancy
*   **A** - **A**dolescent & **A**nti-Larval measures
*   **G** - **G**eriatric Care & **G**rowth Monitoring
*   **E** - **E**ye Care (NPCB) & **E**ducation (Health Education)
*   **S** - **S**ocial Outreach (RHTC/UHTC) & **S**creening (NCDs)
