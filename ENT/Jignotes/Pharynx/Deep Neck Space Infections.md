# [[Pharynx]] > Deep Neck Space Infections

### **Deep Neck Space Infections**

**Definition**
Deep neck infections involve the potential spaces between the layers of the [[deep cervical fascia]]. These infections can spread rapidly, compromise the airway, and lead to fatal complications like mediastinitis,.

---

### **Pertinent Anatomy of Deep Neck Spaces**

The neck fascia is divided into superficial and deep layers. The [[deep cervical fascia]] creates spaces that act as pathways for infection spread.

| **Space** | **Boundaries/Location** | **Contents/Significance** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[Peritonsillar Space]]** | Between [[tonsillar capsule]] (medial) and [[superior constrictor muscle]] (lateral),. | Loose areolar tissue. Site of Quinsy. |
| **[[Parapharyngeal Space]]** | Inverted pyramid from skull base to hyoid. Lateral to pharynx, medial to mandible/parotid,. | Divided by [[styloid process]] into Pre-styloid and Post-styloid compartments. "Hub" of deep neck spaces. |
| **[[Retropharyngeal Space]]** | Between buccopharyngeal fascia (anterior) and alar fascia (posterior). Extends from skull base to T2/T4 (bifurcation of trachea),. | Contains [[Nodes of Rouviere]] (lymph nodes) which atrophy by age 3–4. |
| **[[Danger Space]]** | Posterior to retropharyngeal space. Between alar fascia and [[prevertebral fascia]]. Extends from skull base to **diaphragm**,. | Major pathway for spread to mediastinum ("Lincoln's Highway"). |
| **[[Submandibular Space]]** | Bound by floor of mouth and superficial layer of deep fascia. Divided by [[mylohyoid muscle]],. | Divided into **Sublingual** (above mylohyoid) and **Submaxillary** (below mylohyoid) spaces. |

### **Etiology and Microbiology**

**Common Sources:**
*   **Dental Infections (Odontogenic):** Most common cause in adults (esp. lower molars),.
*   **Tonsillitis:** Most common cause in children.
*   **Trauma:** Penetrating injuries, foreign bodies (fish bones), iatrogenic (endoscopy).
*   **IV Drug Abuse:** Injection into neck veins.

**Microbiology:**
Usually **polymicrobial** (mixed aerobes and anaerobes).
*   **Aerobes:** *Streptococcus pyogenes*, *Staphylococcus aureus*.
*   **Anaerobes:** *Bacteroides*, *Peptostreptococcus*, *Fusobacterium*.

### **Specific Deep Neck Infections**

#### **1. [[Peritonsillar Abscess]] (Quinsy)**
*   **Definition:** Collection of pus in the peritonsillar space.
*   **Pathogenesis:** Acute tonsillitis $\rightarrow$ blockage of [[crypta magna]] (largest crypt) $\rightarrow$ intratonsillar abscess $\rightarrow$ bursts into peritonsillar space,.
*   **Clinical Features:**
    *   **General:** High fever, malaise.
    *   **Local:** Severe throat pain, [[odynophagia]], referred otalgia.
    *   **Signs:**
        *   **Trismus** (spasm of medial pterygoid).
        *   "Hot potato" voice (muffled speech).
        *   **Uvula deviated** to the healthy side.
        *   Tonsil pushed **medially**; anterior pillar congested and bulging,,.
*   **Management:**
    *   **Medical:** IV Antibiotics, hydration.
    *   **Surgical:** **Incision and Drainage (I&D)** or Needle Aspiration.
        *   *Incision Site:* Point of maximum bulge OR intersection of a horizontal line from base of uvula and vertical line from anterior pillar,.
    *   **Interval Tonsillectomy:** Performed 4–6 weeks later to prevent recurrence.

#### **2. [[Parapharyngeal Abscess]]**
*   **Etiology:** Spread from tonsils (most common), dental infection (lower 3rd molar), or petrositis (Bezold's abscess).
*   **Clinical Features (Dependent on Compartment):**

| **Compartment** | **Pre-Styloid (Anterior)** | **Post-Styloid (Posterior)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Key Symptoms** | **Trismus** (hallmark), Odynophagia. | No Trismus. |
| **Signs** | **Tonsil pushed medially** (prolapse of tonsil fossa).<br>Swelling at the angle of the jaw. | Bulge behind the posterior pillar.<br>**CN Palsies:** IX, X, XI, XII.<br>**[[Horner's Syndrome]]** (Sympathetic chain involvement). |

*   **Complications:** **Internal Jugular Vein (IJV) Thrombosis** (Lemierre's syndrome), Carotid artery erosion/rupture.
*   **Treatment:** External drainage via neck incision (trans-cervical approach) to protect great vessels.

#### **3. [[Retropharyngeal Abscess]]**
*   **Types:**
    1.  **Acute:**
        *   **Patient:** Children < 5 years (due to suppuration of Nodes of Rouviere),.
        *   **Cause:** URTI, adenoiditis.
        *   **Presentation:** Dysphagia, stridor, drooling, **torticollis**, **Unilateral/paramedian bulge** on posterior pharyngeal wall,.
    2.  **Chronic:**
        *   **Patient:** Adults.
        *   **Cause:** Tuberculosis of cervical spine (Pott's disease).
        *   **Presentation:** **Midline bulge** (actually a prevertebral abscess), dull pain,.
*   **Investigation:** X-ray Lateral Neck showing **widened prevertebral shadow** (Normal: <7mm at C2; <14mm (kids)/22mm (adults) at C6),.
*   **Treatment:**
    *   *Acute:* Intraoral incision (Trendelenburg position to prevent aspiration).
    *   *Chronic:* Anti-tubercular therapy; External drainage (to avoid fistula).

#### **4. [[Ludwig's Angina]]**
*   **Definition:** Rapidly spreading cellulitis (not abscess) of the bilateral [[submandibular space]] (involving both sublingual and submaxillary spaces).
*   **Etiology:** Dental infection (80%), usually 2nd or 3rd lower molars.
*   **Clinical Features:**
    *   **Brawny edema:** Woody, hard feel of the floor of mouth.
    *   **Raised tongue:** Tongue pushed up and back $\rightarrow$ Airway obstruction.
    *   Drooling, trismus, difficulty speaking ("Hot potato voice"),.
*   **Management:**
    *   **Airway:** Primary concern. Tracheostomy may be required early.
    *   **Incision:** Wide horizontal incision ("collar incision") to decompress the space (pus is often scanty/serous).
    *   **Antibiotics:** High dose IV (covering anaerobes).

#### **5. [[Masticator Space]] Infection**
*   **Cause:** Infection of 3rd molar.
*   **Hallmark:** **Severe Trismus** (masseter/pterygoid spasm) with minimal pharyngeal signs,.

---

### **General Investigations**

1.  **Contrast Enhanced CT (CECT):** Gold standard. Differentiates abscess from cellulitis, defines extent, shows relation to great vessels,.
2.  **X-ray Soft Tissue Neck (Lateral):** Useful for retropharyngeal abscess (prevertebral widening, air-fluid levels).
3.  **Culture & Sensitivity:** From aspirate/pus.

---

### **Complications**

*   **Airway Obstruction:** Laryngeal edema or physical compression (common in Ludwig's).
*   **Mediastinitis:** Spread via [[Danger space]] or Retropharyngeal space ("Lincoln's Highway"),.
*   **Vascular:**
    *   **[[Lemierre's Syndrome]]**: Septic thrombophlebitis of IJV + septic emboli (lung abscesses),.
    *   Carotid artery erosion/aneurysm.
*   **Neurological:** Cranial nerve palsies (IX–XII) in parapharyngeal abscess.

---

### **Mnemonics**

*   **Ludwig's Angina Criteria (Goldring's):**
    *   **B**ilateral
    *   **B**rawny edema (Cellulitis, little pus)
    *   **B**elow mandible (Submandibular)
    *   **B**locked Airway (Risk)

*   **Parapharyngeal Abscess (Pre-styloid) Signs:**
    *   **3 Ts:**
        1.  **T**rismus
        2.  **T**onsil prolapse (medial push)
        3.  **T**umor (swelling) at angle of jaw
