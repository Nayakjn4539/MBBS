# [[Mental Health]] > National Mental Health Program

### [[National Mental Health Program (NMHP)]]

**Launched:** The Government of India launched the National Mental Health Program (NMHP) in **1982** to ensure the availability of mental health care services for all.

**Nodal Agency:** A National Advisory Group was constituted under the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare.

---

### **1. Problem Statement (Burden of Disease)**
*   **Prevalence:** According to the National Mental Health Survey (NMHS) 2015-16, nearly **150 million** Indians aged 13 and above are suffering from one or more mental health problems and need services.
*   **Distribution:** Mental health problems are comparatively more prevalent in **urban areas**.
*   **Gender:** Neurosis and stress-related disorders affect twice as many women compared to men.
*   **Suicide Risk:** Approximately 0.9% of the population is at high risk of suicide.
*   **Treatment Gap:** There is a huge gap between the burden of disease and the availability of infrastructure/manpower.

---

### **2. Aims and Objectives**
The basic philosophy is that mental health care must be integrated with general health services.

**Aims:**
1.  **Prevention** and treatment of mental and neurological disorders and associated disabilities.
2.  Use of **mental health technology** to improve general health services.
3.  Application of mental health principles in total national development to improve **quality of life**.

**Objectives:**
*   **Accessibility:** Ensure availability and accessibility of minimum mental health care for all, especially the vulnerable and underprivileged,.
*   **Application:** Encourage application of mental health knowledge in general health care and social development.
*   **Community Participation:** Promote community participation in service development and stimulate self-help efforts.

**Mnemonic for Objectives: "ACT"**
*   **A** - **A**ccessibility (Minimum care for all)
*   **C** - **C**ommunity Participation
*   **T** - **T**echnology/Knowledge application in general health

---

### **3. Strategies**
1.  **Integration:** Integration of mental health with primary health care through the NMHP.
2.  **Tertiary Care:** Provision of tertiary care institutions for the treatment of mental disorders.
3.  **Rights & Stigma:** Eradicating stigmatization of mentally ill patients and protecting their rights through regulatory institutions like the Central and State Mental Health Authorities.

---

### **4. District Mental Health Program (DMHP)**
This is the core unit of implementation, launched in **1996**. It aims to decentralize mental health services.

**Components of DMHP:**
*   **Training:** Training of all health workers in the mental health team at the nodal institute.
*   **Public Education (IEC):** To increase awareness and reduce stigma.
*   **Early Detection & Treatment:** Provision of OPD and Indoor services.
*   **Data Generation:** Providing experience/data to the state and centre for future planning.

**Newer Components/Promotive Activities in DMHP:**
*   **School Mental Health Services:** Life skills education and counselling in schools.
*   **College Counselling Services:** Through trained teachers/counsellors.
*   **Workplace Stress Management:** For formal and informal sectors (including farmers).
*   **Suicide Prevention Services:** Counselling centres, helplines, and sensitization workshops.

### **5. Services Provided**
The service component includes three main activities:

| Component | Focus Areas |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Treatment** | • Acute psychoses (Schizophrenia, affective disorders).<br>• Chronic/recurring illness (Epilepsy, dementia).<br>• Emotional illness (Anxiety, hysteria, depression). |
| **Rehabilitation** | • Maintenance treatment of epileptics and psychotics at the community level.<br>• Development of rehabilitation centres at district/referral levels. |
| **Prevention** | • Initial focus on prevention of epilepsy and mental retardation.<br>• Later includes prevention of alcohol/drug addiction, juvenile delinquency, and suicide. |

---

### **6. Role of Health Personnel**
Integration into [[Primary Health Care]] means general health staff are trained to handle basic mental health,.

*   **Medical Officer (PHC):** Diagnosis and management of severe mental disorders, referral of difficult cases, supervision of MPWs,.
*   **Health Assistants:** Early recognition and management of priority psychiatric conditions under supervision.
*   **Multipurpose Workers (MPWs):** Early recognition of severe disorders/epilepsy, referral to PHC, regular follow-up in the community, and educating family members.
*   **ASHA/Health Guides:** Case identification, referral, and supervising follow-up/maintenance therapy.

---

### **7. Important Policy & Legislative Milestones**

#### **A. National Mental Health Policy (2014)**
*   **Vision:** Promote mental health, prevent illness, enable recovery, and ensure accessible/affordable care.
*   **Goals:** Reduce distress/disability/mortality, enhance understanding of mental health, and strengthen leadership at all levels.

#### **B. The Mental Healthcare Act, 2017**
*   Replaced the Mental Health Act of 1987.
*   **Key Feature:** **Decriminalization of Suicide.** Attempting suicide is presumed to be under severe stress and is not punishable; the government must provide care.
*   **Rights:** Protects the right to access mental healthcare and treatment without discrimination.
*   **Definition:** Mental illness is defined as a substantial disorder of thinking, mood, perception, orientation, or memory that grossly impairs judgment or ability to meet ordinary demands of life. *Note: Mental retardation is excluded from the definition of mental illness under this Act*.

---

### **Clinical Relevance**
*   **Availability of Drugs:** Essential psychotropic medicines (e.g., Chlorpromazine, Amitriptyline, Lithium, Diazepam) are included in the Essential Medicines List to ensure availability at PHC/CHC levels.
*   **Referral:** General practitioners and PHC doctors must identify "warning signals" of poor mental health (e.g., changes in sleep, excessive worry, inability to concentrate) and refer appropriate cases to the DMHP psychiatrist.
