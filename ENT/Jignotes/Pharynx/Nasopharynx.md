# [[Pharynx]] > Nasopharynx

### **NASOPHARYNX (EPIPHARYNX)**

The [[nasopharynx]] is the uppermost part of the [[pharynx]], situated behind the [[nasal cavity]] and above the [[soft palate]]. It serves as a common channel for respiration and ventilation of the middle ear but does not transmit food during deglutition.

---

### **1. Anatomical Boundaries**

The nasopharynx resembles a cuboidal box with rigid walls that remain patent (non-collapsible) for air passage.

| Wall | Structures/Boundaries |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anterior** | Communicates with the nasal cavity via the **posterior nasal apertures ([[choanae]])**. Separated by the posterior border of the [[nasal septum]]. |
| **Posterior & Roof** | Formed by the [[basisphenoid]], [[basiocciput]], and the anterior arch of the **C1 vertebra (atlas)**. The roof and posterior wall merge imperceptibly. |
| **Inferior (Floor)** | Formed by the upper surface of the [[soft palate]] anteriorly and the **nasopharyngeal isthmus** posteriorly. |
| **Lateral** | Contains the **Eustachian tube orifice**, [[torus tubarius]], and [[fossa of Rosenmuller]]. |

### **2. Histology (Lining Epithelium)**

*   **Type:** **Pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium** (Respiratory epithelium).
*   **Exception:** There is a transitional zone where it meets the [[oropharynx]] (lower part), changing to stratified squamous epithelium.

---

### **3. Key Structures in the Nasopharynx**

#### **A. Eustachian Tube Opening (Auditory Tube)**
*   **Location:** Lateral wall, approximately **1.25 cm** behind the posterior end of the [[inferior turbinate]].
*   **Function:** Ventilates the [[middle ear]] and equalizes pressure.

#### **B. Torus Tubarius**
*   A cartilaginous elevation guarding the upper and posterior lips of the Eustachian tube opening.
*   Formed by the medial end of the tubal cartilage.

#### **C. Fossa of Rosenmuller (Lateral Pharyngeal Recess)**
*   **Location:** A deep, slit-like recess situated **posterior** to the [[torus tubarius]] and extending laterally.
*   **Depth:** Can be up to 1.5 cm deep.
*   **Relations:**
    *   **Anterior:** Eustachian tube.
    *   **Posterior:** Pharyngobasilar fascia and retropharyngeal space.
    *   **Superior:** [[Foramen lacerum]] and [[carotid canal]].
*   **Clinical Significance:**
    *   It is the **most common site of origin** for [[Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma]] (NPC).
    *   The [[internal carotid artery]] lies just deep to the lateral wall of this fossa.

#### **D. Adenoids (Nasopharyngeal Tonsil)**
*   **Location:** Junction of the roof and posterior wall.
*   **Structure:** Composed of vertical ridges of lymphoid tissue.
*   **Unique Features:** NO capsule, NO crypts.
*   **Growth Cycle:** Present at birth, hypertrophies until age 6, then atrophies at puberty, usually disappearing by age 20.
*   **Blood Supply:** Ascending pharyngeal, ascending palatine, and pharyngeal branch of maxillary artery.

#### **E. Pharyngeal Bursa (Thornwaldt’s Bursa)**
*   A median recess in the adenoid mass.
*   **Origin:** Remnant of the connection between the notochord and pharyngeal endoderm.
*   **Pathology:** Infection leads to **Thornwaldt’s cyst/disease** causing persistent post-nasal drip and occipital headache.

#### **F. Rathke’s Pouch**
*   A dimple in the roof/septum junction; remnant of the invagination that forms the anterior [[pituitary gland]].
*   **Pathology:** Origin of [[Craniopharyngioma]].

---

### **4. Associated Spaces and Ridges**

#### **Sinus of Morgagni**
A potential gap between the base of the skull and the upper border of the **superior constrictor muscle**. It is closed by the **pharyngobasilar fascia**.

**Structures passing through Sinus of Morgagni:**
*(Mnemonic: **TAALA**)*
1.  **T**ensor veli palatini
2.  **A**scending palatine artery
3.  **A**scending pharyngeal artery (branch)
4.  **L**evator veli palatini
5.  **A**uditory tube ([[Eustachian tube]])

#### **Passavant’s Ridge**
*   **Formation:** A mucosal ridge formed by the contraction of specific fibers of the [[palatopharyngeus]] muscle (some sources say superior constrictor).
*   **Function:** The [[soft palate]] elevates against this ridge to close the nasopharyngeal isthmus during swallowing and speech (prevents nasal regurgitation).

---

### **5. Neurovascular Supply**

*   **Arterial Supply:**
    *   Ascending pharyngeal artery.
    *   Ascending palatine branch of [[facial artery]].
    *   Pharyngeal branch of [[maxillary artery]].
*   **Venous Drainage:** Pharyngeal venous plexus.
*   **Nerve Supply:**
    *   **Sensory:** Primarily the **Maxillary nerve (V2)**. The glossopharyngeal nerve (IX) supplies part of the floor.
    *   **Autonomic:** Pterygopalatine ganglion.

---

### **6. Lymphatic Drainage**

The nasopharynx has a rich submucosal lymphatic plexus. Drainage is directed to:
1.  **Retropharyngeal Nodes (Nodes of Rouviere):** Located in the space of Gillette (lateral retropharyngeal space).
2.  **Upper Deep Cervical Nodes (Jugulodigastric):** Level II nodes.
3.  **Spinal Accessory Nodes:** In the posterior triangle (Level V).

> **Clinical Insight:** [[Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma]] often presents *first* with a painless upper neck mass due to this extensive drainage.

---

### **7. Applied Clinical Anatomy**

#### **A. Adenoid Hypertrophy**
Enlargement of the adenoids can obstruct the choanae and Eustachian tubes.
*   **Clinical Features:**
    *   **Nasal:** Mouth breathing, snoring, nasal obstruction, nasal discharge.
    *   **Aural:** [[Eustachian tube dysfunction]] leading to Recurrent Acute Otitis Media (RAOM) or [[Otitis Media with Effusion]] (OME/Glue ear).
    *   **Adenoid Facies:** Long face, dull expression, pinched nostrils, high arched palate, open mouth, crowded teeth.
    *   **Speech:** Hyponasality (**Rhinolalia Clausa**).
*   **X-Ray Assessment:** Lateral neck view shows soft tissue mass compromising the nasopharyngeal air column.

#### **B. Juvenile Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma (JNA)**
*   **Demographic:** Almost exclusively **adolescent males**.
*   **Site:** Arises near the **Sphenopalatine foramen**.
*   **Features:** Profuse recurrent epistaxis, nasal obstruction.
*   **Frog Face Deformity:** Widening of the nasal bridge and proptosis due to anterior extension.
*   **Imaging:** Contrast CT shows **Holman-Miller Sign** (anterior bowing of the posterior wall of the maxillary sinus).
*   **Biopsy:** **CONTRAINDICATED** due to massive bleeding risk.

#### **C. Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC)**
*   **Site:** Most commonly the [[Fossa of Rosenmuller]].
*   **Etiology:** Genetic (Chinese/Mongoloid), Viral ([[Epstein-Barr Virus]]), Environmental (Salted fish/Nitrosamines).
*   **Trotter’s Triad:** (Symptoms of lateral spread)
    1.  **Conductive Deafness** (Eustachian tube occlusion).
    2.  **Immobility of Soft Palate** (Direct invasion or Vagus nerve palsy).
    3.  **Trigeminal Neuralgia** (Pain in the distribution of V3 - mandibular nerve).
