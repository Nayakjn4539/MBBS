# [[INTERNATIONAL HEALTH]] > UNICEF

## UNICEF (United Nations Children's Fund)

[[UNICEF]] is a specialized agency of the United Nations, dedicated to the long-term humanitarian and developmental assistance to children and mothers.

### **Historical Background and Organization**
*   **Establishment:** Founded in **1946** by the UN General Assembly.
*   **Original Name:** United Nations International Childrenâ€™s Emergency Fund. It was created to provide relief to children in war-ravaged countries after World War II.
*   **Name Change:** In **1953**, the UN extended its mandate indefinitely. The words "International" and "Emergency" were dropped, becoming the **United Nations Children's Fund**, but the acronym **UNICEF** was retained.
*   **Headquarters:** **New York, USA**.
*   **Governance:** It is governed by an **Executive Board** (comprising 30-36 nations).
*   **Regional Office:** For the **South Central Asian Region** (covering India, Afghanistan, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Pakistan, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Nepal), the regional office is located in **New Delhi** (and Kathmandu).

### **Core Philosophy: The "Whole Child"**
*   In the early years, UNICEF worked closely with [[WHO]] on specific disease campaigns (Malaria, TB, VD).
*   Recently, the focus shifted from specific disease eradication to the concept of the **"Whole Child"**.
*   This approach implies that assistance is geared not just towards immediate health and nutrition, but also towards the long-term personal development of the child and the development of the country they live in.
*   This is often implemented through **[[Country Health Programming]]**.

### **The "GOBI-FFF" Strategy**
UNICEF launched a low-cost, high-impact strategy known as the "Child Health Revolution" to improve child survival and development.

| **Mnemonic** | **Component** | **Description** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **G** | **[[Growth Charts]]** | To better monitor child development and detect malnutrition early. |
| **O** | **[[Oral Rehydration]]** | To treat mild and moderate dehydration caused by diarrhea. |
| **B** | **[[Breast Feeding]]** | Promotion of exclusive breastfeeding. |
| **I** | **[[Immunization]]** | Against the "Big Six" diseases (TB, Polio, Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Measles). |
| **F** | **Female Education** | Educating mothers improves child survival. |
| **F** | **[[Family Spacing]]** | Proper birth spacing improves maternal and child health. |
| **F** | **Food Supplements** | To combat malnutrition in vulnerable groups. |

### **Key Areas of Work and Assistance in India**

UNICEF works in close collaboration with [[WHO]], [[FAO]], and [[UNDP]].

#### **1. Child Health**
*   **Vaccine Production:** Provided substantial aid for the production of vaccines and sera in India.
*   **Infrastructure Support:**
    *   Supported India's **[[BCG vaccination]]** program.
    *   Assisted in establishing a **Penicillin plant** near Pune.
    *   Donated a **DDT plant** for malaria control.
    *   Donated plants for the manufacture of **Triple Vaccine (DPT)**.
*   **Sanitation:** Promotes environmental sanitation and safe drinking water in rural areas.

#### **2. Child Nutrition**
*   **Applied Nutrition Programme:** In collaboration with FAO, UNICEF assists in community development to help rural populations grow and eat better food.
*   **Micronutrient Deficiencies:**
    *   **[[Vitamin A Prophylaxis]]**: Distribution of large doses of Vitamin A to prevent nutritional blindness/[[Xerophthalmia]].
    *   **Iodine**: Support for the enrichment of salt with iodine in goiter-endemic areas (donated iodization plants).
    *   **Iron**: Provision of Iron and Folate supplements to combat anemia.
*   **Dairy:** Supplied equipment for modern dairy plants in various Indian states to improve milk availability.

#### **3. Family and Child Welfare**
*   Aims to improve care within and outside homes through parent education, day-care centers, and women's clubs.
*   **[[Urban Basic Services]] (UBS):** Since 1976, UNICEF has participated in upgrading basic services (water, sanitation, health) for urban poor/slum dwellers.
*   **[[ICDS]] Support:** UNICEF provides substantial inputs to the Integrated Child Development Services scheme in India.

#### **4. Education**
*   Assists in the expansion and improvement of science teaching in India (providing lab equipment, library books, audio-visual aids).
*   Focuses on both formal and non-formal education.

### **Clinical Relevance**
*   **Cold Chain:** UNICEF supplies cold chain equipment (refrigerators, cold boxes) essential for maintaining vaccine potency in the [[Universal Immunization Programme]].
*   **ORT:** The promotion of Oral Rehydration Therapy has significantly reduced mortality from diarrheal diseases in clinical practice.
