# [[Nose]] > Wegener's Granulomatosis and Sarcoidosis

# Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (Wegener's Granulomatosis)

**Definition:** A systemic autoimmune disease characterized by necrotizing granulomatous inflammation and vasculitis of small and medium-sized blood vessels. It classically involves the triad of the **Upper Respiratory Tract** (nose/sinuses), **Lower Respiratory Tract** (lungs), and **Kidneys**.

### Aetiology and Epidemiology
*   **Cause:** Autoimmune etiology; exact trigger unknown (infectious or environmental) in genetically susceptible individuals.
*   **Age/Sex:** Average onset 40–55 years; affects men and women equally.
*   **Nasal Involvement:** Occurs in >80-90% of cases.

### Clinical Features
**Nasal Manifestations:**
*   **Symptoms:** "Persistent cold" or "sinus" symptoms, nasal obstruction, purulent or blood-stained rhinorrhea.
*   **Crusting:** Extensive crusting is a hallmark finding. No other condition produces larger crusts than [[Wegener's granulomatosis]].
*   **Mucosa:** Friable, granular mucosa with ulceration.
*   **Septal Perforation:** Can involve both the **bony** and **cartilaginous** parts of the nasal septum. Total septal destruction may occur.
*   **Deformity:** Collapse of the nasal bridge leads to a classic **[[Saddle nose deformity]]**.
*   **Other:** Anosmia, facial pain.

**Systemic/Extranasal Manifestations:**
*   **Oral:** **Strawberry gingival hyperplasia** (rare but pathognomonic). Palatal ulceration/perforation may occur.
*   **Ear:** Serous otitis media, sensorineural hearing loss.
*   **Lungs:** Cough, hemoptysis, dyspnea. CXR may show multiple cavitary lesions.
*   **Renal:** Focal segmental glomerulonephritis (rapidly progressive). Urine shows RBC casts.
*   **General:** Fever, malaise, weight loss, migratory arthralgia.

### Diagnosis and Investigations
1.  **Serology (Highly Specific):**
    *   **c-ANCA** (cytoplasmic Anti-Neutrophil Cytoplasmic Antibody) positive in 85–90% of active systemic cases.
    *   Specific for **Proteinase 3 (PR3)**.
    *   Titer correlates with disease activity.
2.  **Biopsy (Definitive):**
    *   Nasal biopsy may be difficult to obtain (often shows only necrosis).
    *   Histology triad: **Necrotizing granulomas**, **Vasculitis**, and **Necrosis**.
3.  **Imaging:**
    *   **CT Paranasal Sinuses:** Bony destruction (septum/lateral wall), mucosal thickening, neo-osteogenesis.
    *   **Chest X-ray/CT:** Nodules or cavities.
4.  **Urinalysis:** Check for red cells and casts (renal involvement).

### Treatment
*   **Medical (Mainstay):**
    *   **Corticosteroids** (Prednisolone) combined with cytotoxic drugs.
    *   **Cyclophosphamide** is the drug of choice for induction.
    *   **Rituximab** (Monoclonal antibody) is an alternative.
    *   Maintenance: Azathioprine, Methotrexate.
    *   **Cotrimoxazole** (Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole): Used for limited disease and to prevent relapse.
*   **Local:** Alkaline nasal douche for crust removal.
*   **Surgical:** Reconstructive surgery (e.g., for saddle nose) only when the disease is in remission.

**Clinical Implication:** Always suspect vasculitis if there is general malaise disproportionate to the clinical findings or if sinusitis is refractory to standard treatment.

# Sarcoidosis

**Definition:** A chronic multisystem inflammatory disease of unknown etiology characterized by the presence of **non-caseating epithelioid granulomas**.

### Clinical Features
**Nasal Manifestations (Sinonasal Sarcoidosis):**
*   **Prevalence:** Affects the nose in 1–4% of cases.
*   **Symptoms:** Nasal obstruction, crusting, epistaxis, facial pain.
*   **Mucosa:** **"Strawberry" appearance** of the nasal mucosa (erythematous mucosa covered with pale granulomas). *Note: Differentiate from the strawberry mass of [[Rhinosporidiosis]]*.
*   **Nodules:** Submucosal nodules on the septum or inferior turbinate.
*   **Septal Perforation:** Usually involves the anterior **cartilaginous** part.
*   **Skin:** **[[Lupus pernio]]**—Violaceous, indurated lesions on the tip of the nose, cheeks, and ears. This is pathognomonic for cutaneous sarcoidosis.

**Systemic Manifestations:**
*   **Lungs:** Bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy (classic finding).
*   **Heerfordt’s Syndrome (Uveoparotid Fever):** Triad of **Uveitis**, **Parotid enlargement**, and **Facial nerve paralysis**.
*   **Others:** SNHL, peripheral neuropathy, hypercalcemia.

### Investigations
1.  **Biopsy (Gold Standard):** Shows **non-caseating epithelioid granulomas**.
2.  **Blood Tests:**
    *   **Serum ACE** (Angiotensin Converting Enzyme): Elevated (supportive but not specific).
    *   **Serum Calcium:** Hypercalcemia may be present.
3.  **Chest X-ray:** Bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy.
4.  **Kveim Test:** (Obsolete/withdrawn).

### Treatment
*   **Observation:** Spontaneous resolution may occur.
*   **Medical:**
    *   **Corticosteroids:** Systemic (Prednisolone) is the mainstay for progressive disease. Topical steroid sprays for nasal symptoms.
    *   **Cytotoxic agents:** Methotrexate, Azathioprine for steroid-resistant cases.
    *   **Hydroxychloroquine:** For cutaneous lesions (Lupus pernio).

### Comparison: Wegener's (GPA) vs. Sarcoidosis vs. Other Nasal Granulomas

| Feature | **Wegener's (GPA)** | **Sarcoidosis** | **Rhinoscleroma** | **Rhinosporidiosis** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aetiology** | Autoimmune Vasculitis | Unknown (Autoimmune) | *Klebsiella rhinoscleromatis* | *Rhinosporidium seeberi* |
| **Nasal Sign** | Massive crusting, Saddle nose | Strawberry mucosa, Lupus pernio | Woody/Hebra nose (Hard) | Strawberry/Mulberry polypoid mass |
| **Septal Perforation** | **Bony** & Cartilaginous | Cartilaginous | Cartilaginous (Rare) | No perforation |
| **Key Histology** | Necrotizing vasculitis, Giant cells | **Non-caseating** granulomas | **Mikulicz cells**, Russell bodies | **Sporangia** with endospores |
| **Systemic Sign** | Renal failure, Lung cavities | B/L Hilar Lymphadenopathy | Laryngotracheal stenosis | Ocular/Cutaneous spread |
| **Diagnostic Marker** | **c-ANCA (PR3)** | Elevated **Serum ACE** | Biopsy/Culture | Biopsy |
| **Treatment** | Cyclophosphamide + Steroids | Steroids | Ciprofloxacin/Tetracycline | Surgical Excision + Cautery |

### Mnemonic for Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (Wegener's)
**"C-ANCA"**
*   **C** - Cyclophosphamide (Treatment)
*   **A** - Axis (Upper and Lower Respiratory tract)
*   **N** - Necrotizing vasculitis / Nose (Saddle nose)
*   **C** - c-ANCA positive (PR3)
*   **A** - Azotemia (Renal failure/Glomerulonephritis)
