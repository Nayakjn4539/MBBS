# [[Ear]] > Vertigo

# Vertigo

**Definition**
[[Vertigo]] is the illusion of movement of self or the environment, most commonly a sensation of spinning or rotation, resulting from a disturbance of the [[vestibular system]]. It is distinct from lightheadedness or disequilibrium.

### Classification of Vertigo
Vertigo is broadly classified based on the anatomical site of the lesion into **Peripheral** (End organ/Vestibular nerve) and **Central** (Brainstem/Cerebellum/Connections).

| Feature | Peripheral Vertigo | Central Vertigo |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Site of Lesion** | Labyrinth or VIII Nerve | Brainstem, Cerebellum, Cortex |
| **Onset** | Sudden, severe | Gradual, insidious |
| **Duration** | Finite (minutes, days, weeks), recurrent | Chronic, constant |
| **Severity** | Marked/Severe | Mild to Moderate |
| **Aggravating Factors** | Head movement (often) | Variable |
| **Nystagmus** | Horizontal-torsional, unidirectional, fatigable, suppressed by optic fixation | Pure vertical/horizontal/torsional, direction-changing, non-fatigable, not suppressed by fixation |
| **Associated Otologic Symptoms** | Common (Hearing loss, Tinnitus) | Rare |
| **Neurological Symptoms** | Absent | Common (Diplopia, Dysarthria, Ataxia) |
| **Common Causes** | [[BPPV]], [[Meniere's disease]], [[Vestibular neuritis]], [[Labyrinthitis]] | [[Vertebrobasilar insufficiency]], [[Multiple sclerosis]], [[Migraine]], [[Brainstem tumors]] |

### Etiology and Differential Diagnosis (Based on Duration)

A crucial clinical tool for diagnosing vertigo is the **duration of the episode**.

**1. Seconds to Minutes (Short Duration)**
*   **[[Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo]] (BPPV):** Triggered by head position changes (turning in bed, looking up). Lasts < 1 minute.
*   **[[Perilymph Fistula]]:** Associated with straining/trauma.
*   **[[Superior Semicircular Canal Dehiscence]] (SSCD):** Vertigo induced by loud sounds ([[Tullio phenomenon]]).
*   **[[Cervical Vertigo]]:** Associated with neck movement.

**2. Minutes to Hours (Intermediate Duration)**
*   **[[Meniere's Disease]]:** Episodic vertigo (20 min to 24 hours) with triad of vertigo, sensorineural hearing loss, and tinnitus.
*   **[[Vestibular Migraine]]:** Associated with headache, photophobia.
*   **Transient Ischemic Attacks (TIA):** Vertebrobasilar insufficiency.

**3. Days to Weeks (Long Duration)**
*   **[[Vestibular Neuritis]]:** Severe vertigo, *no* hearing loss. Usually following viral infection.
*   **[[Labyrinthitis]]:** Severe vertigo *with* hearing loss.
*   **Head Trauma/Labyrinthine Concussion**.
*   **Infarction** (Brainstem/Cerebellum).

### Clinical Evaluation

#### 1. History Taking
*   **Character:** Spinning (vestibular) vs. blackouts/imbalance.
*   **Duration & Frequency:** Single vs. recurrent episodes.
*   **Triggers:** Head position ([[BPPV]]), loud noise ([[Tullio phenomenon]]), straining ([[Hennebert's sign]]).
*   **Associated Symptoms:**
    *   *Otologic:* Tinnitus, aural fullness, discharge (suggests [[Cholesteatoma]]/fistula).
    *   *Neurologic:* Diplopia, dysarthria, numbness (suggests central lesion).

#### 2. Examination
**A. General & Systemic:** Pulse/BP (rule out orthostatic hypotension), neurological exam (Cranial nerves).

**B. Otoscopy:**
*   Check for wax, [[Acute Otitis Media]] (AOM), [[CSOM]], [[Cholesteatoma]], or vesicles ([[Ramsay Hunt Syndrome]]).
*   **[[Fistula Test]]:** Positive if pressure on tragus/Siegelization causes vertigo/nystagmus. Seen in labyrinthine fistula (CSOM), [[Syphilis]] (Hennebert sign), or [[Meniere's disease]].

**C. Vestibular Function Tests (Bedside):**
*   **[[Spontaneous Nystagmus]]:**
    *   Involuntary rhythmic eye oscillation.
    *   **[[Alexander's Law]]:** In peripheral lesions, nystagmus intensity increases when looking towards the fast phase.
*   **[[Romberg Test]]:** Patient stands with feet together, eyes closed. Swaying/falling indicates vestibular or proprioceptive deficit.
*   **[[Unterberger Test]] (Fukuda Stepping Test):** Patient marches in place with eyes closed. Rotation >30 degrees suggests peripheral lesion on the side of rotation.
*   **[[Head Impulse Test]] (Halmagyi):** Detects vestibulo-ocular reflex (VOR) deficit. Catch-up saccades indicate peripheral lesion.

**D. Positional Testing:**
*   **[[Dix-Hallpike Maneuver]]:** Gold standard for diagnosing Posterior Canal [[BPPV]].
    *   *Procedure:* Head turned 45째, patient laid supine with head hanging 30째.
    *   *Positive:* Latent, rotatory/torsional, geotropic nystagmus directed towards the affected ear. It is fatigable.

### Specific Disorders

#### 1. Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV)
*   **Pathology:** Displaced otoconia (Calcium carbonate crystals) from the [[Utricle]] into the semicircular canals (commonly Posterior SCC).
*   **Theories:**
    *   *Canalithiasis:* Free-floating particles.
    *   *Cupulolithiasis:* Particles adherent to cupula.
*   **Clinical Features:** Short episodes of vertigo (<1 min) provoked by head position changes (rolling in bed). No hearing loss.
*   **Diagnosis:** Positive [[Dix-Hallpike test]].
*   **Treatment:** [[Epley's Maneuver]] (Canalith repositioning).

#### 2. Meniere's Disease (Endolymphatic Hydrops)
*   **Pathology:** Distention of the endolymphatic system (Scala media and Saccule).
*   **Clinical Triad:**
    1.  Episodic Vertigo.
    2.  Fluctuating [[Sensorineural Hearing Loss]] (low frequency).
    3.  [[Tinnitus]] (roaring) and Aural fullness.
*   **Variants:**
    *   *[[Lermoyez Syndrome]]:* Hearing improves after vertigo attack.
    *   *[[Tumarkin Crisis]]:* Drop attacks.
*   **Treatment:** Low salt diet, Betahistine (vasodilator), Diuretics, Intratympanic Gentamicin (Chemical Labyrinthectomy).

#### 3. Vestibular Neuritis vs. Labyrinthitis
*   **[[Vestibular Neuritis]]:** Viral inflammation of the [[Vestibular nerve]]. Sudden severe vertigo, nausea, vomiting. **Hearing is preserved**. Spontaneous nystagmus towards the healthy side.
*   **[[Labyrinthitis]]:** Inflammation of the inner ear. Similar severe vertigo but **associated with Sensorineural Hearing Loss**.
    *   *Serous:* Toxic irritation, reversible.
    *   *Suppurative:* Bacterial invasion, permanent loss of function.

### Investigations

**1. [[Pure Tone Audiometry]] (PTA)**
*   differentiates Conductive vs Sensorineural loss.
*   Low frequency loss: Meniere's.
*   High frequency loss: [[Ototoxicity]], [[Presbycusis]].

**2. [[Caloric Test]] (Fitzgerald-Hallpike)**
*   Irrigation of ear with warm (44째C) and cold (30째C) water.
*   **Mnemonic: COWS** (Cold-Opposite, Warm-Same) regarding the direction of the fast phase of nystagmus.
*   Detects **Canal Paresis** (peripheral lesion) or **Directional Preponderance**.

**3. [[Electronystagmography]] (ENG) / [[Videonystagmography]] (VNG)**
*   Records eye movements to assess Oculomotor and Vestibular systems.

**4. [[Vestibular Evoked Myogenic Potentials]] (VEMP)**
*   Tests the Otolith organs ([[Saccule]] and [[Utricle]]) and vestibular nerve function.
*   Useful in [[Superior Semicircular Canal Dehiscence]] (low threshold).

**5. Imaging (MRI)**
*   Gadolinium-enhanced MRI is the gold standard to rule out **[[Acoustic Neuroma]]** (Vestibular Schwannoma) or central lesions (MS, Stroke).

### Management Principles

1.  **Acute Phase:**
    *   **Vestibular Sedatives:** Prochlorperazine, Promethazine, Benzodiazepines (Diazepam). *Clinical Implication: Do not use for >3-5 days to avoid hampering central compensation*.
    *   Bed rest and hydration.

2.  **Specific Therapy:**
    *   **BPPV:** [[Epley's Maneuver]] (Posterior canal), Barbecue roll (Lateral canal).
    *   **Meniere's:** Salt restriction, Betahistine, Diuretics.
    *   **Bacterial Labyrinthitis:** IV Antibiotics.

3.  **Chronic Phase / Rehabilitation:**
    *   **[[Vestibular Rehabilitation Exercises]] (VRT):** Cawthorne-Cooksey exercises to promote central compensation.

4.  **Surgical:**
    *   Used for intractable cases.
    *   Procedures: Labyrinthectomy, Vestibular Nerve Section, Endolymphatic Sac Decompression.
