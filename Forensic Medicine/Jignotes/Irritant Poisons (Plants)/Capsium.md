# [[Irritant Poisons (Plants)]] > Capsium

### [[Capsicum]] (Chillies / Red Pepper)

**Botanical Name:** *[[Capsicum annuum]]* or *[[Capsicum frutescens]]*
**Family:** Solanaceae
**Common Names:** Chilly, Mirchi, Lal Mirchi, Red pepper, Cayenne pepper.

#### Active Principles
*   **[[Capsaicin]]** (Main active principle, accounts for 69%).
*   [[Capsicin]]
*   Dihydrocapsaicin
*   Capsicutin

**Action:**
*   It is a powerful **irritant**.
*   It depletes **Substance P** from nerve terminals, causing initial intense excitation of sensory nerve endings (burning pain) followed by a period of relative insensitivity (desensitization).

---

#### Physical Appearance
*   **Plant:** A small herb.
*   **Fruit:** Long, tapering, or round pod. Green when unripe, red when ripe. Has a pungent odor and burning taste.
*   **Seeds:**
    *   Numerous, small, flat, and discoid/rounded.
    *   **Color:** Pale yellow.
    *   **Smell:** Pungent.
    *   **Appearance:** Smooth surface.
    *   **Embryo:** Curved inwards like the number **6** or **9**.

**Important:** [[Capsicum seeds]] are often confused with [[Datura seeds]].

#### [[Differentiation: Datura vs Capsicum Seeds]]

| Feature | Datura Seeds | Capsicum Seeds |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Size** | Larger and thicker | Smaller and thinner |
| **Shape** | Kidney-shaped (Reniform) | Rounded / Discoid / Oval |
| **Color** | Dark brown / Yellowish-brown | Pale yellow |
| **Surface** | Pitted / Reticulated / Rough | Smooth |
| **Margin** | Double-edged on convex side | Single sharp edge |
| **Smell** | Odorless | Pungent (irritating smell) |
| **Taste** | Bitter | Pungent / Burning |
| **Embryo** | Curved **Outwards** | Curved **Inwards** |
| **Anatomy** | Embryo looks like an **ear** | Embryo looks like figure **6** or **9** |

#### Clinical Features
**1. Local Application (Skin/Mucosa):**
*   **Burning sensation** and redness.
*   **Vesication:** Formation of blisters if applied for a long time.
*   Lacrimation and rhinorrhea if eyes/nose are exposed.

**2. Ingestion:**
*   **Mouth:** Intense burning sensation in the mouth, throat, and stomach.
*   **GIT:** Salivation, vomiting, abdominal colic, tenesmus, and severe purging (diarrhea).
*   **Urinary:** Dysuria, oliguria, and occasionally nephritis.
*   **General:** Tachycardia, dyspnea, vertigo, and collapse in severe cases.

**3. Inhalation:**
*   Intense irritation of the respiratory tract.
*   Sneezing, coughing, and dyspnea.

**4. Specific Syndrome:**
*   **[[Hunan Hand Syndrome]]:** A form of contact dermatitis seen in workers handling chili peppers, characterized by burning, erythema, and desquamation of the skin.

#### Treatment
**1. Skin Contact:**
*   Wash the affected area with **vinegar**, **vegetable oil**, or **ice-cold water**.
*   Topical analgesics or steroid creams for dermatitis.

**2. Eye Contact:**
*   Copious irrigation with saline or water.
*   Topical anesthetics and antibiotics.

**3. Ingestion:**
*   **Demulcents:** Milk, egg white, butter, or oil to soothe the mucosa.
*   **Gastric Lavage:** With warm water (if large quantity ingested).
*   **Cold:** Sucking ice cubes or sipping ice-cold water.
*   **Symptomatic:** Morphine/Analgesics for pain and treatment for diarrhea/shock.

---

#### Postmortem Findings
*   Findings are generally non-specific.
*   **GIT:** Acute inflammation of the gastrointestinal tract. Stomach mucosa may show **congestion** and **ulcerated patches**.
*   **Respiratory:** Froth at nostrils, congestion of respiratory tract if powder inhaled.
*   **Internal Organs:** Generalized congestion.

---

#### Medicolegal Aspects
1.  **Robbery:** Chilly powder is thrown into the eyes of victims to incapacitate them temporarily to facilitate robbery.
2.  **Torture:**
    *   Introduction of chilly powder or paste into the **nostrils**, **rectum**, **vagina**, or **urethra** by police or robbers to extract confessions or money.
    *   Rubbing powder on sensitive parts like female breasts.
3.  **Punishment:** Historically used as a punishment for infidelity (vaginal introduction).
4.  **Accidental Poisoning:** Mistaken identity between *Datura* seeds and *Capsicum* seeds.
5.  **Malingering:** Used to produce **[[artificial bruises]]** to level false charges of assault.
