### [[World Health Organization (WHO)]]

The World Health Organization is a specialized, non-political health agency of the [[United Nations]], with headquarters in **Geneva, Switzerland**. It is the directing and coordinating authority on international health work.

#### **Origin and Constitution**
*   **1945:** Origin during the UN conference in San Francisco.
*   **1946:** Constitution drafted by the "Technical Preparatory Committee" under the chairmanship of **Rene Sand**.
*   **1948:** Constitution came into force on **7th April**, which is celebrated annually as **[[World Health Day]]**,.

#### **Objective**
*   "The attainment by all peoples of the highest possible level of health.",
*   **Definition of Health:** A state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity. Recently amplified to include the ability to lead a "socially and economically productive life",.

#### **Membership**
*   Open to all countries.
*   Currently has **194 Member States** and 2 Associate Members.
*   Each member contributes to the budget and is entitled to services and aid.

#### **Structure of WHO**
The WHO consists of three principal organs,,:

| **Organ** | **Description & Function** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. [[World Health Assembly]] (WHA)** | • The **"Health Parliament"** of nations.<br>• Supreme governing body.<br>• Meets annually (usually in May) in Geneva.<br>• **Functions:** Determine international health policy, review past work, approve budget, elect member states for Executive Board, appoint the Director-General. |
| **2. [[Executive Board]]** | • Technical executive organ.<br>• Consists of **34 members** technically qualified in the field of health,.<br>• Members are designated by governments but do not represent them.<br>• Meets at least twice a year.<br>• **Functions:** Gives effect to decisions/policies of the Assembly; power to take action in emergencies (epidemics, floods). |
| **3. [[Secretariat]]** | • The administrative unit headed by the **Director-General** (DG).<br>• Current DG: Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus (Note: Update based on current knowledge, sources mention Dr. Gro Harlem Brundtland as past DG).<br>• **Functions:** Provides technical and managerial support to member states for national health development programmes. |

> **Mnemonic for Structure:** **WES**
> *   **W** - **W**orld Health Assembly (Parliament)
> *   **E** - **E**xecutive Board (Experts)
> *   **S** - **S**ecretariat (Staff/Admin)

#### **Functions of WHO**,,

1.  **Prevention and Control of Specific Diseases:**
    *   Both communicable (e.g., [[Malaria]], [[TB]], [[HIV/AIDS]]) and non-communicable diseases (e.g., Cancer, Diabetes).
    *   Global eradication of [[Smallpox]] is a major achievement.
    *   Epidemiological surveillance of diseases subject to **[[International Health Regulations]]** (Cholera, Plague, Yellow Fever, etc.).

2.  **Development of Comprehensive Health Services:**
    *   Promoting **[[Primary Health Care]] (PHC)** concept (Alma-Ata Declaration, 1978).
    *   Strengthening health infrastructure and manpower.

3.  **Family Health:**
    *   Maternal and Child Health ([[MCH]]), Human Reproduction, Nutrition, and Health Education.

4.  **Environmental Health:**
    *   Safe water supply, sanitation, quality of air/food, and radiation protection.

5.  **Health Statistics:**
    *   Dissemination of morbidity and mortality statistics (Weekly Epidemiological Record).
    *   Publishing the **[[International Classification of Diseases]] (ICD)** (Revised every 10 years).

6.  **Biomedical Research:**
    *   Does not do research itself but stimulates/coordinates it through **WHO Collaborating Centres**.
    *   Focus on Tropical Diseases (TDR program).

7.  **Health Literature & Information:**
    *   Acts as a clearing house for information.
    *   Maintains the MEDLARS library system.

8.  **Setting Standards:**
    *   Biologicals, pharmaceutical quality control, and diagnostic procedures.

#### **Key Concepts & Initiatives**

*   **[[Health For All]] (HFA) by 2000:** A social target set in 1977 to permit all citizens to lead a socially and economically productive life,.
*   **Millennium Development Goals (MDGs):** Adopted in 2000; health was central to these goals.
*   **[[Sustainable Development Goals]] (SDGs):** Adopted in 2015. **SDG 3** is explicitly health-related: "Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages".
*   **World Health Day Themes:** Used to focus attention on specific public health issues.
    *   2012: Ageing and Health
    *   2013: High Blood Pressure
    *   2014: Vector-borne diseases
    *   2015: Food Safety
    *   2016: Diabetes.

---

#### **Clinical Relevance**
*   **Guidelines:** WHO guidelines (e.g., for TB, Malaria, HIV, Growth Monitoring) are considered the **Gold Standard** for clinical practice and national health programs in developing countries.
*   **ICD Coding:** Doctors use WHO's **ICD-10/11** codes for standardizing diagnosis and death certification worldwide.
*   **Growth Charts:** The WHO Child Growth Standards are used globally to monitor the nutritional status of children,.
